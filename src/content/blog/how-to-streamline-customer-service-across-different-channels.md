<p>Customer service, once deemed a cost center, is emerging as a key differentiator. By investing in comprehensive strategies and empowering employees, organizations are transforming customer service into a powerful asset that enhances the brand experience and drives customer loyalty.&nbsp;</p><p>As price and product competition intensify, a customer-centric focus becomes crucial for acquiring and retaining customers.</p><p>If you are a brand owner or customer support head, you’ll have to focus on better availability, quality, and knowledge organization across channels to lower cost, empower your agents and customers, and increase customer satisfaction across every touchpoint.&nbsp;</p><p>According to <a href="https://download.microsoft.com/documents/en-us/dynamics/Global_State_of_Multichannel_Customer_Service_Report.pdf.pdf">Microsoft's Global State of Multichannel Customer Service Report</a>, the average consumer uses at least four distinct channels for interaction and expects exceptional service across each.&nbsp;</p><p>On the flip side, Accenture reports that <a href="https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2013/us-switching-economy-puts-up-to-1-3-trillion-of-revenue-up-for-grabs-for-companies-offering-superior-customer-experiences-accenture-research-finds">91% of participants</a> in a survey expressed frustration at having to contact a company repeatedly for the same issue.</p><p>So, how do you avoid all this? The answer is: by streamlining customer service across different voice, SMS, email, or live chat channels. In this article, we will explore seven strategies and best practices to help you get started.</p><h2>How to improve and streamline customer service</h2><p>Here’s how you can start streamlining customer service across diverse channels. Understand and analyze every step customers take when interacting with your support system, identifying roadblocks or frustrations they may encounter.</p><p>For instance, imagine a customer trying to reach out for assistance via email but facing a lengthy response time due to manual routing processes. The delay frustrates the customer and unnecessarily prolongs issue resolution.&nbsp;</p><p>The customer journey map helps you identify this problem. Trigger workflows remotely, filter queues for skill-based routing, and quickly escalate complex issues.</p><h3>2. Use self-service chatbots</h3><p>Self-service chatbots powered by AI allow customers to resolve basic issues independently, improving ticket resolution speed. They also free up live chat agents for more complex issues and improve the agent experience at your company.</p><p>A good example of self-service chatbots is order status inquiry. They can be programmed to access order management systems and provide real-time updates on fulfillment, shipping, and delivery, saving customers time and effort.</p><p>For example, an e-commerce customer wanting to know their order status would typically wait for an agent or search the website. A self-service chatbot streamlines this by allowing customers to input their order number and receive detailed status and delivery information instantly.</p><p>Many customer inquiries involve basic questions already addressed in your website's FAQs. IVR systems empower callers to navigate menus and resolve inquiries without speaking to live agents, enabling quicker routing, shorter wait times, and faster issue resolution.</p><p>For instance, a customer who is facing long wait times due to limited agent availability while reporting a software issue can instead use an IVR system to be guided through troubleshooting steps, resolving their issue quickly and independently.</p><p>Plivo CX offers a fully customizable <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/ivr">IVR solution</a> to facilitate automated first-touch interactions. Analyze customer support metrics</h3><p>You must analyze baseline metrics that help you identify areas for improvement in your customer service. Here's a breakdown of four important metrics you should track and what they signify:</p><ol><li><strong>First response time (FRT)</strong>: This measures how quickly support agents respond to customer inquiries. <a href="https://www.geckoboard.com/best-practice/kpi-examples/first-response-time/">Based on industry standards</a>, customers anticipate receiving an email response within a day. It's best to respond within an hour or less for social media inquiries. The average first response time on phone calls is three minutes or less.</li></ol><ol start="2"><li><strong>Resolution time</strong>: This metric tracks the average time it takes to resolve customer issues. A good average resolution time is five minutes. Anything lower indicates highly efficient problem-solving by your team.</li></ol><ol start="3"><li><strong>Average handle time (AHT):</strong> This reflects an agent's average time handling a customer inquiry, including resolution time and post-interaction tasks. A good AHT is <a href="https://www.zendesk.com/blog/average-handle-time/">around six minutes</a>. Anything higher suggests inefficient processes, complex issues requiring extensive troubleshooting, or lengthy customer explanations.</li></ol><ol start="4"><li><strong>Customer satisfaction (CSAT) score</strong>: CSAT measures customer satisfaction based on feedback provided after interacting with support. CSAT scores vary by industry, but a good score is somewhere between <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/customer-satisfaction-score">75% and 85%</a>. A high CSAT score indicates satisfied customers, while a low score means you need to improve.</li></ol><p>Plivo CX offers a robust <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/metrics">reporting and analytics</a> suite that empowers you to track these critical customer support metrics.
