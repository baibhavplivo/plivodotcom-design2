Here’s how:</p><ul><li><strong>Instant &amp; reliable</strong> – Deliver time-sensitive messages directly to customers.</li><li><strong>Increased conversions </strong>– Timely promotions and reminders lead to more completed purchases.</li><li><strong>Cost-effective &amp; scalable</strong> – Automate outreach without spending heavily on ads.</li><li><strong>Stronger customer relationships</strong> – Keep customers informed and engaged, building long-term loyalty.</li><li><strong>Enhanced security</strong> – Used for OTPs, fraud alerts, and verification.</li></ul><p>Effective customer communication is key, and SMS alerts provide a direct and reliable way to engage your audience.</p><p>Now, let’s compare SMS alerts with push notifications and understand how they each serve different purposes.</p><h2><strong>SMS alerts vs. push notifications – What’s the difference?</strong></h2><p>Both SMS alerts and push notifications keep users informed, but they work differently. Here’s how they work:</p><p><strong>Step 1: Customer opt-in:</strong> Customers opt in through a simple action, like texting a keyword or submitting their phone number online. This ensures compliance and respects customer preferences.</p><p><strong>Step 2: Message creation:</strong> Businesses can now send personalized or automated SMS alerts based on triggers</p><p><strong>Step 3: Message delivery:</strong> The SMS goes through an SMS gateway and reaches the customer’s phone directly.</p><p>To make sure your SMS alerts are compliant with the law, it’s essential to understand the regulations involved. Here's what you need to know.</p><ul><li><strong>Opt-in requirement:</strong> Customers must provide clear consent before receiving messages. This is usually done by confirming their phone number or texting a keyword (e.g., “JOIN”).</li><li><strong>Unsubscribe option:</strong> Every SMS alert must include an opt-out option, allowing customers to easily stop receiving messages by texting “STOP.”</li><li><strong>Privacy compliance:</strong> Businesses must ensure they handle customer data securely and comply with privacy laws like <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">GDPR</a> or <a href="https://www.fdic.gov/regulations/compliance/manual/8/viii-5.1.pdf">TCPA</a>.</li></ul><p>By following these steps and regulations, businesses can use SMS alerts to communicate with customers while maintaining trust and legal compliance effectively.&nbsp;</p><h2><strong>Benefits of using SMS alerts in e-commerce</strong></h2><p>SMS alerts drive engagement for e-commerce businesses, improving customer satisfaction and boosting sales.
