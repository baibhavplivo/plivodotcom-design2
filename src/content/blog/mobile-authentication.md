<p>In 2024, the global average cost of a data breach reached an all-time high of <a href="https://www.ibm.com/reports/data-breach">$4.88 million</a> — a 10% increase from the previous year. Secure solutions like mobile device authentication are just one approach to lowering these costs.</p><p>What is mobile authentication? Simply put, it involves verifying a user's identity using a mobile device along with one or more authentication methods to ensure secure access.&nbsp;</p><p>In this post, we’ll explore why mobile authentication is essential, its best practices, and how Plivo’s <a href="https://www.plivo.com/verify/">Verify API</a> makes the process easy and cost-effective.</p><p>{{cta-style-1}}</p><h2>Optimize mobile authentication for every customer&nbsp;</h2><p>How you implement mobile device authentication depends on the user’s context. Let’s look at three examples to understand it better.</p><h3>Two-factor authentication (2FA) or push notifications during login&nbsp;&nbsp;</h3><p><a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/what-is-two-factor-authentication-2fa/">Two-factor authentication</a> (2FA) enhances security by requiring users to provide an additional piece of information beyond their password. In addition to their password, users may need to enter a security token, like an OTP. This extra layer helps protect the account against unauthorized access.</p><p>Users who log into your application can either enter an OTP or receive a push notification to approve or deny the login attempt. The first scenario adds an extra security layer, while the second provides a smoother, friction-free user experience.</p><h4>Implementation tips:&nbsp;</h4><ul><li>Consider the user experience and your security needs when selecting an authentication factor.&nbsp;</li><li>Pair a password with a mobile-based <a href="https://www.plivo.com/use-case/two-factor-authentication/">one-time passcode (OTP)</a> sent via SMS, voice, or generated by an authenticator app.</li></ul><p><strong><em>&nbsp;</em></strong>For even stronger internal authentication, implement MFA with biometric data or physical tokens.&nbsp;</p><h3>Biometric mobile authentication for high-value transactions</h3><p>Biometric authentication adds an extra layer of protection, making it harder for fraudsters to intercept or misuse the OTP. This means users can confidently conduct high-value transactions knowing their data is secure, all while enjoying a smooth user flow. It’s a win-win for both the user and the business.&nbsp;</p><h4>Implementation tips:&nbsp;</h4><ul><li>Use biometric authentication for sensitive actions like changing payment methods or accessing account information.&nbsp;</li><li>Implement a biometric system depending on the capabilities of the user's device. Use fingerprint recognition on devices supporting it and facial recognition on those with sensors.&nbsp;</li><li>Prompt users periodically to re-enroll their biometric data after a software update.&nbsp;</li></ul><h3>Adaptive authentication for viewing or managing sensitive data&nbsp;</h3><p>Adaptive authentication enhances security by adjusting the authentication requirements based on the context of the access attempt, such as the location or device used.&nbsp;</p><p>So, if a customer tries to access sensitive information from an unfamiliar device or unusual location, they may be required to undergo additional verification such as receiving an OTP on their mobile device or confirming their identity via a secondary email.</p><h3>Implementation tips:&nbsp;</h3><ul><li>Choose an authentication provider offering adaptive authentication capabilities such as Auth0, Microsoft Azure, etc.&nbsp;</li><li>Identify risk levels and triggers such as unusual locations, unknown devices, or unusual access times.&nbsp;</li><li>Implement context-based policies such as requiring additional OTP verification or biometric checks for new device access, or using MFA and real-time fraud detection during high-risk transactions.&nbsp;</li></ul><h2>Why implement mobile authentication</h2><p>Without mobile authentication, unauthorized individuals easily breach your systems, leading to potential data theft. However, the benefits of implementing a mobile authentication system go beyond preventing unauthorized access.&nbsp;</p><h3>Increased customer retention&nbsp;</h3><p>Improving mobile authentication also improves customer retention.&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/alex-vasylenko/">Alex Vasylenko</a>, a tech entrepreneur and founder of The Frontend Company, witnessed a 15% customer retention rate increase and a 25% successful logins after implementing mobile authentication.&nbsp;</p><p>Alex noted, “We also saw a 30% drop in account-related support queries. Mobile authentication has positively impacted our customer experience and retention.”</p><h3>Fewer compliance risks&nbsp;</h3><p>Mobile authentication is your first line of defense against hefty fines due to noncompliance. With mobile authentication, you're saving your business from costly compliance issues.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>Protect your company’s reputation</strong>&nbsp;</h3><p>It’s not just about the immediate financial fallout — data breaches can seriously damage your company’s reputation. When your company’s security is compromised, customers lose trust in your brand, damaging your customer loyalty and market position.
