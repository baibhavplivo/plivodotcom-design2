Agents spend nearly <a href="https://www.salesforce.com/content/dam/web/en_us/www/documents/research/salesforce-state-of-sales-report-6-ed.pdf">70% of their week</a> on tasks that don’t drive revenue: searching for information, entering data, or answering repetitive queries.</p><p>While they jump between systems, customers are kept waiting. Supervisors catch these inefficiencies too late, and reactive tools only add to the delay.</p><p>Real‑time agent assist (RTAA) turns this around. It delivers the right information at the exact moment agents need it, helping them move faster and stay focused.</p><p>The results speak for themselves. Early adopters report a <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.11771">15% increase in agent productivity</a>.</p><p>Want to see how RTAA can transform your support ops? Let’s get into it.</p><h2>What is real‑time agent assist?</h2><p>Real‑time agent assist is a tool that gives support agents the information they need during a live call. It relies on <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/nlp-steps/">natural language processing (NLP)</a> and machine learning (ML) to understand what’s being said.</p><p>RTAA pulls relevant data from knowledge bases and customer relationship management (CRM) systems to show the best suggestions in real time. This cuts down on guesswork and lets agents handle issues more smoothly.</p><h2>How does real-time agent assist work?</h2><p>When an agent-customer call starts, the tool quietly works alongside them.</p><p>First, the system uses <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/introducing-general-availability-for-automatic-speech-recognition/">automatic speech recognition</a> (ASR). The technology listens to the conversation and turns spoken words into written text.</p><p>Next, both the text and voice inputs are sent to spoken language understanding (SLU), which analyzes the words to understand their meaning and the emotions behind them. Then, before anything appears on the agent's screen, all this processed information passes through the recommendation system.</p><p>It analyzes everything and decides what insights are most useful for the agent at that exact moment. Some information becomes real-time guidance, while other details might be shown with specific advice.</p><p>Finally, agents see these processed insights instantly on their screens.</p><table> <tbody><tr><td>💡Example: A customer calls and says, “I’ve been overcharged again, and no one’s fixing it. <li>The recommendation engine processes this data and identifies:</li> 
<ul>
 
<li>A billing issue is the topic</li>
 
<li>This is a repeat complaint</li>
 
<li>The customer is at escalated risk due to repeated unresolved issues</li> 
</ul>

<li>In under a second, the agent’s screen updates with:</li> 
<ul>

<li>Quick link: Pre-filled template for refund escalation.</li>
 
<li>Alert: “Repeat complaint; consider offering goodwill credit.”</li> 
</ul>

<li>The agent then calmly says, “I’m really sorry this has happened again. Let me pull up your previous charges and make this right immediately.”

They miss patterns like a surge in refund requests or customers repeatedly asking about the same policy.</p><p>Without real-time visibility, there’s no way to coach during the moment or step in before things escalate. Feedback feels disconnected, and agents miss the chance to improve where it matters most.</p><p>For example, a customer keeps pushing back on refund terms, and the agent hesitates. A missed upsell, a delay in apologizing, or a dropped follow-up often surfaces after the call ends, when it’s too late to fix.</p><p>Agents need prompts while the conversation is still happening.</p><p>For instance, during a billing call, a customer might ask if their current plan includes international coverage, signaling potential interest in an upgrade. Without a timely prompt, the agent moves on, missing a clear opportunity to upsell.</p><p>Without this real-time guidance, teams stay stuck in reactive mode as missed chances pile up, and customer trust takes a hit.</p><h2>Benefits of real-time agent assist</h2><p>With real-time support built into every step, agents don’t have to slow down or second-guess. Here’s how it plays out on live calls:</p><h3>Live call and screen monitoring</h3><p>Real-time tools follow both the conversation and what’s happening on the agent’s screen. If a customer asks a tricky question, the system can suggest a reply or surface the right info instantly.</p><p>The agent responds faster, and the call keeps moving.</p><h3>Support that works anywhere</h3><p>Agents get the same real-time guidance and assistance no matter where they’re based: at home, in the office, or working a hybrid schedule.</p><p>The system stays consistent across every setup, so there's no drop in support quality, no missing context, and no disruption to the customer experience.</p><h3>Faster fixes, lower costs</h3><p>When agents get instant guidance, they don’t need to put customers on hold or transfer the call. Problems get solved faster, customers don’t have to wait, and agents save time.</p><p>In the long run, this can bring down call costs without reducing service quality.</p><h3>Less agent churn</h3><p>Live calls are tough, especially when agents feel stuck. Real-time support takes some of that pressure off by offering help in the moment. It builds confidence, shortens training time, and makes the day-to-day feel less overwhelming.</p><h2>How to implement real-time agent assist</h2><p>Rolling out real-time agent assist successfully takes the right setup, the right tool, and a plan to make it stick.</p><p>Here’s how to do it step by step:</p><h3>Step #1: Assess readiness</h3><p>Before you roll out real-time agent assist, take stock of what you already have. Where do they lose time or get stuck? Knowing this helps you spot where RTAA can step in and where you might need to adjust workflows.</p><p>And don’t forget your customers. What are the recurring problems they face? Look for solutions with proven uptime, low latency, and robust application programming interface (API) coverage.</li><li><strong>No learning curve for agents:</strong> If it takes a training manual to use it, agents won’t. The tool should feel intuitive, like a natural extension of their workflow.</li></ul><p>That’s exactly what you get with Plivo’s Unified Agent Desktop.
