<p>Every customer call holds valuable information about what they really want and how they feel about your company. Now that we can improve customer experience with artificial intelligence (AI), manually reviewing hundreds of sales calls to analyze the most or least effective talking points is a thing of the past.</p><p>Analyzing phone calls with AI reveals what resonates with customers and what doesn’t. If your call center AI tools highlight frequent inquiries about billing issues or service plans, you need to refine your service plan documentation and improve billing transparency.</p><p>The global AI market in the call center segment is projected to exceed <a href="https://www.marketsandmarkets.com/Market-Reports/call-center-ai-market-263925467.html">$4 billion by 2027</a>. This means many businesses are using AI call analysis to save time and improve customer experience. Whether you’re new to AI call analysis or looking to improve your existing strategies, this blog offers valuable insights to help you succeed.</p><h2>How does AI call analysis work?</h2><p>You can use AI to analyze phone calls and find valuable insights into customer needs and preferences. Here are some key approaches:</p><h3>Transcribe audio to text</h3><p>An AI call analysis tool transcribes conversations from audio to text in real time. This lets you search through call transcripts for specific sentences or phrases without listening to every call.</p><p>Say you notice a sudden spike in complaints about a product or service. You can quickly surf through call transcripts and search for specific words to pinpoint customer complaints. This way, you derive meaningful insights from customer calls in less time.</p><h3>Sentiment analysis</h3><p>An AI-based call analysis tool goes beyond words. It finds trends in conversation, including repeated keywords, speech patterns, and even customer sentiments and tone.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>The tool extracts emotions from various sources, including customer emails, chatbot histories, customer service call transcripts, comments on returns and refunds, and customer feedback or surveys.</p><h3>Keywords and topic detection</h3><p>Phone call analysis with AI automatically identifies key topics and keywords discussed during calls. This helps you spot trends, like recurring questions about a particular feature or a product, allowing you to update FAQs, training materials, or even product documentation.</p><p>The benefit? It significantly reduces call handling times and pushes agents to provide accurate and timely responses, automatically improving customer service.</p><h3>Predictive analysis</h3><p>AI call analysis has the power to analyze past interactions and predict future behaviors. So if a customer frequently asks about a product upgrade, you might proactively reach out with customized offers or share new product updates with those customers.</p><p>Using AI for customer service also facilitates market segmentation. Analyzing data on customer behavior, preferences, and demographics helps you identify segments that are likely to be the most profitable.&nbsp;</p><p>This approach lets you focus on high-value customer segments, leading to lower costs for acquiring new customers.</p><h2>Benefits of using AI to analyze phone calls</h2><p>Analyzing phone calls with AI offers numerous advantages that can transform customer service operations, including:</p><h3>Learning customer sentiments</h3><p>Talking to hundreds of customers each day, it’s easy to misinterpret a good call for a bad one and vice versa. An AI-powered sentiment analysis helps understand how customers feel during interactions. It assesses the tone and emotion behind their words for you to pinpoint areas of frustration or satisfaction and tackle them head-on.</p><p>For example, if AI detects a high level of frustration in calls about a specific service, it prompts you to inform your marketing team or provide additional training to agents who may lack the necessary information to effectively address customer concerns.</p><h3>Identifying trends</h3><p>AI for customer service uncovers emerging trends in customer inquiries and concerns. Analyzing call data over time reveals recurring issues or topics, such as a rise in questions about a new product feature.</p><p>Use this insight to adapt your offerings, enhance marketing strategies, or update training materials to better equip your team.</p><h3>Improving training and development initiatives</h3><p>AI call analysis also identifies common challenges agents face.
