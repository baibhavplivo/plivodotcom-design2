<p>The clock strikes 8 am. You mount your desk only to be greeted with dozens of unresolved tickets from the previous day and a row of new ones. This has been the norm over the past few months.&nbsp;</p><p>It used to be manageable, but now, as your business scales, your ticket queue is gradually spiralling out of control.&nbsp;</p><p>Believe us, you’re not alone; many customer support managers face the same challenge. Ticket overload can result in team burnout, decrease the quality of support to customers, and dent your brand reputation if not well managed.&nbsp;</p><p>Unsurprisingly, <a href="https://khoros.com/blog/must-know-customer-service-statistics">65%</a> of customers will switch to another brand after a bad experience with your support team.</p><p>And that’s why you need AI agents. Let’s see how these models can reduce your support tickets without raising costs or compromising quality.</p><h2>How do AI agents deflect support tickets</h2><p>AI agents are advanced AI models equipped with ML, NLP, and reinforcement learning algorithms to accurately analyze, interpret, and execute tasks. Most importantly, they are autonomous—meaning AI agents are capable of self-reasoning instead of constantly depending on user inputs.</p><p>To deflect support tickets, AI agents employ four automated approaches: ticket analysis, categorization, routing, and sentiment analysis. Let’s take a detailed look:</p><h3>1. AI-powered ticket analysis</h3><p>When a customer logs a complaint, your AI agent instantly analyzes the ticket using ML and NLP to understand its context. It assesses priority, urgency, and complexity while identifying whether:</p><p>‍</p><ul><li>You’re dealing with a first-time complaint from a new user</li><li>It’s a follow-up on an unresolved issue</li><li>It’s a new concern from a previously satisfied customer</li></ul><h3>2. Smart categorization</h3><p>What comes next is categorization based on a combination of the metrics above. By urgency, tickets can be grouped into:</p><ul><li>High urgency needing immediate response, such as in a product security crisis or VIP complaints</li><li>Standard resolution time following the SLA, such as in general troubleshooting</li><li>Low urgency in cases of feedback requests and suggestions</li></ul><p>By priority, we have:</p><ul><li>High priority when it affects the customer’s business operations, like in service outages</li><li>Medium priority when it affects users but not business critical. Example is a feature malfunction</li><li>Low priority if it’s just general requests and non-urgent inquiries</li></ul><p>Lastly, based on complexity:</p><ul><li>Basic L1 requests such as repetitive inquiries</li><li>L2 complaints, such as product bugs, and usually require technical interventions</li><li>L3 tickets featuring highly technical issues like API failures</li></ul><p>Another salient categorization method is based on the ticket’s relevant department. Complaints about subscriptions fall under billing and payments, while logins and password resets fall under account management.</p><h3>3. Intelligent routing</h3><p>Following the categorization above, an L1 ticket with low urgency and low priority will be automatically routed to AI chatbots. If it’s L1 with low urgency and high priority, it will still likely be routed to an AI chatbot but will be addressed before other L1 tickets of lower priority.</p><p>On the other hand, all L2 and L3 tickets are automatically assigned to relevant service reps or senior support agents. The urgency and priority of the tickets determine which ones are assigned first.</p><p>Your AI agent also considers the status of your support agents before assigning them a ticket. This includes their availability, ticket load, previous tickets successfully closed, categories of ticket topics they find easier to resolve, and how fast they complete the task.</p><h3>4. Post-support sentiment analysis</h3><p>Traditionally, you can collect post-support feedback through surveys or thumbs up/down options. However, AI agents take a more efficient route which involves using NLP to analyze the tone, phrases, or keywords used by customers during support resolution.</p><p>If there’s a negative sentiment, it triggers follow-up actions or even escalations. For neutral sentiments, AI agents consider what could have been done better.</p><ul><li>For instance, if the negative sentiment is because of agent-customer mismatch, your AI agent uses available data to refine its routing approach.</li><li>If a ticket was successfully resolved but slowly, resulting in neutral sentiment, the AI model decides whether to increase the urgency of such issues in the future.</li></ul><p>Also, compiled reports from these analyses can help your service reps refine their strategies and customer resolution approach.</p><h2>Benefits of AI agents on ticket resolution</h2><p>According to Hubspot’s 2024 State of Service report, teams adopting AI have reported up to a <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/state-of-service-report">30%</a> reduction in their support volume. 92% say it has helped improve their response time. This is as a result of the following:</p><h3>Faster categorization and routing</h3><p>Manual ticket sorting is a repetitively monotonous task and there are two problems with it.&nbsp;</p><p>First, it’s slow—imagine having to categorize over 100 tickets in a day and still expecting to deliver a response to each complaint in less than 10 minutes. That’s virtually impossible.</p><p>Second, it consumes your service reps’ time and prevents them from focusing on the majors.</p><p>AI agents eliminate these outcomes and handle all the categorization processes in seconds, freeing your team to handle core tasks. This optimizes time to first response and time to resolution, which, in turn, enables faster response times to your customers.</p><h3>Minimized ticket collision</h3><p>Ticket collision is when two service reps unknowingly work on the same ticket, whether in parallel time or one after the other. This is usually as a result of errors in manual routing, delayed update of ticket status, and or lack of a centralized agent dashboard to track backend activities.</p><p>AI agents minimize the incidence of these errors and ensure each ticket gets updated—pending, ongoing, active, or closed—in real-time so as to avoid collision.&nbsp;</p><p>As an omnichannel platform designed to scale your support team, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/uad">Plivo CX’s Unified Agent Desktop</a> also helps your reps visualize ticket statuses and eliminate collision.</p><figure><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67fe0a69212c5c0afcd8d4c6_67d9ae7c329679c6dc254b33_AD_4nXc7EVgoWD1e1xl-cJQ2OupzFTILDJENALaSlbL7_dIffnTIRfgchiZqzjbQa32p9lx_X3ku5Zg-nqeHZyEyAJz2CrXEJWxE8CiC0107nBTAHuesDLX-7M4_m_qOCc0Ls5VKxUVBVQ.png" alt="Image showing Plivo CX's UAD in action" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption>Monitor ticket statuses with a unified system to avoid ticket collision</figcaption></figure><h3>Reduced service operational cost</h3><p>A surge in tickets either means your brand is in high demand or you’re turning off your customers really bad. And that’s cost-ineffective, especially if you have a limited budget.</p><p>AI agents reduce costs by handling the bulk of ticket management, no matter the volume. In fact, according to <a href="https://www.bain.com/insights/retaining-customers-is-the-real-challenge/">Bain &amp; Company</a>, increasing retention by 5% can boost profits by as much as 95%.</p><h3>Less employee burnout and more efficiency</h3><p>Ticket overload can result in customer service burnout.
