<p>Voice commerce is a <a href="https://www.thebusinessresearchcompany.com/report/voice-commerce-global-market-report">$421.7 billion</a> opportunity.</p><p>Yes, more than 27% of U.S. consumers made <a href="https://www.statista.com/topics/5406/voice-commerce-in-the-united-states/#topicOverview">online payments with voice assistants</a> instead of manually browsing multiple screens and navigating the checkout process. Devices like Amazon Echo, Google Home, and Samsung SmartThings now act as virtual shopping assistants.</p><p>With the number of voice search users anticipated to reach <a href="https://www.statista.com/statistics/1299985/voice-assistant-users-us/">157.1 million by 2026</a>, voice commerce opens up a faster purchasing channel. For businesses, this means a higher conversion rate, stronger sales, and streamlined customer support.</p><p>In this blog post, we’ll explore voice commerce, how it works, and its benefits for your business.</p><h2>What is voice commerce?</h2><p>Voice commerce, or v-commerce, is a technology-driven shopping experience that allows customers to search for, purchase, and manage orders using voice commands. It reduces the reliance on screens and keyboards during the buying process.</p><p>The technology relies on two components: <strong>smart speakers</strong> that convert speech into digital commands and <strong>AI assistants</strong> that interpret these commands and take action. Customers can shop hands-free while cooking, driving, or multitasking.</p><h2>How does voice commerce work?</h2><figure style="max-width:657px" data-rt-max-width="657px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67d2744265d39aa60f449f73_AD_4nXccYRCeJ2BPcEZXq96tr-gx2uQkNGg1h0CKfUHMmzRL5sdG3Gxl4wVjD4iAk7Gs9XElUwaDI4SZcrXokY01QGQKCe9ClQtch-4VK9Rph-0oV7NhYU5rBF1xnmIMRkEuLRaCFGrc5Q.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Voice command conversion process for online shopping transactions" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.plivo.com/ai-voice-agents/">Source</a></figcaption></figure><p>The process starts when your customer speaks a command into voice-enabled devices. While it seems simple on the surface (the customer speaks and the device responds), the technology performs several steps to complete each task. These steps include:</p><ul><li><strong>Converting speech to actionable data:</strong> Your customer's spoken command gets captured and converted to text through <a href="https://www.plivo.com/voice/automatic-speech-recognition/">automatic speech recognition (ASR)</a> algorithms. For example, when they say <em>"buy new headphones,"</em> the system captures each word.</li><li><strong>Understanding customer intent:</strong> The system analyzes this text to understand what your customer wants. It breaks down the command to identify the action<em> (buying)</em> and the product <em>(headphones)</em>. This involves natural language processing (NLP) to interpret the meaning behind the words.</li><li><strong>Finding the right products: </strong>Once the system understands the intent, it searches your product database or online store for relevant items. It pulls up matching products, prices, and availability.</li><li><strong>Creating the right response: </strong>Then, the system creates a response based on its findings. If multiple headphone options exist, it might ask for more specifics about the price range or features your customer wants.</li><li><strong>Completing transaction:</strong> The customer can then confirm their choice, and the system completes the transaction — adding headphones to the cart, processing payment, or scheduling the delivery.</li></ul><p>When you implement voice commerce for your business, pay attention to the following:</p><ul><li>Speech recognition must handle different accents and background noise.</li><li>Your product database needs clear categorization for accurate matching.</li><li>Response systems should sound natural while providing accurate information.</li></ul><p><a href="https://www.plivo.com/ai-voice-agents/">Plivo’s AI voice agent</a> makes integrating voice commerce simple. The system must accurately capture and convert voice commands to text, even with different accents and noisy backgrounds.
