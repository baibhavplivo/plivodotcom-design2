<p>Ever wondered how companies get your phone number to send you text messages?</p><p>The answer lies in SMS opt-ins. It helps in building a direct communication channel with customers, allowing businesses to send targeted marketing messages.</p><p>According to Forbes, <a href="https://www.forbes.com/councils/theyec/2024/02/05/is-sms-marketing-an-affordable-small-business-growth-booster/">SMS has a 98% open rate</a>—3.7x higher than email (26.8%). Also, 90% of people reply within 30 minutes, making it perfect for urgent messages.</p><p>However, it's crucial to know the importance of obtaining consent with laws such as <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">GDPR</a>, <a href="https://www.fdic.gov/regulations/compliance/manual/8/viii-5.1.pdf">TCPA</a>, etc., before sending messages. Consent is required to comply with legal regulations. Ignoring these regulations can lead to severe consequences.</p><p>However, obtaining consent demands a thorough compliance with privacy-focused laws such as GDPR and TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) before sending messages.</p><p>Companies that don't follow these laws can face serious consequences. For example, in 2021, <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2021-07-30/amazon-given-record-888-million-eu-fine-for-data-privacy-breach">Amazon was fined a whopping €746 million (about $888 million)</a> for violating GDPR rules in Europe.&nbsp;</p><p>In this article, let’s explore SMS opt-ins are and find out some real-world SMS opt-in message examples.</p><h2>What is an opt-in text message?</h2><p>An opt-in text message is a permission-based SMS campaign where subscribers explicitly consent to receive marketing messages from a business. This process involves more than just collecting phone numbers; it's about establishing a clear agreement between the company and the customer.</p><h3>Key types of SMS opt-in procedures</h3><p>Typically, customers agree to receive opt-in SMS by texting a keyword, giving the business permission to send them messages. The three primary types of SMS opt-in procedures are as follows:</p><h4>1.Single opt-in</h4><p>A single opt-in is the simplest process where a recipient gives their consent in one step without any confirmation.</p><p><strong>Example</strong>:</p><figure style="max-width:500px" data-rt-max-width="500px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67fe0a7614815d95b02969c8_674e20ae424f625d16c298c8_AD_4nXesyGjyLwM6MSCWEFn_q2x7MQRYRZ8Bj800Tdl89oAM6HXB-4lJ7X65r8wBNxjxL9zRCJiB-YVfFH0ODyNoCXLNoNJijPCNLcNvY99QbcU2UsU-56CJ5euE71eNl1QOoBA2v7YnCQ.png" alt="" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://textspot.io/resources/free-compliance-text/">Source</a></figcaption></figure><h4>2.Double opt-in</h4><p>Double opt-in involves an extra layer of verification. After the initial consent, the recipient receives a confirmation message (e.g., “Reply YES to confirm”). This ensures the number belongs to someone genuinely interested.</p><p><strong>Example</strong>:&nbsp;</p><figure style="max-width:281px" data-rt-max-width="281px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67fe0a7514815d95b02969ae_674e20ab5dab6d5fadeb63f9_AD_4nXfMUCzFo_eBEwoVeA9sog3MqxvhoqcRzbItDb321RPY0jHJ3PZW_9Vk-P80ms25seBnGC0UyM8tkM_UBwfUP-dYizkkrBE41snDQS3PYbdN73HNEsRpPCB8BxGs7_PxY00gHFq0.png" alt="" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://support.yotpo.com/docs/sms-double-opt-insms-double-opt-in-information-for-us-shoppers">Source</a></figcaption></figure><h4>3.Soft opt-in&nbsp;</h4><p>The "soft opt-in" rule lets businesses send marketing messages to customers who've bought from them before, without needing explicit permission each time. Any company selling directly to consumers can use it, as long as they're promoting similar products or services. This is especially handy for businesses with repeat customers, like retail shops or subscription services.<br></p><p>But there are some important rules to follow. The customer's details must have been collected during a sale or negotiation, and they must be given an easy way to opt out in every message. It doesn't work for charities, political groups, or new contacts from bought lists.</p><p>Misusing this rule can lead to hefty fines, as <a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-9688069/Papa-John-s-fined-sending-customers-unlawful-nuisance-marketing-messages.html">Papa John's found out</a> when they were hit with a £10,000 penalty.</p><p><strong>Example</strong>: "Thank you for your recent purchase! Don’t miss our latest offers: [link]. Reply STOP to opt out."</p><h2>Additional types of SMS opt-in processes&nbsp;</h2><p>Prospects can consent to receive text messages from your business in the following five ways as well:</p><h3>1.Verbal consent</h3><p>The individuals can verbally consent to receive SMS messages over the phone or in person. Businesses must document this consent and a summary of the conversation.</p><h3>2.Website form opt-in</h3><p>Customers can provide consent by filling out a form on your website. This can be a dedicated opt-in form or a form embedded in other website content.</p><h3>3.Paper form opt-in</h3><p>Customers can consent by completing a physical form in-store. The form should clearly state that the customer is opting in to receive SMS messages and an explicit opt-in message.</p><h3>4.Text-to-join</h3><p>Your contacts can initiate the opt-in process by texting a keyword or specific phrase to your business's designated number.&nbsp;</p><h3>5.QR code opt-in</h3><p>Customers subscribe by scanning a QR code, which prompts them to enter their phone number to opt in. This code can be on posters, product packaging, or your website.</p><h2>SMS regulations and compliance: An outline</h2><p><em>Please note that SMS regulations and compliance requirements can vary by country and jurisdiction. It's essential to consult with legal counsel to ensure full compliance This information is provided for general knowledge only and does not constitute legal advice.</em></p><p>In 2024, IBM notes that the <a href="https://www.ibm.com/reports/data-breach">global average cost of a data breach hit $4.88 million</a>, a 10% rise from the previous year. This shows the growing financial impact of cybersecurity incidents worldwide.​</p><h3>Complying with SMS regulations</h3><p>To ensure compliance with SMS regulations, it's necessary to adhere to specific guidelines. Here are some key points to remember:</p><ul><li><strong>Explicit consent:</strong> As noted before, obtain clear consent from customers before sending promotional or marketing text messages.</li><li><strong>Clear opt-out instructions:</strong> Provide easy-to-follow instructions for customers to opt out of receiving messages. This can typically be done by texting a specific keyword like "STOP."</li><li><strong>Frequency:</strong> Be mindful of the frequency and timing of your messages. Aim to send about 4-5 texts per month to start, and don't go over 10-15 unless you want customers hitting that unsubscribe button.&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Timing</strong>: Here, <a href="https://thriveagency.com/news/the-complete-guide-to-sms-marketing/">Adhere to the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) regulations</a>—no texts before 8 AM or after 9 PM in the recipient's time zone. Mid-afternoon usually works best, but avoid Mondays and rush hours. The sweet spot is between 10 AM and 8 PM.</li><li><strong>Carrier compliance:</strong> Adhere to carrier-specific guidelines and regulations.</li></ul><h3>Compliance measures regarding promotional and transactional messages</h3><p>When sending SMS messages, it’s important to distinguish between promotional and transactional messages. They both serve different purposes and are subject to varying compliance regulations from one country to another. Here’s how:</p><h4>1.Promotional messages</h4><p>These messages are designed to enable customer engagement, such as making a purchase or taking advantage of a special offer. Promotional messages generally include texts about discounts, flash sales, and exclusive promotions.&nbsp;</p><p>Marketing SMS faces stricter rules that vary by country, including the US and UK. For instance:</p><ul><li><strong>U.S</strong><ul><li>The <a href="https://www.fdic.gov/regulations/compliance/manual/8/viii-5.1.pdf">Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)</a> requires explicit opt-in consent before sending promotional SMS.</li><li>Messages cannot be sent before 8 AM or after 9 PM in the recipient's local time.</li></ul></li><li><strong>UK</strong><ul><li>The <a href="https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2003/2426">Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR)</a> mandates businesses to obtain prior consent for promotional texts.</li></ul></li></ul><figure style="max-width:709px" data-rt-max-width="709px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67fe0a7614815d95b02969f6_674e20ab943992dec4ba57fc_AD_4nXelEk71X0d7ezFu3BM5Fv19pB6eO2yHXgxdZT3vHtxBbNnnaQnvjywhP1jpWmoUyv1BG76JOoyqB9w5L2jly1Blo3nQvzI9HPDr659woT9oJpTUh5c4NCTOI99_DrmAVEShnstqUg.png" alt="" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://smsarchives.com/messages/goodwill-text-message-marketing-example-08-16-2021/">Source</a></figcaption></figure><h4>2.Transactional messages</h4><p>Transactional messages provide essential information and are subject to fewer regulations. Common examples include order confirmations, password resets, and two-factor authentication codes.</p><ul><li><strong>U.S</strong><ul><li>TCPA rules allow transactional messages without explicit opt-in, provided the recipient’s number was collected as part of the transaction.</li></ul></li><li><strong>UK</strong><ul><li>PECR allows transactional messages without prior consent, provided they are necessary for fulfilling a contract or providing a requested service.<br></li></ul></li></ul><p>SMS marketing compliance varies by country, with regions like Canada, Australia, and the EU enforcing strict privacy rules. Businesses should also understand local regulations, like <a href="https://chamber.ca/">CASL</a> in Canada or <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">GDPR</a> in the EU, to ensure global compliance.</p><h2>SMS opt-in message examples</h2><p>Here are some practical SMS opt-in message examples used by brands in different business scenarios:</p><h3>SMS marketing</h3><p><strong>Popup
