<p>To minimize spam texting in India, the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) mandates that all entities that want to send SMS messages must register information such as their entity/organization and message templates on distributed ledger technology (DLT) platforms. Read our blog post for complete details.</p><p>Part of the registration process requires organizations to register message content templates. A content template includes the actual text of an SMS message to be sent, with space designated for variables where appropriate, such as an account number or balance.</p><p><a href="https://plivo-webflow.webflow.io/sms">SMS message</a> content falls into four categories, and therefore so do content templates: transactional, service implicit, service explicit, and promotional. This table shows each type of content and shows how they differ from each other.</p><style>

<p style="font-size: 14px;">*The first character of a promotional header must start with the numeral that represents the business category that matches the category preference in National Customer Preference Register (NCPR) (DND Registry). 1 — Banking/insurance/financial products/credit cards 2 — Real estate 3 — Education 4 — Health 5 — Consumer goods and automobiles 6 — Communication/broadcasting/entertainment/IT 7 — Tourism and leisure 8 — Food and beverages</p><p>Finally, certain types of messages are always prohibited: political messaging during elections and gambling- and cryptocurrency-related content.</p><p>We’ve included all the information you need to know about registering on DLT platforms into an <a href="https://support.plivo.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046769131-DLT-Registration-Process-for-Sending-SMS-to-India">article in our support portal</a>. It should guide you through registration for whichever content types you plan to use.</p><p>‍</p>
