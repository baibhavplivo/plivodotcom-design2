<p>Gone are the days when customers used to interact with brands on just one platform. Now, they may check a product online and go to the nearest brand store to pick it up if they need it urgently.</p><p>Alternatively, they may see a product in-store but order it online to take advantage of offers. They may also spot a product randomly on Instagram and then Google your store name. Finally, they may purchase the products after seeing the retargeting ads.&nbsp;</p><p>So, it all boils down to this: a customer flow is not linear anymore. Therefore, creating a unified experience across all touchpoints has become non-negotiable. Omnichannel Shopify marketing can help you with this.&nbsp;</p><p>In this post, we will cover how you can create a Shopify omnichannel marketing strategy and why you need one. You'll also discover some standout examples for inspiration.</p><h2>What is omnichannel marketing?&nbsp;</h2><p>Omnichannel marketing is a customer-focused strategy that integrates all channels (physical store, website, social media, email, sms, etc) a business uses to interact with customers.&nbsp;</p><p>This strategy creates a consistent experience for the customer regardless of the channel and helps to create a strong brand identity.&nbsp;</p><p>The numbers speak for themselves: brands witness a remarkable <a href="https://embryo.com/blog/omnichannel-marketing-statistics/">30% higher</a> lifetime value from customers who engage across multiple channels.&nbsp;</p><p>Furthermore, omnichannel Shopify marketing isn't limited to engagement; it directly impacts the bottom line and brings in nearly <a href="https://www.bettercommerce.io/blog/omnichannel-statistics-every-business-needs-to-know-for-2024">6X more sales</a> than single-channel approaches.&nbsp;</p><p>And the benefits don't stop there; companies prioritizing omnichannel customer engagement see a staggering <a href="https://www.bettercommerce.io/blog/omnichannel-statistics-every-business-needs-to-know-for-2024">89% customer retention rate</a>, with the potential to increase sales by an <a href="https://wisernotify.com/blog/omnichannel-stats/">impressive 287%</a>.</p><p>Brands have been using multiple channels for a while, so how does omnichannel marketing differ from the usual multichannel marketing? It's the brand's outlook on how to operate these channels. They can manage it independently by using different tools and may use different branding on various channels.&nbsp;</p><p>On the other hand, omnichannel marketing takes a more organic and unified approach. This strategy integrates all channels and aims to provide consumers with a more seamless and consistent brand experience. </p><table>  <tbody><tr>   <td><strong>Aspects</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Multichannel</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Omnichannel</strong>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Key focus</strong>   </td>   <td>Brand   </td>   <td>Customer   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Channels</strong>   </td>   <td>Multiple independent channels   </td>   <td>Multiple integrated channels   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Brand experience and messaging</strong>   </td>   <td>May vary across channels    </td>   <td>Consistent across channels    </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Personalization</strong>   </td>   <td>Only possible based on data available in the current channel   </td>   <td>Data can be used across channels for personalization    </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Customer journey</strong>   </td>   <td>Can only check individual channels   </td>   <td>Can analyze customer touchpoints across channels   </td>  </tr></tbody></table><h2>Why Shopify brands need an omnichannel marketing strategy&nbsp;</h2><p>For any growing Shopify brand, there are numerous marketing strategies, and in all of this, why should you prioritize the omnichannel Shopify marketing strategy?&nbsp;</p><p>Here are the top five reasons:</p><h3>1. Creates a strong brand presence</h3><p>An omnichannel marketing strategy helps you reach customers across multiple channels. And on top of it also ensures you have a consistent brand message, visuals, and voice across all channels, from social media to online/physical stores. This strategy solidifies your brand identity in front of customers and creates a strong presence.&nbsp;</p><h3>2. Provides better customer insights</h3><p>With an omnichannel marketing approach, you get a 360-degree view by integrating data from all channels. It helps to pull out key details like:</p><ul><li>Customer journey analysis</li><li>Cost per impression</li><li>Click-through rate</li><li>Multi-channel engagement rate</li><li>Cross-channel conversion rate</li><li>Customer acquisition cost</li><li>Average order value</li><li>Customer lifetime value</li><li>Customer satisfaction score</li><li>Retention rate</li><li>Return on investment.</li></ul><h3>3. Boosts personalized marketing</h3><p>Another benefit of having consolidated data across channels is you can better personalize your campaigns. You can send targeted recommendations to customers based on their interests and purchase history.&nbsp;</p><p>You can also analyze behavior, segment data, and run targeted campaigns.</p><h3>4. Improves customer service</h3><p>An omnichannel approach also improves customer service in two ways:</p><ul><li><strong>Ability to reach customers where they are</strong>: Some customers choose to reach out via social media, some use live chat on the website, and some prefer to email. An omnichannel strategy helps you to reach users where they are active.</li><li><strong>Advanced personalization</strong>: With a unified view of customer interactions across channels, the customer support team can easily look into all the past interactions of the customers. It ensures customers don't have to answer the same questions again and again (creating a poor brand experience). The customer support team can pick a conversation where it was left last and leave a positive impression.&nbsp;</li></ul><h2>How to create an omnichannel Shopify marketing strategy&nbsp;</h2><p>Creating a Shopify omnichannel marketing strategy involves understanding your customer's behavior across channels and creating a seamless experience across all touchpoints.&nbsp;</p><p>Here is a roadmap to accomplish this:</p><h3>1. Understand your target consumers</h3><p>The first step is understanding your customer. You can use your existing data or conduct customer interviews/surveys to gather more data. From this data, you can develop user personas.&nbsp;</p><p>A user persona is a representation of your target audience highlighting key details required for marketing, such as demographics, interests, values, online behavior, challenges, and more.&nbsp;</p><p>Sharing an example below for reference:</p><p><strong>Sample user persona:&nbsp;</strong></p><p><strong>Name</strong>: Catherine</p><p><strong>Description</strong>: Catherine is a young working professional struggling to manage time. She values quick shopping options and would prefer faster shipping over flash discounts.</p><p><strong>Demographics</strong>:</p><ul><li>Age: 25</li><li>Gender: Woman</li><li>Location: New York City, NY</li><li>Income: $60,000-$150,000</li></ul><p><strong>Interests</strong>: Fast fashion, Pop culture</p><p><strong>Values</strong>: Avoids brands with a history of animal cruelty</p><p><strong>Challenges</strong>: I want clothes that stand out and fit well, but don't have time to spend hours in the mall.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Online behavior: </strong>Tiktok, Instagram, YouTube</p><h3>2. Create a customer journey map</h3><p>After you have a user persona, you can create a customer journey map. A customer journey map is a visual representation of how a customer interacts with your business. It gives you a quick summary of the usual user activity across all touchpoints and helps you plan your campaigns accordingly.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Sample customer journey map:</strong></p><table>  <tbody><tr>   <td>   </td>   <td><strong>Awareness</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Consideration</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Purchase</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Retention</strong>   </td>   <td><strong>Loyalty</strong>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Customer actions</strong>   </td>   <td>Looking for a product   </td>   <td>Conducting research and evaluating the product   </td>   <td>Make a purchase   </td>   <td>Repeat order or buy add-ons   </td>   <td>Becomes a long-term customer and refer products to friends/family   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Channels</strong>   </td>   <td>TikTok, Instagram, and Google   </td>   <td>Website, YouTube, Instagram   </td>   <td>Website, mobile app   </td>   <td>SMS, email   </td>   <td>SMS, email, Instagram   </td>  </tr></tbody></table><p>Once you have a clear customer journey map, you can plan which campaigns to run on which channel.
