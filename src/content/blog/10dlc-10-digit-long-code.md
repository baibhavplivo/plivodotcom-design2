It establishes a credible, authentic, direct line of contact between a business owner (you) and a 10DLC recipient (your audience).&nbsp;</p><p>Here are some of the key benefits of 10DLC:&nbsp;</p><h3>1. Get higher messaging deliverability and low latency&nbsp;</h3><p>Since 10DLC numbers are authorized and vetted by TCR, messages sent via these numbers have a higher chance to pass through carrier spam filters as compared to messages sent via traditional long code numbers.&nbsp;</p><p>As a result, 10DLC campaigns tend to have higher SMS open rates, better customer engagement, and higher conversion rates.&nbsp;</p><p>For example, at <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/fluent-webinar-recap/">Fluent</a>, whose business revolves around customer acquisition, using Plivo as one of their cloud communications platforms for 10DLC helped their business meet its messaging goals.&nbsp;</p><p>Additionally, 10DLC has low latency, meaning there’s minimal delay between sending a message and its delivery to the targeted recipient. This is critical for time-sensitive communications, ensuring that messages are received almost in real-time, enhancing the effectiveness of customer interactions and services.&nbsp;</p><h3>2. Reduce costs and drive higher ROI</h3><p>10DLC provides a more affordable entry point for businesses to engage in A2P messaging. The lower cost barrier allows small and medium-sized businesses to effectively utilize SMS marketing and business communication.&nbsp;</p><p>Besides, 10DLC can be used for both A2P and P2P (Person-to-Person) messaging, so businesses can consolidate their messaging activities under one number, reducing the need for multiple numbers and the associated costs.</p><h3>3. Ensure better compliance</h3><p>10DLC minimizes the risk of non-compliance penalties, which can be costly. By adhering to carrier and regulatory guidelines, businesses can protect their reputation and avoid potential fines.&nbsp;</p><p>Additionally, using a compliant and carrier-approved messaging channel like 10DLC enhances a business’s reputation among consumers, which in turn, impacts brand affinity and customer retention.&nbsp;</p><h3>4. Provide a more localized experience&nbsp;</h3><p>Given 10DLC numbers maintain the standard 10-digit format familiar to users, they are perceived as local or regional numbers. Since a local phone number feels more familiar, it can increase the likelihood of message engagement, as recipients may be more inclined to trust and respond to messages from numbers that appear local.&nbsp;</p><p>It also presents a great opportunity for businesses to tailor their messaging campaigns to local audiences, increasing the relevance of their campaigns.&nbsp;</p><h2>What are the limitations of A2P 10DLC?</h2><p>While 10DLC comes with plenty of benefits, it also has a few limitations that could impact your business and specific needs.&nbsp;</p><h3>1. Lack of support for lengthy messages</h3><p>If you frequently send long SMS messages to your audience, you might find 10DLC limiting. That’s because 10DLC systems may impose restrictions on the length of messages that can be sent.&nbsp;</p><p>These restrictions may be challenging for companies trying to convey complex information or multiple calls to action within a single message.&nbsp;</p><h3>2. Appears like a regular phone number</h3><p>One of the strengths of 10DLC — its appearance as a standard, local phone number — can also be a double-edged sword. This feature may also result in a lower perceived authority or prestige compared to messaging from short codes, which are often associated with large-scale brands or nationwide campaigns.&nbsp;</p><p>For businesses seeking to establish a commanding brand presence, this might be seen as a drawback.</p><h2>Business messaging: who should consider using 10DLC?</h2><p>10DLC is an incredible solution packed with benefits, but certain businesses may find it more useful than others.
