<p>Delivering great customer experiences requires quick responses, personalized interactions, and communication through the customer’s preferred channels.&nbsp; Yet, managing high call volumes, addressing complex requests, and meeting ever-growing expectations can make this a significant challenge.</p><p>Modern contact center technologies can bridge this gap with tools that streamline operations, improve customer communication, and empower contact centers to meet customer expectations.</p><p>In this blog post, we’ll explore the top 12 contact center technologies transforming customer interactions and how they can benefit your business.</p><h2>1.VoIP</h2><p>A Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) system lets businesses make and receive phone calls over the Internet, removing the need for outdated landlines, desk phones, mobile devices, and computers. However, the benefits of switching from legacy landline systems to VoIP go beyond cost savings and improved call quality.</p><p>A reliable VoIP system:</p><ul><li>Eliminates the need for desk phones and outdated systems.</li><li>Offers add-ons like <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/two-factor-authentication/">two-factor authentication (2FA)</a> to verify callers and reduce fraud calls, smart interactive voice response (IVR) scripts for streamlined self-service, and call routing to connect customers with the right agents.</li><li>Minimizes dropped calls and ensures crystal-clear communication.</li></ul><figure style="max-width:533px" data-rt-max-width="533px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/6762cfbc311e646bd463c437_AD_4nXdsS_Ph61vaQ7QR6VbfT6nbNwrY2hwDCTARDuRvotgYtnI_QLEHEKB_zQ0VlAM9UIRp04Pvdl_mn4FMYt-gkrryFXJp9dyxthQmP1H2iySvg4LyD1_JWNP16hhrpovwoJImiTXtng.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="An image showing features of Plivo’s Voice API" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.plivo.com/voice/">Image source</a></figcaption></figure><p>If your contact center manages a high volume of customer inquiries, use a reliable VoIP system to ensure consistent communication and reduce call drops. Apply <a href="https://www.plivo.com/resources/best-practices-for-voice-calling-with-plivo-voice-api/">best practices</a> to improve implementation and achieve better communication outcomes.</p><h2>2.CRM integration</h2><p>A Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system houses detailed information about previous customer interactions, their preferences, purchase history, and more. When you integrate this rich data into your contact center, agents get a comprehensive view of each customer to provide highly personalized assistance.</p><p>With this data readily available, agents can quickly identify previous issues or anticipate future needs, leading to faster resolutions and a better overall customer experience.</p><h2>3.Call recording</h2><p>You can capture customer interactions with call recording tools and gain valuable insights from them. For example, if customers frequently complain about a specific product, the tool captures this feedback. You can then use this data to improve the product’s quality, determine whether the issue is with marketing or product design, and train your staff accordingly.</p><p>Moreover, you don’t need to worry about customer data privacy as these tools encrypt the recordings.</p><p>To further improve customer experience, opt for an automated call transcription feature tool. <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/why-transcribe-calls/">Transcribing calls</a> improves customer insights with more accurate analysis, helping you identify trends and address issues more effectively.</p><p>Analyze call recordings alongside contact center performance metrics and agent productivity tools to pinpoint areas for improving agent training or call-handling strategies.</p><h2>4.Call queuing</h2><p>Customers dislike waiting, however, call queuing systems make the experience more manageable. They inform customers of their expected wait times, play custom messages, and offer callback options. It’s one of the most effective contact center technology trends, especially when managing a surge of customer calls.</p><p>Such transparency reduces caller frustration and improves agent productivity. Some <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/learn/contact-center/cloud-contact-center/">cloud-based contact centers</a> also have automated callbacks, allowing customers to hang up without losing their spot in the queue.</p><p>You can even customize queues with maximum wait times and personalized messages or music with providers like Plivo. This ensures that customers are connected with the right agents at all times.</p><p>The system routes works based on:</p><ul><li><strong>Skill-based routing: </strong>Routes callers to agents with particular skills or training based on their needs</li><li><strong>Time-of-day routing: </strong>Routes calls to agents depending on their time zone.</li><li><strong>Priority-based routine</strong>: Routes callers with urgent issues to the top of the queue.</li></ul><p>ACD eliminates the need for manual call forwarding or transferring, reducing wait times and improving overall customer experience.</p><h2>7.Chatbots</h2><p>The adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) in contact centers is on the rise, and AI-powered customer service chatbots are leading the charge. This leads to repetitive questions, frustrated customers, and a negative impact on the experience.</p><h2>9.Call analytics</h2><p>Real-time and historical call analytics provide insights into performance metrics like call quality, resolution times, and call drops.
