<h2>FAQs</h2>
<h3>1. Which insurance calls should an AI voice agent handle vs. avoid?</h3>
<p>Automate low-risk, high-volume tasks like identity verification, claim status, FNOL intake, renewals, reminders and appointment booking. Escalate coverage disputes, liability decisions, injuries, cancellations, and underwriting exceptions to licensed staff.</p>
<h3>2. How do outbound compliance and consent actually work?</h3>
<p>Outbound flows must check consent records, scrub federal and state DNC lists, enforce time-of-day rules and provide instant opt-out. Opt-outs should be written back to your CRM/AMS in real time so future calls are blocked automatically.</p>
<h3>3. What data should an FNOL voice agent collect to avoid rework?</h3>
<p>Minimum set includes policy number, insured name, contact details, date/time of loss, location, cause and callback preference. For auto, it should include vehicle/VIN, drivable status, injuries, police report availability. Always confirm with a read-back before creating the claim.</p>
<h3>4. How do warm transfers to adjusters work without frustrating callers?</h3>
<p>The agent authenticates the caller, captures intent, summarizes the conversation and should tag key fields. On transfer, the adjuster receives the summary and structured data so they start informed so no repetition is required.</p>
<h3>5. What does a realistic pilot timeline look like?</h3>
<p>Week 1: provide provision numbers, connect telephony, configure prompts, webhooks and test live audio streaming. Then test internally. Week 2: Try limited live traffic (e.g., claim status), daily transcript reviews, conduct tuning for latency and accuracy, then expand hours once quality stabilises.</p>
