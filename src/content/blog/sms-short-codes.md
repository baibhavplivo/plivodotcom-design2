<p>SMS marketing has an open rate of <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/5-shocking-sms-marketing-statistics-show-why-2023-year-sms-local">over 90%, compared to just 20%- 30% for email</a>. However, managing a text message marketing campaign with a standard 10-digit phone number can be cumbersome.&nbsp;</p><p>Fortunately, SMS short codes are a great alternative. This article explains why SMS short codes are your secret weapon for high-volume text message marketing. We'll explain what they are, why short codes are better than regular numbers, and how to leverage them to boost audience engagement.</p><h2>What is a short code?</h2><p>Short codes, also known as short numbers, are brief digit sequences, typically five or six digits long. They enable businesses to send and receive text messages at scale. Short codes are easy to remember and quick to type in, improving the user experience.</p><p>{{cta-style-1}}</p><p>Let's delve into the different types of short codes and how they function.</p><h3>Types of SMS short codes</h3><p>There are two types of short codes used in SMS marketing and communications.&nbsp;</p><h4>Shared short codes</h4><ul><li>Shared short codes are like public phone numbers. Multiple businesses can use the same short code.</li><li>They are the most affordable option.</li><li>However, you have no control over who else uses the code, which can confuse customers.</li><li>You might also miss out on replies sent to the shared code by other companies.</li></ul><p>Note: Shared short codes are phased out in countries like the USA and Canada.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h4>Dedicated short codes (leased short codes)</h4><ul><li>Dedicated short codes are exclusive to your business.</li><li>You have complete control over how the code is used.</li><li>They ensure customers only receive your messages and the replies go directly to your system.</li><li>Dedicated codes come at a higher cost than shared codes.</li></ul><p>Whichever type you decide to use, all short codes can offer specific benefits to your business.&nbsp;</p><h2>What are the benefits of short codes for businesses?</h2><p>Short codes offer several advantages over long codes or email.&nbsp;</p><h3>1) Improved deliverability</h3><p>One of the foremost challenges in marketing is ensuring your messages reach the intended audience. Emails can be blocked by spam filters and social media ads get lost in viewers’ feeds. Short codes, however, have high delivery rates. People are accustomed to checking their text messages frequently, and short codes stand out from spammy marketing tactics.</p><h3>2) Enhanced brand recall</h3><p>Short codes are far easier to remember than standard ten-digit phone numbers. This memorability translates to better engagement and campaign participation. Vanity short codes take recall a step further. These spellable codes align with your brand name or campaign (e.g., "4Alerts" for a notification service). Vanity codes enhance brand recognition but may come with an additional cost.</p><h3>3) High throughput and performance</h3><p>Designed to handle large volumes of messages efficiently, short codes are a feasible solution for high-traffic campaigns like contests, discount alerts, or appointment reminders. They allow you to send messages to a large audience quickly and reliably, ensuring smooth communication and timely delivery.</p><h3>4) Cost-effectiveness (for shared short codes)</h3><p>Shared short codes offer an excellent solution for businesses looking to optimize their SMS marketing campaigns without breaking the bank. Multiple companies can share the same short code, reducing overhead costs.</p><p>Here’s a quick comparison of pricing in the United States for the different types of short codes.&nbsp;</p><figure><div><img src="/images/blog/66979a8991303807cb3c75c3_AD_4nXcIk8eBzbz41ZtdjCz1biL2_EHWC0yfjfdCDVe47S-8eK2dYNR-6751c831.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.plivo.com/sms/shortcode/">Source</a>&nbsp;</figcaption></figure><p>We recommend shared short codes for businesses exploring SMS marketing on limited budgets. However, a shared code means less control over branding than a dedicated code. Also, they can be used to send messages only in countries where they are provisioned. So, if your customers are not located in the US, do not get a US short code.</p><h2>How do businesses use short codes?</h2><p>Short codes aren't a one-size-fits-all solution. This section helps you learn how short codes are used across different industries for different purposes.&nbsp;</p><h3>Short codes in financial services and banking&nbsp;</h3><p>Banking and financial services rely on short codes to implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for their customers. MFA adds a layer of security to protect sensitive information and prevents unauthorized account access.&nbsp;</p><p>For example, the bank sends a text message with a unique code to the customer’s phone. The customer must enter the code to make the transaction or access their accounts online.</p><h3>Short codes in retail/e-commerce&nbsp;</h3><p>Short codes can send a high volume of text messages over a short period of time. This capability makes them useful for retail and e-commerce marketing campaigns, such as sweepstakes or giveaways, as well as flash sales, discounts, and promos. Short codes can also be used in event-based promotions.&nbsp;</p><h3>Short codes in healthcare&nbsp;</h3><p>SMS short codes support two-way communication, allowing patients to respond to messages such as booking reminders. Healthcare providers and organizations can send post-visit surveys to understand patient feedback and satisfaction, regular checkup reminders, and vaccine availability.&nbsp;</p><h2>How do you get short codes for business text messaging?</h2><p>Here’s how to secure a short code for your business with Plivo:&nbsp;</p><ol start=""><li>‍<strong>Read the guidelines:</strong> Before applying, ensure your campaign meets the guidelines for <a href="https://support.plivo.com/hc/en-us/articles/21634897946393-US-Short-Code-Application-Guidelines">the US</a> or <a href="https://support.plivo.com/hc/en-us/articles/21635157943705-Canada-Short-Code-Application-Guidelines">Canada</a>.<strong>‍</strong></li><li><strong>Download the application:</strong> Get the <a href="https://www.plivo.com/assets/dist/Plivo_US_short_code_application.docx/">application form for your country</a> and fill it out carefully.<strong>‍</strong></li><li><strong>Submit your application:</strong> Apply with Plivo's support team.
