<h2>FAQs</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>What’s the difference between a dialer, conversation intelligence, and an AI voice agent?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>A dialer places calls and manages pacing. Conversation intelligence analyzes calls to coach or summarize. An AI voice agent does the talking in real time, handles objections, and books next steps, then passes to a human when needed.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>How do I keep my calls from getting flagged as spam?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Use verified caller IDs, maintain clean number pools, honor opt‑outs quickly, and monitor reputation signals with your provider. Keep your opening clear about who you are and why you’re calling.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>What latency target should I design for?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Treat mouth‑to‑ear delay as a core KPI. Keep the audio path lean, test under load, and choose components that minimize round‑trip delay so interruptions feel natural.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Can an AI agent legally call prospects in the U.S.?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Yes, with conditions. Ensure proper consent where required, follow quiet hours, provide recording disclosures, and make opt‑out easy. Document your policies and audit regularly.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>How should handoffs to humans work?</strong></li>
</ul>

<p>Trigger a warm transfer when intent is high or the conversation reaches a defined complexity. If no one’s available, book a time and send a summary to the owner so follow‑up is immediate.</p>
