<p>The <a href="https://www.ajmc.com/view/wait-times-patient-satisfaction-scores-and-the-perception-of-care">American Journal of Managed Care</a> states that clinic wait times affect overall patient satisfaction and influence perceptions of healthcare providers. In 2023 alone, over <a href="https://www.hipaajournal.com/healthcare-data-breach-statistics/">382 million</a> health records were compromised — 1.2 times the U.S. population.</p><p>For healthcare providers, the message is clear: effective communication and tight compliance are key. Communication gaps can result in missed appointments, no-shows, data breaches, and delayed patient care.</p><p>That’s where healthcare interactive voice response (IVR) systems come in. They streamline patient workflows and help you focus solely on providing quality patient care.</p><p>But how do you know if your facility will benefit from IVR?</p><p>In this blog post, we’ll cover everything you need to know about healthcare IVR and its benefits so that you can discern whether it’s the right fit for your facility.</p><h2>What is IVR in healthcare?</h2><p>IVR in healthcare automates communication between providers and patients. Through an IVR, patients can navigate options, schedule appointments, and even get answers to common questions, without needing to talk to a live agent.</p><p>Simply put, an IVR for hospitals is a virtual receptionist that works 24/7 without getting overwhelmed.</p><p>What may happen when a healthcare facility doesn’t use an IVR system? Well, consider these:</p><ul><li>Legal penalties and data breaches can cost your facility millions annually.</li><li>Your staff may get overwhelmed by calls, becoming more likely to make mistakes. This also leads to frustrated patients and increased liability.</li><li>Repetitive, time-consuming tasks like scheduling appointments and sending patient reminders drain morale, causing high turnover rates and additional hiring costs.</li><li>Long wait times and poor communication drive patients away, damaging your facility’s reputation and revenue.</li></ul><p>Healthcare IVR systems simplify workflows, reduce such errors, and <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/ivr-customer-experience/">improve patient experience</a> by streamlining communication.</p><h2>Benefits of using IVR in Healthcare</h2><p>All patient and provider workflows can work without an IVR, but they significantly become more efficient with one in place. Plus, IVR benefits in healthcare extend beyond patients, they’re just as valuable for healthcare providers.</p><p>Let’s understand the benefits of healthcare IVR solutions for your facility.</p><h3>For patients</h3><h4>24/7 accessibility to information and services</h4><p>The last thing any healthcare facility wants is for patients to feel stranded during emergencies or delayed care. IVR ensures that doesn’t happen.&nbsp;</p><p>With automated, pre-recorded responses, patients can get their queries resolved and schedule appointments, even outside facility hours.</p><p>Patients don't need to wait long to connect to a nurse or admin staff for simple, non-urgent tasks. For instance, they can access critical information like clinic hours, lab results, or prescription refills, whenever they need it.</p><p>When patients feel heard, it automatically decreases the number of no-shows or patients going to other, more accessible facilities.</p><h4>Faster appointment booking and issue resolution</h4><p>When patients are able to book appointments at their convenience without waiting for clinic hours, their trust in the healthcare provider increases. In addition, it reduces frustration from limited clinic hours or long hold times on the phone, leading to higher satisfaction.</p><p>If the requested time slot isn't available or the patient needs to cancel their appointment, the IVR can offer alternatives and cancel the appointment by prompting patients to enter the reason.</p><p>The added benefit is that it frees up your administrative staff to focus on patient care.</p><h4>Improved privacy during sensitive communications</h4><p>Patients can securely input sensitive information like their date of birth, medical conditions, insurance details, etc. with voice prompts. It makes patients feel safer than sharing such information in an environment with a staff with the possibility of someone overhearing it or misusing it.</p><p>For added protection, IVR systems can also authenticate callers with secure methods like PINs, passwords, or date of birth before granting access to personal health information (PHI) so that only authorized individuals can access sensitive data.</p><p>Providers like <a href="https://www.plivo.com/use-case/healthcare-api/">Plivo</a> go a step further by offering compliance with national and global privacy and security regulations including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), the second of three System and Organization Controls (SOC 2), Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS), and General Data Protection Regulation (EU) (GDPR). This reassures patients their data is safe with your facility.</p><h3>For providers</h3><h4>Increased security and compliance</h4><p>Along with safeguarding patient trust, IVR systems also shield healthcare facilities from costly lawsuits and fines due to data breaches.</p><p>HIPAA violations can cost up to <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK500019/#:~:text=Civil%20violations&amp;text=For%20a%20HIPAA%20violation%20due,or%20$1.5%20million%20per%20violation.">$50,000</a> per incident, with a maximum annual penalty of $1.5 million for repeated violations. Hence, having HIPAA-compliant IVR systems becomes key. They securely handle patient information, whether collecting payment details or routing sensitive calls, and minimize the risk of data breaches.</p><p>Moreover, during IVR feedback collection, patients can use harsh language to express their frustration. However, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/voice/automatic-speech-recognition/">Plivo’s automatic speech recognition</a> (ASR) feature uses a profanity filter to ensure the transcription excludes offensive terms. This helps maintain a professional and neutral tone in records and protects your staff from unnecessary negativity.</p><h4>Reduced errors in patient interactions</h4><p>More than <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499956/">200,000 annual patient deaths</a> in the United States occur due to preventable medical errors. While these include severe surgical mistakes, they also often stem from errors like miscommunication, double booking, or delays in follow-up care.</p><p>With automated patient communication, IVR eliminates such issues.</p><p>It takes care of missed or delayed follow-ups with specialists, incorrect patient records due to manual errors, overlooked warning signs due to gaps in follow-up care, etc. Automating such critical communication touchpoints reduces recurring errors and medical errors.</p><h4>Reduced staff workload and operational costs</h4><p>No-shows hit where it hurts: revenue. While cancellation fees might offset some of the losses, they don’t eliminate the financial strain on your facility.</p><p>IVR appointment booking, patient reminders via IVR, and confirmations can reduce no-show rates by <a href="https://emitrr.com/blog/how-to-reduce-no-show-rates-in-outpatient-clinics/">up to 30%</a> in outpatient clinics.</p><p>For busy clinics, this means significant cost savings annually. Plus, fewer calls about basic tasks like appointment scheduling or follow-up reminders mean less workload for staff, reducing burnout and improving productivity.</p><h4>Improved patient satisfaction scores</h4><p>The <a href="https://www.cms.gov/data-research/research/consumer-assessment-healthcare-providers-systems/hospital-cahps-hcahps">Center for Medicare and Medicaid Services</a> (CMS) provides a standardized way to access the Hospital Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems (HCAHPS) score. It helps hospitals and governing bodies evaluate patient satisfaction through quantitative measurement.</p><h2>How do IVR systems help?</h2><p>Well, they improve these scores by automating patient satisfaction surveys. Patients can easily share feedback about their experience, from communication and staff responsiveness to wait times. This data helps hospitals or clinics identify and address problem areas.</p><p>As per the program, facilities with higher HCAHPS scores earn higher reimbursements. So happier patients can directly boost your facility’s bottom line.</p><h2>Real-life Use Cases of IVR in Healthcare</h2><p>From scheduling appointments to getting patients’ prescriptions, IVR for hospitals can make many workflows efficient. Doing so in person can be inefficient, with nurses potentially asking inconsistent questions or overlooking crucial information, increasing the risk of errors.</p><p>IVR systems support preliminary health assessments with voice or keypad prompts to collect necessary information from patients before speaking to their healthcare provider. This saves time when dealing with urgent cases.</p><p>For instance, if you use <a href="https://www.plivo.com/ai-voice-agents/">Plivo-powered AI voice agents</a>, it can identify high-priority cases immediately based on patient responses. The system can route the call to a nurse or schedule an immediate appointment.</p><h3>Multilingual support</h3><p>With <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/6931059_Language_Barriers_to_Health_Care_in_the_United_States">over 49.6 million people</a> in the U.S. speaking a language other than English, language barriers quickly become a hindrance to providing quality care.</p><p>To make healthcare more accessible, healthcare providers use IVR systems with multilingual support. When calling a provider, patients can select their language using a keypad or voice command. This reduces miscommunication and missed appointments due to patients being unable to navigate the system in their preferred language.</p><h3>Appointment booking</h3><p>It now takes an <a href="https://www.wsha.org/articles/new-survey-physician-appointment-wait-times-getting-longer/">average of 26 days</a> to schedule a new patient physician appointment in 15 of the largest cities in the United States. Naturally, a busy clinic receiving over 100 appointment requests a day will struggle to manage scheduling.</p><p>You can implement an appointment scheduling <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/ivr-script-examples/">IVR script</a> to take care of the scheduling workflow:</p><ul><li>The IVR greets the caller and asks them to select a service or department.</li><li>The patient inputs their preferred date and time (via text or speaking it aloud).</li><li>The system checks the clinic's scheduling system in real-time and confirms the slot, or offers alternative slots.</li><li>Finally, the IVR sends confirmation through the patient's provided contact details.</li></ul><h3>Patient medication reminders and lab results</h3><p>Typically, nurses or administrative staff manually sift through patients' medical files to identify who needs medication reminders and who has pending lab results. They spend hours attempting to reach patients and document the responses received.</p><p>However, an IVR menu automatically identifies patients requiring medication and those with new lab results. It also places calls at pre-scheduled times and confirms whether patients have taken medications or if they have any issues to report.</p><p>Healthcare providers now only have to review the flagged cases that require urgent attention.</p><p>It’s also convenient to automate post-care calls and messages or direct incoming callers to the provider via IVR menus.</p><p><strong><em>Pro Tip:</em></strong><em> Opt for an IVR provider that supports both dual-tone multi-frequency (DTMF) and voice input for greater accessibility and ease of use.</em></p><h3>Prescription refill requests</h3><p>Patients can call anytime for prescription refill requests. Here’s what the process would look like:</p><ul><li>Patients call the clinic or pharmacy and input their prescription details via keypad or voice.</li><li>The system automatically routes the request to the appropriate pharmacy or alerts the doctor for approval.</li><li>Once approved, the patient receives a notification via text or email with the refill status and pick-up details.</li></ul><h3>Billing and payment-related inquiries</h3><p>Map out the flow focusing on common billing-related inquiries and admin tasks like:</p><ul><li>Checking account balance</li><li>Making a payment</li><li>Insurance-related questions or coverage verification</li><li>Check claim status</li><li>Get financial assistance</li><li>Receive personalized support in cases of disputes</li></ul><p>If the query requires further assistance, like disputing a charge, the IVR routes the call to the billing department.
