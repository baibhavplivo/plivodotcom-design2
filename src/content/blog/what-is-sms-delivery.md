<p>Effective SMS communication hinges on one key factor—ensuring messages are successfully delivered.&nbsp;</p><p>However, not every text message reaches its destination.&nbsp;</p><p>Various technical and regulatory factors can interfere with SMS transmission, preventing messages from being received.</p><p>For instance, in 2023, <a href="https://www.statista.com/statistics/1387722/e-commerce-sms-marketing-benchmarks-worldwide/">e-commerce brands worldwide sent 57.9% more SMS campaigns</a> than the previous year, yet despite a solid 7.6% click rate and a modest 0.13% conversion rate, the overall increase in orders was only 9.4%.&nbsp;</p><p>This underscores the impact that undelivered messages can have on campaign success. To tackle this, understanding the SMS delivery process helps businesses identify potential delivery roadblocks and refine their messaging strategy.&nbsp;</p><p>In this article, we’ll explore how SMS delivery works, the different stages of message transmission, common reasons for failed deliveries, and strategies to maximize SMS reach and effectiveness.</p><h2><strong>How does SMS delivery work?</strong></h2><p>SMS delivery is the process of transmitting text messages from a sender to a recipient's mobile device. Understanding the distinctions between different stages of SMS delivery can help businesses optimize their communication strategies.&nbsp;</p><p>‍</p><p>Here’s a breakdown of the key concepts and the SMS delivery process:</p><table><tbody><tr>   <td><strong>Status</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td><strong>Description</strong><p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Sent messages</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Indicates that the SMS has left the sender’s platform and is on its way through the carrier network.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Delivered messages</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Confirms that the SMS has successfully reached the recipient’s device.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Failed messages</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>These are messages that could not be delivered to the recipient's device. Common reasons include an invalid number, network issues, or a blocked recipient.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Filtered messages</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>These messages are stopped by carriers or network providers before reaching the recipient. This usually happens if the content of the message triggers spam filters or if the sender's number is deemed suspicious.<p></p>   </td>  </tr></tbody></table><p>‍</p><h3><strong>SMS delivery process</strong></h3><p>Knowing how SMS is delivered can optimize your SMS campaigns. Here's a brief overview of how a message travels from initiation to delivery or potential blockage:</p><ol><li><strong>Message sent</strong>: The sender’s platform creates and sends the SMS.</li><li><strong>Message routed:</strong> The SMS gateway receives the message and directs it to the appropriate mobile carrier. This step involves passing through various network gateways, and possibly different carriers if the sender and recipient are on different networks.<strong>‍</strong></li><li><strong>Carrier processing:</strong></li></ol><p> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;👉 The carrier checks network availability and the recipient's status to determine if it can deliver the message.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;👉 If approved, the carrier delivers the message to the recipient’s device.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;👉 If flagged, the carrier filters or blocks the message, preventing delivery.</p><h2><strong>Key factors that<em> </em>impact SMS deliverability</strong></h2><p>Understanding what affects SMS deliverability is crucial for successful messaging. Here are the key factors that impact SMS deliverability:</p><h3><strong>1. Carrier filtering</strong></h3><p>Most mobile carriers actively prevent spam by filtering messages that show suspicious patterns. These filters analyze the sender's behavior, such as message frequency and the consistency of volume over time.</p><p>For example, a sudden spike in message volume from a new number may trigger carrier filters to block those messages, interpreting them as potential spam.</p><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Establish a consistent sending pattern and stick to it. Engage with carriers to understand better and align with their filtering criteria.<p></p>   </td></tr></tbody></table><p>‍</p><h3><strong>2. Message content</strong></h3><p>The actual text of your messages can trigger spam filters if they contain certain keywords or phrases typically associated with spam. Messages that include phrases like "You've won!" or "Claim your prize now!" are more likely to be flagged as spam.</p><p>Additionally, messages that include shady links or that do not comply with formatting rules (like including non-standard characters) are more likely to be filtered.</p><p>‍</p><h3><strong>3. Sender reputation</strong></h3><p>Carriers maintain reputation scores for senders to ensure that only legitimate messages are delivered. A poor reputation can result from high numbers of complaints or blocks from users.</p><p>Numbers recognized as 'trusted' by carriers—such as shortcodes, long codes, and toll-free numbers that are properly registered—face fewer hurdles.&nbsp;</p><p>For example<strong>,</strong> a business repeatedly flagged by users for unwanted messages may find all its communications being blocked, regardless of content.</p><div data-rt-embed-type="true"></div><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Use language that is straightforward and aligned with your brand voice. Regularly update your messaging templates to avoid phrases that are known to trigger spam filters.<p></p>   </td></tr></tbody></table><h3><strong><br>4. Network issues</strong></h3><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Use verified numbers like shortcodes, which are 5-6 digit numbers, are known for high throughput, and are closely monitored for compliance, making them less likely to be blocked. Monitor feedback and adjust practices to reduce complaints and blocks.<p></p>   </td></tr></tbody></table><p>‍</p><h3><strong>4. Network issues</strong></h3><p>Factors such as network reliability during high traffic periods or technical failures can hinder SMS delivery success beyond content and reputation.&nbsp;</p><p>Similarly, if the recipient's phone is off, out of range, or has inbox storage issues, these problems must be resolved before the message can be delivered.</p><p>For example, during natural disasters, networks may become congested, leading to higher rates of undelivered messages.</p><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Schedule critical communications for off-peak times and monitor carrier network status reports to plan around potential disruptions.<p></p>   </td></tr></tbody></table><p>‍</p><h3><strong>5. Regulatory compliance</strong></h3><p>Regulations around SMS messaging vary significantly by region and country with local and international laws. Most regions have Do Not Disturb (DND) lists and strict opt-in and opt-out rules that must be adhered to.</p><p>Regulations like the <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">GDPR</a> in Europe and the <a href="https://www.fdic.gov/regulations/compliance/manual/8/viii-5.1.pdf">TCPA</a> in the U.S. govern how businesses can collect, store, and use mobile numbers. Non-compliance can not only lead to blocked messages but also hefty fines.</p><p><strong>For example,</strong> a U.S. company must have explicit consent from users before sending them marketing SMS and must provide an easy way to opt out of every message.</p><table><tbody><tr><td><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Maintain detailed records of consent for each contact. Regularly review and update compliance measures to keep pace with legislative changes.<p></p>   </td></tr></tbody></table><p>Understanding and addressing these factors will help businesses maintain high deliverability rates for their SMS campaigns, ensuring that messages reach their intended recipients efficiently and legally.</p><h2><strong>Common SMS delivery failure reasons &amp; solutions</strong></h2><table><tbody><tr>   <td><strong>Factor</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td><strong>Solution</strong><p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Carrier filtering</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Establish consistent sending patterns; engage with carriers to align with filtering criteria.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Message content</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Use clear language; avoid spam-trigger words; regularly update templates.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Sender reputation</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Use verified numbers like shortcodes; monitor feedback and adjust practices.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Network issues</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Schedule messages during off-peak times; monitor network status.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Regulatory compliance</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Obtain explicit consent; provide clear opt-out options; maintain detailed consent records.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>DND &amp; opt-out lists</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Regularly update contact lists to remove opted-out or DND numbers.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Invalid phone numbers</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Verify and validate phone numbers before sending; ensure correct formatting; keep contact lists updated.<p></p>   </td>  </tr></tbody></table><p><strong>Proven best practices to maximize SMS delivery rates</strong></p><p>Ensuring high SMS delivery rates is critical for effective communication. Here are proven best practices to maximize SMS deliverability.</p><h3><strong>1. Keep a clean contact list</strong></h3><p>Invalid or outdated numbers can lead to high bounce rates and lower overall deliverability.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Example:</strong></p><p>An online fashion store sends a flash sale SMS to 50,000 contacts but notices a 20% bounce rate. After auditing their contact list, they found that many numbers were outdated or mistyped. They introduce an SMS verification step at checkout, reducing their bounce rate and ensuring messages reach real customers.</p><p>Regularly clean your contact list by:</p><ul><li>Removing inactive or invalid numbers</li><li>Using opt-in methods to ensure recipients want to receive your messages</li><li>Updating contact information periodically to avoid sending messages to non-existent numbers</li></ul><h3><strong>2. Optimize message formatting &amp; structure</strong></h3><p>Poorly formatted SMS messages can be blocked by carriers or ignored by recipients. To improve deliverability, keep messages short, avoid excessive special characters, and make the CTA clear.</p><p><strong>Example:<br>Poor formatting:</strong></p><blockquote><strong>‍<br></strong>"🔥 BIG SALE!! Click NOW 👉 bit.ly/abcd to get 50% OFF!! Limited Time! 🚀"</blockquote><p><strong>Better formatting:</strong></p><p><strong>‍<br></strong>"Hey [Name], our biggest sale of the season is live! Get 50% off sitewide. Shop now: www.brand.com"</p><p>By using a branded link instead of a generic shortener, the e-commerce brand avoids carrier spam filters and increases trust.</p><h3><strong>3. Timing and frequency</strong></h3><p>The timing and frequency of your messages can affect both user engagement and deliverability.&nbsp;</p><p>Schedule SMS to arrive during optimal times based on customer behavior patterns and avoid sending too many messages in a short period, which can lead to unsubscribes or spam complaints.</p><p><strong>Example:</strong></p><p>An electronics store adjusts their cart abandonment SMS reminders from 30 minutes to 4 hours after customers leave the site. After analyzing the results, this timing adjustment significantly boosts recovery rates, as customers are more responsive later</p><h3><strong>4. Customer segmentation</strong></h3><p>Not all recipients should receive the same message. Segmenting your audience allows for personalized and relevant communication. Consider:</p><ul><li>Segment contacts by demographics, purchase history, or engagement</li><li>Send targeted messages based on preferences and past interactions</li><li>Use dynamic content for personalized, high-response messages</li></ul><p>Generic messages don’t work for e-commerce brands. Segmenting customers ensures that messages are relevant, leading to higher open and conversion rates.</p><p><strong>Example:<br></strong>A skincare brand segments its customers into three groups:</p><ul><li>New buyers receive a welcome SMS with a 10% discount on their next purchase</li><li>Frequent shoppers get exclusive early access to sales</li><li>Inactive customers receive a “We miss you” message with a special offer</li></ul><h3><strong>5. Ensure compliance with local &amp; global SMS laws</strong></h3><p>Adhering to SMS regulations is non-negotiable for legal operations and maintaining customer trust.</p><p>Familiarize yourself with local and international laws such as <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">GDPR</a> in Europe or <a href="https://gdpr-info.eu/">TCPA</a> in the U.S. Always get consent before sending SMS, provide clear opt-out options, and maintain records of these consents.</p><h3><strong>6. Monitor &amp; analyze delivery reports</strong></h3><p>Continuous monitoring and analysis of your SMS campaigns’ performance help in understanding delivery success rates and identifying areas for improvement.</p><p>Key metrics to monitor include:</p><ul><li>Delivery rates to detect potential carrier or formatting issues</li><li>Failure reports to understand common reasons for non-delivery</li><li>Response times to measure engagement and campaign effectiveness</li></ul><p><strong>Example:</strong></p><p>An online pet supply store finds that messages to specific regions are blocked, leading to high undelivery rates. After analyzing reports and adjusting sender IDs with their SMS provider, they significantly improve deliverability in those areas.</p><p>By implementing these best practices, businesses can ensure
