<p>Variety Research’s <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">The State of SMS Marketing in 2023 report</a> reveals that nearly 45% of consumers prefer receiving promotional messages from brands. Combine that with the fact that people check their phones <a href="https://fortune.com/well/2023/07/19/how-to-cut-back-screen-time/">144 times a day</a>, and SMS automatically becomes a marketing channel you cannot overlook.</p><p>Developed as one of the most direct and unassuming communication channels, SMS helps you deliver messages that reach your customers where they’re already spending time—right on their phones.</p><p>But that’s only <em>one</em> of the advantages of <a href="https://www.plivo.com/resources/sms-marketing-guide/">SMS marketing</a>.&nbsp;</p><p>What else can it do for your business? How can you get the maximum ROI out of your SMS campaigns? And how can you integrate SMS with your other marketing channels for a truly connected customer experience?</p><p>In this guide, we will go over the advantages of SMS marketing. Let’s begin!</p><h2>Defining the SMS</h2><p>SMS stands for short message service. It is a type of text message sent from one mobile device to another over any cellular network.&nbsp;</p><p>These messages are generally limited to 160 characters and contain only text—it doesn’t support images, videos, or other media.&nbsp;</p><p>However, if you want to add images, videos, or audio—or go beyond the 160-character limit—your message will turn into a multimedia messaging service (MMS).&nbsp;</p><p>Note that you require an internet connection to send and receive MMS.&nbsp;</p><h2>6 important benefits of SMS marketing</h2><p>Plus, according to <a href="https://www.salesforce.com/ap/resources/research-reports/state-of-the-connected-customer/">Salesforce’s State of the Connected Consumer Report</a>, 66% of customers prefer to be contacted through text messages instead of other channels. Also, the latest <a href="https://simpletexting.com/blog/2024-texting-and-sms-marketing-statistics/#:~:text=45%25%20more%20businesses%20are%20using%20SMS%20marketing%20now%20compared%20to%20this%20time%20two%20years%20ago.">SimpleTexting survey</a> suggests that 45% more businesses are using SMS marketing now compared to this time in 2022.&nbsp;</p><p>So, what is driving this surge?&nbsp;</p><p>Let’s explore a few core advantages that are convincing marketers to go for SMS instead of other marketing channels:</p><h3>1. SMS marketing is trustworthy and personal</h3><p>As noted above, SMS messages go straight to your customer's phone. This direct line makes them feel more personal than emails or calls. People often check texts immediately, sometimes reading them from the notification bar.</p><p>In fact, according to the <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">Validity report from 2023</a>, 19% of consumers prefer getting messages via SMS, which is higher than social media (16%), app notifications (6%), and direct mail (6%).</p><figure style="max-width:449px" data-rt-max-width="449px"><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a3dfbb8b9736b812ef7_6729c4d99be5dc257066bc47_AD_4nXe2-iWgz4DavFz81EngqjpB9u-022172c8.png" alt="" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">Source</a></figcaption></figure><p>Another reason SMS is popular is that—unlike long, drawn-out emails, texts are short, to the point, and easily skimmable.&nbsp;</p><h3>2. It's particularly effective for time-sensitive communications and can complement other marketing efforts.&nbsp;</p><p>Moreover, during high-volume sales periods like Black Friday, <a href="https://www.enterpriseappstoday.com/stats/black-friday-statistics.html">SMS marketing can deliver up to a 2000% ROI</a>, showcasing its effectiveness in driving revenue.</p><p>Now, let us review how SMS marketing stacks up against other marketing channels based on the latest data:&nbsp;</p><h4>SMS vs. email marketing</h4><ul><li><strong>ROI</strong>: SMS marketing generates<a href="https://porchgroupmedia.com/blog/100-compelling-email-statistics-to-inform-your-strategy-in-2023/#:~:text=The%20average%20expected%20ROI%20is,any%20other%20digital%20marketing%20channel."> $71 in revenue for every $1 spent</a>, while email marketing returns $42 for every $1 invested. SMS marketing is more impactful</h3><p>The <a href="https://www.wyzowl.com/human-attention-span/">human attention span is notoriously shrinking</a>. In this context, SMS marketing can grab attention quickly through short, impactful messages. Compared to email marketing, where lengthy messages can get buried or cold calling, the response to which can often depend on the receiver’s mood, SMS marketing is a non-intrusive way to deliver real-time, high-impact information on the fly.</p><p>As noted above, SMS has a staggering open rate—2.7x more than the <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/average-email-open-rate-benchmark">37.3% average open rate for email marketing</a> and 19.6x the <a href="https://www.cognism.com/blog/cold-calling-success-rates#:~:text=our%20findings%20%F0%9F%91%87-,What%20is%20the%20success%20rate%20of%20cold%20calling%3F,implemented%20into%20your%20sales%20strategy.">4.8% average response rate for cold calls</a>.&nbsp;</p><p>This means your messages are almost guaranteed to be seen when delivered via SMS.</p><p>This high engagement is because of the to-the-point, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/top-sms-marketing-apps-for-shopify">personal nature of SMS marketing</a> and the fact that readers are not distracted away from the CTA.&nbsp;</p><h3>4. SMS marketing has low competition</h3><p>Another vital aspect is competition—only <a href="https://textus.com/playbook/state-of-sms-engagement-2024/">60.3% of companies</a> use SMS marketing. In contrast, <a href="https://snov.io/blog/email-marketing-statistics/">87% of marketers use email marketing</a> to distribute content, and <a href="https://snov.io/blog/email-marketing-statistics/">31% use email newsletters</a> to nurture leads.&nbsp;</p><p>This lower level of competition presents a distinct advantage, allowing businesses to stand out and deliver impactful messages.</p><figure style="max-width:822px" data-rt-max-width="822px"><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a3dfbb8b9736b812efe_6729c531e783307e21810fb8_AD_4nXcyXXyOOIjnDrMq3QyGlWAq1g-109b73d3.png" alt="" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.salesforce.com/ap/resources/research-reports/state-of-the-connected-customer/">Source</a></figcaption></figure><h3>5. SMS marketing can be easily combined with other channels</h3><p>Your customers can use any channel at any time. So, engaging them via multiple channels and a consistent brand experience increases the chances of conversion.&nbsp;</p><p>You can use both SMS and email marketing simultaneously to <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/omnichannel-best-practices">improve customer experience</a> by:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Using email to <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/collect-phone-numbers-for-sms-marketing">collect phone numbers for SMS marketing</a> campaigns</li><li>Recovering lost sales with abandoned cart emails combined with text messages</li><li>Sending personalized recommendations via email and following up with an SMS reminder</li><li>Creating a multi-touchpoint experience where you use SMS to drive attention to your email campaign</li><li>Using SMS to announce flash sales while your emails and social media posts get into the details of the various deals being offered</li></ul><p>Some marketers also sync their company’s calendars with SMS to send appointment and event reminders, such as for webinars or service appointments, to reduce no-shows and increase engagement.&nbsp;</p><h3>6. No changing algorithms to battle with in SMS marketing</h3><p>Social media platforms and search engines like Google use sophisticated algorithms to control content visibility and recommendations. This means your content is constantly at the mercy of these shifting algorithms.&nbsp;</p><p>Your content might be on Google's first page and then suddenly get buried under the results pile after a Helpful Content Update (HCU), which<a href="https://searchengineland.com/impact-of-the-google-september-2023-helpful-content-was-big-for-the-seo-industry-432751"> sent shockwaves</a> through the SEO industry because it penalized niche blogs and content creators. Websites with stable rankings for years were pushed off the top in favor of larger, corporate-owned websites.&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/03/core-update-spam-policies">Google’s March 2024 Core Update</a> made things even worse because it targeted (and <a href="https://neilpatel.com/blog/google-march-2024-algorithm-update/">deindexed</a>) older domains—further punishing independent content creators. Bloggers and niche website owners were penalized <em>again</em> despite sticking to best practices.&nbsp;</p><p>While the <a href="https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/08/august-2024-core-update">August 2024 update</a> did fix some of the damage, thousands of sites never bounced back to their pre-September 2023 rankings. This is the reality of marketing on search engines and creates endless frustration for marketers, with many feeling powerless as their hard-earned rankings disappear overnight.&nbsp;</p><p>Social media is no different—algorithm updates on platforms like Instagram and Facebook may suddenly reduce your organic reach, which forces you to pay for visibility.</p><p>In contrast, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/what-is-sms-marketing-tips-types-and-strategies">SMS marketing</a> has no changing algorithms that dictate whether or not your messages will be seen. Once you send an SMS, it lands <em>directly</em> in your customer’s inbox—which makes this channel simple, stable, and reliable.&nbsp;</p><p>This means you have zero need to constantly adjust your strategy to work around changing guidelines or fight for visibility.&nbsp;</p><p>If utilized properly with ample personalization and the right tools, SMS marketing gives you full control over how and when you reach your audience without being buried by algorithms.</p><h2>Elevate your SMS marketing strategy with Plivo CX</h2><p>SMS messages are a reliable, cost-effective, and impactful way to connect with your audience directly.
