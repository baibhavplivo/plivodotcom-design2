Here’s a quick breakdown of the most common types :</p><h3>Conversational agents</h3><p>These AI-driven tools engage users through natural language processing to carry out conversations. They’re widely used in virtual assistants, chatbots, and customer support to deliver human-like interactions.</p><h3>Recommendation engines</h3><p>These agents suggest products, services, or content based on user behavior and preferences. They help personalize experiences and are widely used across e-commerce sites, streaming platforms, and social media.</p><h3>Predictive analytics agents</h3><p>Used in industries such as finance, healthcare, and marketing, these agents analyze historical data using machine learning to forecast trends and inform strategic decisions.</p><h3>RPA (Robotic Process Automation) agents</h3><p>These agents are designed to automate routine tasks such as data entry and transaction processing, help organizations reduce manual effort, improve accuracy, and boost productivity.</p><h3>Personalized learning agents</h3><p>These AI agents are focused on education, adapting to individual learning needs, tracking progress, and enhancing online learning platforms to improve educational outcomes.</p><h2>How AI agents work</h2><p>To understand what makes custom AI agents effective for <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/ai-for-customer-engagement/">customer engagement</a>, it’s helpful to look at how they operate behind the scenes.
