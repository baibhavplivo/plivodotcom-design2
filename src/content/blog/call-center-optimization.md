Tracking these metrics reveals strengths, exposes weaknesses, and sets a baseline for progress.</p><h3>First call resolution (FCR) rate</h3><p>How often do your agents solve issues on the first try? FCR matters because customers hate repeating themselves. A strong FCR rate (70-80%) cuts follow-ups, saves time, and keeps customers happy.</p><p>Check this by tracking resolved calls against the total first contacts over a week. Low numbers mean your agents might need better tools or training.</p><h3>Average handling time (AHT)</h3><p>This is the clock on each call — start to finish, including hold time and wrap-up. AHT averages <a href="https://www.callcentretools.com/tools/erlang-calculator/">6 minutes and 10 seconds</a> industry-wide, but shorter isn’t always better.</p><p>For example, a low AHT might indicate quick resolutions, but if paired with a low CSAT, it could suggest rushed interactions. Measure it daily to spot patterns (say, longer calls during peak hours) and adjust staffing or scripts accordingly.</p><h3>Customer satisfaction score (CSAT)</h3><p>CSAT, gathered from post-call surveys (typically on a 1-5 scale), tells you if your customers are walking away pleased. If your scores dip, learn why.</p><p>Generic post-call surveys (How satisfied are you?) often miss the point. Ask specific questions: Did the agent resolve your issue? Or were you transferred unnecessarily?</p><h3>Schedule adherence</h3><p>It’s the percentage of time call center agents are available to handle calls compared to their scheduled work time, accounting for breaks and other non-call activities. Low adherence means missed calls and frustrated customers.</p><p>So, track it weekly to catch chronic no-shows or overstaffing.</p><h3>Call abandonment rate</h3><p>This is the percentage of callers who hang up before speaking to an agent. A high rate can signal long waits or routing problems, which explains why they leave. Implement callbacks or improved <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/learn/contact-center/ivr-contact-center">interactive voice response (IVR) systems</a> to reduce call center wait times and retain customers.</p><h3>Net promoter score (NPS)</h3><p>NPS measures customer loyalty by asking how likely customers are to recommend the company to others, typically on a 0-10 scale, with promoters (9-10), passives (7-8), and detractors (0-6). It’s a strong indicator of overall satisfaction and can predict business growth through word-of-mouth.</p><p>Start here. Measure these metrics now, and you’ll know exactly where to push harder.</p><h2>Now, let’s begin optimizing your call center</h2><p>You’ve assessed your call center’s current performance, and it’s time to improve it.
