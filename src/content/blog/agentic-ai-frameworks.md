These agents perceive inputs, process them using algorithms or large language models (LLMs), and execute actions like retrieving data, automating workflows, making API calls, or responding to users.</p><p>Businesses use agentic frameworks to automate routine operations through pre-built modules, simplifying development and ensuring scalability and efficiency.</p><table>

</tr></tbody></table><h2>The core components that make agentic AI intelligent and autonomous</h2><p>Agentic AI frameworks rely on interconnected components to enable autonomous decision-making, action, and learning. These modules work together to process inputs, analyze data, execute tasks, and adapt without constant oversight. Here’s a closer look.</p><h3>Perception module (data ingestion and signal processing)</h3><p>The perception module collects and processes information from various sources, creating a foundation for all AI decisions. Here’s how it works:</p><ul><li><strong>Data collection: </strong>Uses sensors like cameras, mics, or web scrapers to pull in text, images, or audio.</li><li><strong>Preprocessing:</strong> Normalizes and filters raw data to make it usable.</li><li><strong>Feature extraction:</strong> Extracts key details (like objects or voices) using machine learning (ML) or neural networks.</li><li><strong>Integration:</strong>&nbsp; Sends processed data to other modules for decisions or complex tasks.</li></ul><h3>Cognitive module (decision engine and computational core)</h3><p>The cognitive module processes information and makes decisions based on goals, constraints, and available data. This central component determines the AI's actions. It:</p><ul><li>Defines goals (say, optimizing workflows or resolving issues).</li><li>Selects the best course of action to achieve the goals through reinforcement learning.</li><li>Applies probabilistic reasoning to handle uncertainty and solves problems using logic.</li><li>Uses meta-learning to adapt strategies over time.</li></ul><h3>Action Module (execution layer and task orchestration)</h3><p>The action module turns decisions into results through direct task execution. It connects the AI system to external platforms, allowing it to interact with business systems and implement its decisions.</p><p>Here’s what it does:</p><ul><li>Automates repetitive tasks without human intervention.</li><li>Controls physical devices when needed (like robotic systems).</li><li>Monitors execution to track the progress of operations.</li><li>Maintains control algorithms that direct specific operations.</li></ul><h3>Learning module (adaptive intelligence and model refinement)</h3><p>The learning module enables AI to improve through experience and feedback. It examines what worked, what failed, and why, then applies these insights to future operations.</p><p>This adaptive capability reduces the need for constant reprogramming as business conditions change. See how it works:</p><ul><li>Uses reinforcement learning to identify successful approaches through trial and error.</li><li>Analyzes historical performance data to refine decision-making across the board.</li><li>Implements continuous optimization to increase precision and effectiveness.</li><li>Adjusts to new situations and challenges based on past experiences.</li><li>Self-corrects to improve outcomes over time.</li></ul><h3>Collaboration module (multi-agent coordination and system integration)</h3><p>The collaboration module connects AI with humans and other systems to enable teamwork. It ensures that AI works effectively as part of a larger technology ecosystem rather than as an isolated tool. This module:</p><ul><li>Integrates with business tools like customer relationship management (CRM) systems and enterprise resource planning (ERP) software.</li><li>Streamlines workflows that span multiple systems.</li><li>Provides user-friendly interfaces for human interaction.</li><li>Coordinates communication between different AI agents.</li><li>Maintains consistency across connected platforms.</li></ul><h3>Security module (threat mitigation and data protection)</h3><p>The security module safeguards operations and information from threats. Its protective barrier allows AI systems to access sensitive information without compromising privacy or compliance requirements.</p><p>This module:</p><ul><li>Detects threats like malware or unauthorized access in real time.</li><li>Encrypts sensitive data to prevent breaches.</li><li>Protects against cyber attacks.</li><li>Ensures responsible AI system delivery.</li></ul><p>The effectiveness of an agentic framework depends on how well these components integrate and communicate with each other.</p><h2>Key features of a robust agentic AI framework</h2><p>Here’s what separates basic AI tools from other powerful autonomous systems: seven core features that enable agentic AI systems to function independently.</p><h3>Pre-built modules</h3><p>These building blocks contain defined triggers and actions that control AI agent behavior, like workflow templates, data pattern recognition systems, and application integrations. Instead of programming every function from scratch, your teams can select and configure pre-built modules that handle routine operations, making setup easy.</p><h3>Memory management</h3><p>AI agents need to recall past interactions to handle extended tasks.</p><p>Memory systems store conversation histories, user preferences, and previous decisions to maintain context across sessions. It lets agents pick up where they left off and build an established knowledge base to create a consistent experience that doesn't require users to repeat information.</p><h3>Multi-agent AI systems collaboration</h3><p>Agentic frameworks support multiple AI agents working together on big projects. Instead of relying on a single agent to do everything, these systems use orchestrators to divide tasks among specialized agents.</p><p>For example, one agent might handle email account creation while another manages software licensing, improving accuracy since each agent has specific responsibilities to focus on.</p><h3>Planning and reasoning</h3><p>AI agent frameworks provide reasoning engines that enable AI to plan and adapt. Using LLMs and extensive datasets, these systems analyze situations, set goals, and develop strategies to achieve them. Unlike traditional AI that follows fixed instructions, these agents adjust to changing circumstances.</p><h3>Customized decision-making</h3><p>AI-driven decision-making frameworks tailor interactions by analyzing user preferences and behavior patterns. This personalization creates more relevant experiences for your customers, from product recommendations to support solutions.</p><p>Your developers can also adjust response styles, add features, and refine training data to match specific business requirements, helping convert first-time users into repeat customers.</p><h3>Self-learning</h3><p>These frameworks learn from results and feedback, constantly refining their performance. For example, your marketing AI agents can <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/metrics">review metrics</a> of your campaigns, identify what drove conversions, and apply those insights to future campaigns — all without manual updates from your team.</p><h3>Contextual awareness</h3><p>These AI automation frameworks understand context and respond appropriately. They process multiple information streams to grasp what's happening now and what matters most.</p><p>For example, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/agentic-ai-in-healthcare">agentic AI in healthcare</a> uses this awareness to prioritize emergency cases over routine appointments. In <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/customer-service-ai-agent/">customer service</a>, it helps distinguish between minor inquiries and urgent problems that need immediate escalation.</p><h2>Why agentic AI frameworks are important</h2><p>According to Gartner, by 2029, <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-03-05-gartner-predicts-agentic-ai-will-autonomously-resolve-80-percent-of-common-customer-service-issues-without-human-intervention-by-20290">agentic AI will autonomously handle 80% of customer service issues</a> without human involvement, reducing operational costs by 30%.</p><p>This growth, you see, isn't just hype.
