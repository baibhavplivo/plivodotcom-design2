<p><a href="https://www.emarketer.com/content/5-charts-showing-potential-of-text-message-sms-marketing">66% of customers</a> prefer to use text messages to engage with companies. Since text is more accessible, <a href="https://www.emarketer.com/content/5-charts-showing-potential-of-text-message-sms-marketing">37.5% of customers</a> actually wish for brands to use text message marketing.&nbsp;</p><p>But here’s the challenge: with up to 98% open rate for text messages—the invincibility of SMS marketing invincibility undoubtedly draws a huge number of marketers to the fray. As a result, customers are bombarded with SMS from e-commerce brands all over the world.&nbsp;</p><p>This means you have to compete for every click and conversion. To stand out from the crowd, you need to use best practices for SMS marketing.&nbsp;</p><p>In this blog post, we’ve compiled a list of some tried-and-tested best practices for SMS marketing to help you kickstart your next SMS marketing campaign.&nbsp;</p><h2>Wait, let’s take care of the law first!</h2><h3>CAN-SPAM Act</h3><p>Enforced by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business">the CAN-SPAM Act</a> covers certain types of SMS marketing. This law sets rules for commercial messages and gives people the right to opt out if they don’t want to hear from you anymore.&nbsp;</p><p>You definitely wouldn’t want to ignore it as the outcome can cost you big time—hefty fines, which can reach up to $51,744 per individual violation. Also, companies may risk being blacklisted by telecom providers if they consistently send unwanted messages.</p><p><strong>What you need to do:</strong></p><ul><li>Make sure your messages aren’t misleading</li><li>Clearly identify yourself as the sender</li><li>Always include a way for people to opt-out</li><li>Respect opt-out requests immediately</li></ul><h3>GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)</h3><p>GDPR is important if you’re doing business in the EU or have customers there. This law regulates how you can collect, store, and use personal data—including for SMS marketing.</p><p><strong>What you need to do:</strong></p><ul><li>Ensure that customers actively opt-in to receive SMS communications by requiring them to check a box or click a button</li><li>Collect distinct consent for each communication channel (e.g., SMS, email, push notifications).</li><li>Be transparent about how you’ll use their data</li><li>Keep data secure and only use it for the purposes they agreed to<br></li></ul><h3>TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)</h3><p>As per the TCPA, you need to have their express written consent before sending out any marketing messages.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>What you need to do:</strong></p><ul><li>Get clear consent before sending marketing texts</li><li>Send marketing messages only during reasonable hours, generally between 8 AM and 9 PM in the recipient's local time zone</li><li>Keep a record of the consent provided by each customer</li></ul><h2>Best practices for SMS marketing&nbsp;</h2><h3>First, introduce yourself!</h3><p>Imagine receiving a text from a random number with a great offer, but you have no idea who it's from. The first instinct might be to ignore it or, worse, report it. That’s the last thing you want for your brand.&nbsp;</p><p>Therefore, you should introduce yourself in each message to ensure your audience knows exactly who’s reaching out and why they should pay attention.</p><p>A simple introduction helps establish credibility and prevents your messages from being mistaken for spam. As mentioned above, the opt-in process is legally required in many regions.</p><p>As noted above, regulations like the TCPA are set up in the U.S. to protect consumers from unwanted communication. If you fail to comply with these regulations, you may face fines or penalties.</p><p>By having customers opt-in, you confirm that they are interested in hearing from you. It also increases the chances of customers reading your messages and responding to your offers.</p><p><strong>💡Pro Tip:</strong> Always send a confirmation message whenever someone opts in. This not only reassures them that they’re successfully subscribed, but it also provides an opportunity to thank them and perhaps offer an immediate incentive, like a discount code, to start the relationship on a positive note.</p><p>For example, after someone opts in, you could send a message like: "Thank you for subscribing to [Your Brand Name] updates! Enjoy 10% off your next purchase with the code WELCOME10."</p><h3>Provide opt-out options</h3><p>In any SMS marketing strategy, you must give recipients an easy way to opt-out. Like opt-in, the TCPA in the U.S. requires that all marketing messages include a clear and simple way for recipients to opt-out. Failing to provide this can result in significant legal penalties.</p><p>Also, providing an opt-out option shows that you respect your customers' choices.</p><p>At the end of each SMS, include simple, clear instructions on opt-out. A common practice is to add a line like "Reply STOP to unsubscribe."</p><h3>Don’t send messages too often</h3><p><a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">96% of customers</a> say that they are occasionally annoyed by brand messages. The number one reason for this annoyance was “messaging too frequently”.&nbsp;</p><p>​​While there’s no one-size-fits-all rule, you can limit your promotional messages to 2-4 monthly messages. This allows you to stay on your customers' radar without overwhelming them.&nbsp;</p><p>This number can vary depending on your industry, your specific audience, and the messages you send. For instance, a daily deal alert might justify more frequent messaging, but less is often more for general updates or promotions.</p><h3>Time your texts well</h3><p>To <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/top-customer-engagement-platforms-features-pricing">maximize customer engagement</a>, it’s important to send your messages during the hours when your customers are most likely to be receptive.&nbsp;</p><p>Generally, the best times are between 9 AM and 8 PM local time.
