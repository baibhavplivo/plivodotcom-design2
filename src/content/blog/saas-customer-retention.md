<p>For SaaS companies offering services through subscriptions, customer retention significantly controls the bottom line. Customers generally pay only a fraction of their lifetime value upfront. Therefore, retaining them is crucial for driving recurring revenue. According to HubSpot, increasing customer retention rates by just 5% can <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/customer-acquisition-vs-retention">boost profits by 25% to 95%</a>.&nbsp;</p><p>However, retention alone isn't enough—businesses must balance acquiring new customers and keeping existing ones to sustain growth. Effective customer retention also opens up more opportunities for revenue growth through cross-sells, upsells, upgrades, and referrals. This also drives sustainable growth for many SaaS companies.</p><p>As <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbesbusinesscouncil/2022/12/12/customer-retention-versus-customer-acquisition/?sh=1ccdcb691c7d">Forbes points out</a>, a company cannot grow without attracting new buyers. The key is finding the right customers with a high lifetime value (LTV) that justifies their customer acquisition costs (CAC) and retention costs.</p><p>Also, great products and services are the baselines—to thrive truly, SaaS sellers must develop tailored retention strategies that keep current customers subscribing. This is especially critical given the continual nature of SaaS tools and the potential for multi-year contracts with add-on upsell opportunities.&nbsp;</p><p>In this article, we will explore how, with the right approach and tools balancing acquisition and retention, SaaS companies can acquire more valuable customers while preventing churn of existing higher-value ones.</p><h2>Why is SaaS Customer Retention Important?</h2><p>Here’s why SaaS customer retention is important:</p><h3>Faster growth and increased revenue</h3><p>According to a recent <a href="https://chartmogul.com/reports/saas-retention-report/2023/#NRR-over-2021-2022:~:text=Companies%20with%20best%2Din%2Dclass%20retention%20grow%20at%20least%201.5%2D3x%20faster%20than%20their%20peers.">ChartMogul report</a>, companies that prioritize customer retention grow 1.5 to 3 times faster than others.&nbsp; If these numbers tell us one thing alone, it’s that customer retention is the centerpiece when looking to scale your business and drive revenue growth.</p><h3>Indicates a strong product-market fit</h3><p>Product-market fit is a nuanced concept. Despite conducting pre-launch feasibility studies, many businesses fall into a real vs perceived demand fallacy, realizing one or both of these a little too late:<br></p><ul><li>Their product is not needed</li><li>Customers are unwilling to pay for it.</li></ul><p>One surefire way to confirm product-market fit early and pivot, as needed, is to look at customer retention rates over time. A steady stream of return buyers shows that you’re solving a real problem and providing adequate value to customers.</p><h3>Reduced CAC</h3><p>Acquiring new customers is often expensive, so retaining existing ones can enhance cash flow and reduce marketing costs. The happier your customers are, the less you'll spend on acquiring new ones.&nbsp;</p><p>As a SaaS company, Zendesk's success demonstrates this, as their focus on customer satisfaction lowered acquisition costs and improved loyalty.</p><h3>Increased loyalty</h3><p>Satisfied customers who consistently choose your product and have positive experiences tend to become more loyal. This fosters trust, making them less likely to switch to competitors, even if offered similar features or lower prices.</p><h3>Repeat customers are more likely to recommend&nbsp;</h3><p>Satisfied customers who regularly use your product are more likely to become loyal brand advocates and refer others. This translates to a lower CAC and higher customer lifetime value (CLTV) for your SaaS business.&nbsp;</p><p>By prioritizing a consistently positive customer experience, you're not just building a loyal following; you're creating a sustainable growth engine.</p><h2>How Do You Measure Customer Retention?</h2><p>Your customer retention rate (CRR) shows how you retained customers over a specific period.&nbsp;</p><p>Calculate customer retention by first subtracting the number of new customers you acquired within a period from the total number of customers you have at the end of the period. Next, divide that figure by your number of customers at the start of the period and multiply your answer by 100.</p><p>Here’s the simple <strong>formula for calculating your SaaS retention rate</strong> over a specific period:</p><p><em>Customer retention rate (CRR)</em><strong><em> </em></strong><em>= </em>[<em>(No of customers at the end of the period - No of new customers acquired during the period))/No of customers at the start of the period] x 100</em></p><h2>SaaS Customer Retention Metrics and Benchmarks</h2><p>SaaS customer retention metrics are KPIs that measure how effectively a company retains customers over time, providing insights into customer loyalty, satisfaction, and overall business health. <br><br>Here are some key <strong>B2C and B2B SaaS retention benchmarks</strong> to note as you grow your business:</p><ul><li><strong>Customer Retention Rate: </strong>Some experts say best-in-class SaaS businesses have customer retention rates between 85% and 87%. Meanwhile, UserPilot asserts that a good SaaS retention rate to <a href="https://userpilot.com/blog/average-customer-retention-rate/#What-is-a-good-retention-rate-for-SaaS-companies?:~:text=The%20entire%20SaaS,falling%20fairly%20consistently.">aim for is 90%</a>. Either way, this number can fluctuate over time due to factors like product updates, economic instability, average uptime percentage, and more.</li><li><strong>Customer Retention Rate by ACV: </strong>According to 2023 <a href="https://www.saas-capital.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/RB28WS1-2023-B2B-SaaS-Retention-Benchmarks.pdf">SaaS Capital</a> benchmarks, products with higher annual contract values (ACV) record higher net and gross retention rates (GRR). Companies with ACVs over $250,000 had a median NRR of 110% and a GRR of 95%, while those under $12,000 had a median NRR of 100% and a more variable GRR.</li></ul><ul><li><strong>ARR Per FTE Benchmark:</strong> Founder and angel investor Bartek Pucek suggests that at scale, companies should target a high revenue per employee, aiming for <a href="https://newsletter.pucek.com/p/working-with-people-who-give-a-st">$200k+ in ARR per FTE</a>. This range indicates efficient use of human resources relative to revenue generation, suggesting operational effectiveness.&nbsp;</li></ul><h3>Understanding and tracking key metrics</h3><p>Consider tracking these additional SaaS customer retention metrics for deeper insights:</p><h4>Customer churn rate</h4><p>Customer churn rate is the percentage of customers who stop using your product or service during a specific period, the opposite of customer retention rate.</p><p><em>Churn Rate = (No of customers lost during a period/No of customers at start of period) x 100</em></p><h4>Customer lifetime value (LTV)</h4><p>Customer lifetime value estimates the total revenue your company can expect from a single customer throughout their relationship with you, reflecting the long-term impact of your acquisition and retention efforts.<br></p><p><em>Customer LTV = Average purchase value x Average purchase frequency x Customer lifespan</em></p><h4>Repeat purchase rate</h4><p>Repeat purchase rate shows the percentage of your customers who make more than one purchase from you over a specified period. It is a key customer loyalty indicator.</p><p><em>Repeat purchase rate = (No of repeat customers/Total no of customers) x 100</em></p><h4>Net promoter score (NPS)</h4><p>Net promoter score (NPS) measures customer loyalty and satisfaction by asking how likely they are to recommend your product or service on a scale of 0 to 10.&nbsp;</p><p>It categorizes customers into promoters, detractors, and passives, then calculates the score by subtracting the percentage of detractors from promoters.</p><p><em>Net Promoter Score = % Promoters - % Detractors</em></p><p><strong>Where:&nbsp;</strong></p><ul><li>Promoters are respondents who rate your company 9 - 10</li><li>Passives are respondents who score 7 - 8.</li><li>Detractors are respondents who rate your company 0 - 6&nbsp;</li></ul><h4>Customer satisfaction score (CSAT)</h4><p>Customer satisfaction score measures customers’ satisfaction with your company by asking them to rate their experience on a predefined scale, typically from 1 to 5 or 1 to 10. This helps you assess customers' immediate sentiment about your business and identify areas for improvement.</p><p><em>Customer Satisfaction Score = (No of satisfied customers/No of survey responses) x 100</em></p><p><strong>Where</strong>:</p><ul><li>Satisfied customers are respondents who rate their experience with your brand positively (usually 4 or 5 on a 5-point scale or between 8 and 10 on a 10-point scale).</li></ul><h4>Active users</h4><p>Active users are the number of unique users engaging with your product or service over a specific period. They indicate product stickiness, user-friendliness, and utility.&nbsp;</p><p>Calculate active users daily, weekly, or monthly to gain insights into engagement patterns and the impact of marketing campaigns, new features, or other changes.</p><ul><li><strong>Daily active users (DAU) </strong>refers to the number of unique users who engage with your product or service in a single day.</li><li><strong>Weekly active users (WAU)</strong> refers to the number of unique users who engage with your product or service within one week.</li><li><strong>Monthly active users (MAU) </strong>refers to the number of unique users who engage with your product or service over the span of a month.</li></ul><p>For deeper insights, segment users by demographics or behavior and analyze activity patterns.&nbsp;</p><p>However, first, define what “active users” or “user activity” means for your business. This could include specific user actions like logging in, engaging with content, making purchases, or other key activities. Analytics tools like Google Analytics or Amplitude can then be used to monitor and optimize user activity.</p><h2>SaaS Customer Retention Strategies in 2024 with Examples</h2><p>SaaS retention strategies, shaped by personalization and the right CX software, can significantly boost customer satisfaction and loyalty.
