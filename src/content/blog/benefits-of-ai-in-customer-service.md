<p>AI is becoming an important tool in customer service, changing how businesses and customers interact every day. By making tasks easier and quicker, AI helps address some of the most common customer service issues—like slow responses, limited customer control, and a lack of help outside of business hours.</p><p>When customers use AI tools like chatbots or digital assistants, they know they’re speaking to a machine, but they value the faster response times and around-the-clock support. Emarketer reports that <a href="https://www.emarketer.com/insights/chatbot-market-stats-trends/">49% of US adults</a> have used an AI chatbot for customer service in the past 12 months, according to November 2023 data from Google and Ipsos.</p><p>When used effectively, AI&nbsp; can help&nbsp; streamline customer service tasks and scale&nbsp; support team operations. Beyond enhancing the customer experience, it can help brands reduce costs, increase efficiency, and save time.&nbsp;</p><p>For these reasons (and more), more businesses are investing in AI to elevate their customer service.&nbsp;</p><p>According to data from <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/state-of-ai-in-service">HubSpot’s State of Service 2024 report</a>, 84% of survey respondents said AI/automation tools will be instrumental in helping them meet customer service expectations. In addition, 86% said AI will transform the experience customers get with their company, and 75% also agree that AI/automation tools will help improve customer service response time.</p><p>For customers, it’s all about feeling in control and getting the assistance they need without a long wait. For businesses, the goal is to keep customers satisfied. By combining traditional customer service with AI tools, companies can create positive experiences that make a real difference.</p><p>In this blog, we’ll dive into the ways AI can elevate support and streamline efficiency — paving the way for a smoother, more satisfying customer experience — and show you how you can incorporate AI into your customer service operations.&nbsp;</p><h2>What is AI in customer service?&nbsp;</h2><p>AI is already playing a major role in customer service, and it’s only going to grow. AI technologies such as natural language processing (NLP), speech recognition, and machine learning, are helping customer support teams deliver seamless experiences by taking some of the load off human agents.</p><p>Gartner even predicts that by 2025, <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2023-08-30-gartner-reveals-three-technologies-that-will-transform-customer-service-and-support-by-2028">80% of customer service and support organizations</a> will be using generative AI to improve agent productivity and customer experience. This makes it essential for you to get a solid understanding of how AI tools work and what they can bring to customer support.</p><p>So, what’s driving the rise of AI in customer service? For one, AI tools can handle routine tasks and automate workflows, allowing agents to focus on more complex issues. They can also predict customer needs, offering personalized recommendations based on browsing history, demographics, and trends — all of which help enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty.</p><p>Common AI tools in <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/top-ai-contact-center-software">customer support</a> include:</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/solutions/self-service-chatbot">AI chatbots</a> that handle customer inquiries around the clock.</li><li>Generative AI tools like ChatGPT, which can help write responses to customer questions.</li><li>Routing tools that direct service requests to the right agent quickly and efficiently.</li></ul><p>AI is transforming <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/ai-customer-service-chatbot">customer support</a>, and it’s clear that this technology is here to stay.</p><h2>Top 6 benefits of AI in customer service&nbsp;</h2><p>There are several benefits of AI-based customer service, but let’s take a look at the 6 most popular ones: :&nbsp;</p><h3>1.Reduce average handling time (AHT)&nbsp;</h3><p>Average handle time (AHT) is the average length of a customer's call. Customers want quick answers and resolutions, and ideally, they'd love it if everything gets handled in one go — they don’t want to keep reaching out about the same issue. Reducing the AHT is one way to enhance operational efficiency.&nbsp;</p><p>That’s where AI-driven customer support can help. AI-powered chatbots reduce hold times by instantly responding to customers’ common queries using your company’s knowledge bases and past interactions, improving AHT, first response and resolution times, and customer satisfaction scores (CSATs).&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Example:</strong> If a customer asks for product-related information, AI-powered chatbots can quickly search through knowledge bases and recommend relevant videos and FAQs to help the user.&nbsp;</p><h3>2.Uncover insights by analyzing large volumes of customer data&nbsp;</h3><p>AI-driven customer service platforms can help you analyze large volumes of customer data using machine learning and natural language processing algorithms. This means you can now easily scan your customers' browsing history, feedback, and social media interactions, to unlock valuable insights.&nbsp;</p><p>With these insights, you can:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Recognize behavior patterns and trends for customer profiling and segmentation&nbsp;</li><li>Assess customer satisfaction levels, anticipate problems, and predict churns</li><li>Customize sales and marketing campaigns and increase revenue</li></ul><h3>3.Offer omnichannel support&nbsp;</h3><p>AI helps you provide <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/omnichannel-customer-experience">omnichannel customer support</a>, connecting with users through&nbsp; their preferred touchpoints, whether that’s social media, websites, or messaging apps.</p><p><strong>💡Tip:</strong> Integrate an <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/benefits-of-omnichannel-customer-service">omnichannel desktop</a> with your existing business systems to centralize customer information and provide agents with quick access to context. This ensures that&nbsp; even if a customer starts a conversation on Instagram, asks a question on your website, and chats with your WhatsAppr chatbot, the interaction remains seamless and efficient.&nbsp;</p><h3>4.Deliver hyper-personalized experiences</h3><p>Two-thirds of all respondents for a <a href="https://www.statista.com/statistics/1419129/consumer-interest-in-ai-shopping-advice-by-generation/">2023 survey by Statista</a> revealed that they’re open to buying products/services recommended to them by generative AI.</p><p>AI can curate personalized customer experiences by adapting to a customer's tone and using user data to send birthday or anniversary wishes with special discounts. This, in turn, boosts customer satisfaction and retention.</p><h3>5.Scale your customer service and business&nbsp;</h3><p>With AI-powered customer service, scaling your business becomes seamless as it grows. AI chatbots can handle countless conversations quickly and efficiently while prioritizing urgent queries.</p><p>The result? You can handle peak hours without the need for extra hiring, allowing your&nbsp; agents to focus on&nbsp; complex issues and high-value interactions&nbsp; that truly need&nbsp; a human touch. Plus, businesses can stay online 24×7 and provide support to their customers.</p><h3>6.Automate and streamline customer service workflows&nbsp;</h3><p>AI-driven customer service solutions automate routine workflows, helping you reduce agent burnout and improve productivity. Insights from a <a href="https://www.statista.com/statistics/1484479/contact-centers-ai-and-customer-info/">2023 Statista survey</a> revealed that 40% of all respondents felt AI has made their work a lot easier.&nbsp;</p><p>A few ways in which AI can streamline customer service workflows include:</p><ul><li>Intelligent ticket triaging and assignment</li><li>Transcribing customer calls into written texts</li><li>Offering proactive support throughout the customer lifecycle&nbsp;</li><li>Sending agent notifications to follow up on pending customer service tickets&nbsp;</li><li>Automatically handling calls, greeting users, and directing them to the right agent&nbsp;</li></ul><h2>What to consider when implementing AI in customer service?&nbsp;</h2><p>AI tools can benefit both your business and your customers. This facilitates swift, accurate responses for customers.</p><h3>Budget and implementation&nbsp;</h3><p>Factor in the costs of hardware, software, implementation, and licensing when incorporating AI into your workflows.
