<h2>What is MMS Messaging?</h2><p>When you go out in public these days, few people are interacting with each other — typically, they’re all on their phones. But they aren’t talking — they’re sending GIFs, emojis, and short video clips. This visual content allows consumers to interact with each other and the messaging service that lets you incorporate images, audio, and video into text messages is MMS. </p><h2>What does MMS mean?</h2><p>MMS — Multimedia Messaging Service — allows your phone to send emojis, pictures, videos, GIFs, audio recordings, and other non-text content. MMS can be more personalized than a plain text message. While you can send an emoji in an SMS message, you can send a GIF in MMS to amplify the tone of your message. </p><figure><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/6580175d90246e46223970bf_mms-example-300.png" alt="MMS Example" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div></figure><h2>MMS vs. SMS: Which is better for your use case?</h2><p>An MMS with graphical elements can be visually engaging for message recipients. Corporate marketing departments have trouble getting potential customers to pay attention to their messages. The visual element that MMS provides can solve that problem. According to <a href="https://www.salesmate.io/blog/how-to-use-gifs-emojis-videos-to-increase-email-open-response-rates/" target="_blank">Salesmate</a>, “Pop an emoji or emoticon in your subject lines, and your open rates could soar. <a href="https://www.salesmate.io/blog/email-etiquette-sales-emails/" target="_blank">56% of brands</a> that use emoticons in that way have higher open rates. It’s something your business can’t afford to neglect.”</p><p>MMS has unique strengths for several industries. Consider retail for instance — retailers can use MMS to send coupons with embedded QR codes, or receive product registrations that include images of barcodes or QR codes. We wrote a blog post about <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/mms-for-retail/" target="_blank">how retailers can take full advantage of MMS</a>. Recommended uses include coupons and promotions, personal shopping, product registrations, and branded content. Even if you’re not a retailer, it has ideas you might be able to apply to your business.</p><p>You can use MMS for any of the <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/what-is-sms/" target="_blank">same use cases as SMS</a>. Check out <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/guide-for-getting-started-with-sms-marketing-strategy/" target="_blank">our guide on getting started with SMS marketing</a> for more information.</p><p>As MMS is such a personal form of communication, we advise businesses to be thoughtful about when and how they use it. A messaging strategy loses its power when you inundate consumers with irrelevant messages or send messages too often or at inconvenient times, such as when they’re settling into bed for the night. </p><h2>Plivo’s MMS API</h2><p>Make sure to work with a high-quality MMS API provider that can provide the features, network, and infrastructure needed to help make your strategy successful.
