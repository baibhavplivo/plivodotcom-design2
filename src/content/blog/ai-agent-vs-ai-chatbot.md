<p>AI chatbots and AI agents—have increasingly become instrumental in reducing ticket queues, streamlining support delivery, and enhancing overall customer experience.&nbsp;</p><p>That’s unsurprising, given that AI chatbots can resolve as much as <a href="https://www.comm100.com/blog/state-of-automated-customer-service/#:~:text=Competition%20is%20at%20an%20all,so%20too%20does%20the%20latter.">80%</a> of your routine queries, and <a href="https://downloads.ctfassets.net/xny2w179f4ki/3FxNFG5dIUBgphM6xqLgPy/ecfaca62ff0550e4d345b31addbff762/Intercom_Customer_Service_Trends_Report_2024.pdf">47%</a> of CS specialists believe AI agents can bolster operational workflow. However, it’s quite easy to mix up both solutions and assign them to the wrong roles.&nbsp;</p><p>While AI chatbots handle basic requests such as “where is my order?” or “how do I reset my password?”, AI agents are designed to manage more nuanced interactions.&nbsp;</p><p>This includes making critical situation analysis based on provided data, handling multi-step processes, and adapting to new contexts.</p><p>In this article, we’ll explore their differences and when to use each to modernize your customer support.</p><h2>What is an AI Chatbot?</h2><p>An AI chatbot is a program designed to simulate human-like interactions with customers and provide helpful answers to questions. It uses NLP to understand and respond to customers’ queries.&nbsp;</p><p>However, AI chatbots are primarily trained on structured datasets and hooked on each end by a stock of rules that define how they respond to user inputs. The chatbot also simplifies and translates brand documents for easy reading.&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Sales assistance:</strong> <a href="https://www.renascence.io/journal/how-sephora-enhances-customer-experience-cx-through-personalization-and-digital-innovation">Sephora</a> uses its chatbot to provide product recommendations, which has helped drive sales by 35% while reducing response time by 40%.</li><li><strong>Learning and development: </strong><a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/bernardmarr/2023/04/28/the-amazing-ways-duolingo-is-using-ai-and-gpt-4/">Duolingo</a> uses AI-powered chatbots to simulate conversations with users in order to enhance interactive language learning and provide real-time feedback.</li></ul><p>Amazon and H&amp;M also implement some of the <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/best-ai-chatbots-for-customer-service">best AI chatbots</a> to reduce ticket load and improve customer service experience.</p><h2>What is an AI Agent?</h2><p>An AI agent is a more sophisticated software program designed to execute complex tasks and achieve set goals with minimal help from your support team. This is useful for setting ticket priority, assigning tickets to the right service rep, or escalating them when necessary.</li><li><strong>Predictive AI:</strong> Predictive AI agents can autonomously analyze customer data for patterns and deduce future behaviors, preferences, and potential issues. This enables proactive support delivery.</li></ul><p>Outside of customer support, AI agents from brands like <a href="https://please.ai/">Please</a> help users make restaurant reservations by accessing booking websites in real time and confirming their availability, costs, and menu.</p><h2>AI Agent vs. AI Chatbot: 6 key differences&nbsp;</h2><p>Let’s see how AI agents and AI chatbots differ.</p><table><tbody><tr>   <td><strong>Aspects</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td><strong>AI Chatbots</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td><strong>AI Agents</strong><p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Autonomy</strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Depends on user inputs or prompts to execute any tasks. <p></p>   </td>   <td>Can function independently of constant inputs and human interventions.<p></p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td><strong>Complexity of interaction </strong><p></p>   </td>   <td>Sometimes rigid. But how do you know when to use it to achieve these results?</p><h3>Scaling first-line support</h3><p>Most L1 queries are routine and monotonous—and you’ve likely gotten them more than a dozen times. You can use AI chatbots to manage these requests while assigning your service reps to higher-level (L2 and L3) complaints.</p><h3>Cost efficiency in handling high volumes</h3><p>The amount of tickets you log increases as your business grows. And that means hiring more hands to sustain delivery, which might be cost ineffective. In this case, you can employ AI chatbots to do the heavy-lifting and save your business over <a href="https://botscrew.com/blog/customer-service-chatbots-cut-costs/">30%</a> in recruitment costs.</p><h3>Enhancing response time &amp; customer satisfaction</h3><p>Consumers now want an immediate response. According to a valuable report from <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/live-chat-go-to-market-flaw?__hstc=225566981.6f787291645ed6d108360f592b5d2131.1649238994415.1649591639800.1649598244463.4&amp;__hssc=225566981.1.1649598244463&amp;__hsfp=1205817453">Hubspot</a>, “immediate” can be 10 minutes or even less. Your customers are having issues initiating a product refund. And your service reps are off to bed, leaving them with no one to talk to. That’s one of the fastest ways to lose your customers.</p><p>‍</p><p>If this sounds familiar, getting an AI chatbot may be your next go-to to provide round the clock support.</p><h2>When to use AI Agents</h2><p>When you need a self-thinking AI solution that can autonomously handle nuanced tasks rather than a bot that solely depends on prompts, AI agents are your best bet.</p><h3>Ticket categorization</h3><p>Logging, sorting, and labeling each ticket manually is draining. Assessing the priority level of each complaint before routing it to the appropriate support tier and rep can also be quite difficult.</p><p>‍</p><p>If this is you, you might want to use an AI-powered ticketing agent. These agents can autonomously log, categorize, and prioritize incoming tickets using NLP and sentiment analysis without human prompt or intervention.&nbsp;</p><p>‍</p><p>They also learn from past interactions, refine classification accuracy, and route issues to the right support tier based on urgency, expertise, and workload.</p><h3>Handling personalized and context-aware interactions</h3><p>A customer logs a ticket and is frustrated about transferring funds to the wrong account. Your chatbot replies, “<strong><em>We’re sorry this happened. Please check out these articleson how to transfer funds to the right account.</em></strong>”</p><p>For an already frustrated customer, things could turn ugly.</p><p>Thanks to sentiment analysis, reinforcement learning, and adaptive algorithms, you can use AI agents to avert such situations. AI agents can understand the customers' emotions, dig up the necessary data, and walk them through personalized assistance.&nbsp;</p><p>In fact, <a href="https://www.winsavvy.com/the-rise-of-personalized-marketing-key-statistics/">76%</a> of consumers say personalization is essential in deciding whether to repurchase from a brand or not.&nbsp;</p><h3>Automating end-to-end customer workflows</h3><p>Customer workflows for escalating hard-to-resolve queries and providing proactive customer service are often disconnected. They’re usually a bunch of triggers and branched flows. This results in siloed lapses which can delay support delivery and cap your productivity.</p><p>In this case, it is crucial to use AI agents to unify and automate these systems. AI agents can autonomously utilize APIs to fetch customers’ real-time data, analyze ticket status and priority, synchronize with existing workflows, and use the resulting analysis to trigger escalation or predictive and proactive support.</p><h2>AI Agents vs. AI Chatbots: Which one should you use?</h2><p>Both!
