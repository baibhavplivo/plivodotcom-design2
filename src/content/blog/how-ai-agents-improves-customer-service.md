<p>While voice assistants like Alexa and Siri have become familiar names, the next wave of AI customer service agents is more than that. They're designed to handle nuanced conversations, anticipate customer interactions, and deliver consistent, human-like support 24/7.</p><p>This has profoundly impacted customer expectations to the point where <a href="https://downloads.ctfassets.net/xny2w179f4ki/3FxNFG5dIUBgphM6xqLgPy/ecfaca62ff0550e4d345b31addbff762/Intercom_Customer_Service_Trends_Report_2024.pdf">68% of customer support teams</a> believe AI has directly influenced them.</p><p>This blog post will help you understand the concept of an AI agent and how it improves customer service. It will also discuss real-life use cases and challenges to help you choose the perfect AI agents to provide unmatched service.</p><h2>What is an AI agent?</h2><p>AI agents are software programs designed to replace or augment the work of human agents. They are trained to understand the user's needs, respond to inputs, and act without human intervention.</p><p>They rely on ML and NLP to execute diverse tasks, from answering simple questions to resolving complex issues such as sentiment analysis, fraud detection and customer issue prediction.</p><h2>How does an AI agent work?&nbsp;</h2><p>AI agents don’t just follow orders. They set tasks, determine priorities, and reprioritize tasks until the overall goal is achieved. The fundamental design of AI agents revolves around three key components: perception, decision-making and action.</p><p>Let’s understand how they work:</p><figure style="max-width:1376px" data-rt-max-width="1376px"><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a53ed99c64d4448f0cb_678f792dc776956ba0976579_AD_4nXcmYcFvWdYzrB542PqZODCnLh-25865685.jpeg" alt="AI agents processing information" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://aiplanet.com/learn/rag-agents-bootcamp/module-1/2429/how-do-agents-work"><em>Source</em></a></figcaption></figure><p><strong>Perception: </strong>AI agents gather data from various sources, including customer interactions, transaction histories and social media. This helps them understand the context and nuances of customer queries.</p><p><strong>Decision-making: </strong>The AI agent uses sophisticated machine learning models to identify patterns and make decisions based on the collected data. 	The agent improves decision-making by learning from past experiences and continuously refining responses.</p><p><strong>Action: </strong>Once the decision is made, AI agents can execute tasks such as answering a customer query, processing a request, or escalating complex issues to a human agent.</p><h2>How do AI agents improve customer service?</h2><p>Parul Mishra, VP of product management in digital labor at <a href="https://www.ibm.com/us-en">IBM</a>, says,&nbsp;</p><p>“<em>While the capabilities of AI agents across specific domains will vary depending on industries and use cases, the key point is that those agents will work in collaboration with AI assistants alongside a suite of other tools to transform a chatbot experience into a multi-dimensional system that can plan, test, write, and autonomously implement solutions.”</em></p><figure style="max-width:1600px" data-rt-max-width="1600px"><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a53ed99c64d4448f0cf_678f792def4b67e3a684c91c_AD_4nXf6RjbbEICIBdeJf3wMxQHzpI-dcb092a8.jpeg" alt="A bar chat about AI agents" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.salesforce.com/content/dam/web/en_us/www/documents/research/State-of-the-Connected-Customer.pdf"><em>Source</em></a></figcaption></figure><p>Here is how AI agents can improve customer service:</p><h3>Faster response times</h3><p>AI agents are available 24/7, ensuring customer inquiries are answered promptly, regardless of time zones or business hours. Being continuously available enables customers to meet their needs via self-service while improving satisfaction.</p><h3>Personalized customer interaction</h3><p>With AI entering the scene, <a href="https://www.salesforce.com/content/dam/web/en_us/www/documents/research/State-of-the-Connected-Customer.pdf">73% of </a>customers feel they are treated as unique individuals in 2024, up from 39% in 2023.</p><p>This is because AI analyzes important information such as customer name, location, and past purchases in real time to tailor services to customers so that they feel valued and important.</p><h3>Managing large-scale inquiries</h3><p>AI agents can handle thousands of queries simultaneously without delays. They integrate with knowledge bases to provide instant and accurate information and automate routine tasks such as order tracking and password resets.</p><p>This ensures they maintain consistent service levels and enhance overall customer satisfaction.</p><h3>Proactive customer support</h3><p>AI customer service agents can determine the next best action for your customers by analyzing information from customers' contracts, warranties, and purchase history. For example, AI can set email alerts when it’s time to renew a subscription or when a product upgrade or discount is available.</p><h3>Improved accuracy and consistency</h3><p>AI agents can improve the accuracy of responses through agentic loops and human-like reasoning. This ensures consistent and accurate responses to customer queries and reduces the risk of errors.</p><p>Delivering consistent results helps build trust and confidence in the brand by meeting customer expectations.</p><h3>Data-driven insights</h3><p>Incorporating AI can help you gain deeper insights into every interaction to understand customer satisfaction levels. It enables you to track key metrics such as CSAT, CES, and NPS to optimize processes and proactively address customer needs.</p><h2>Types of AI agents in customer service</h2><p>From a support tool to a strategic game-changer, here are the most impactful ways you can use AI agents in customer service:</p><figure style="max-width:1600px" data-rt-max-width="1600px"><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a53ed99c64d4448f0c1_678f792dc776956ba097656f_AD_4nXdl8ImStHwb7CfYESnwydBni5-81bea605.jpeg" alt="Customers’ degree of comfort with AI agents" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.salesforce.com/content/dam/web/en_us/www/documents/research/State-of-the-Connected-Customer.pdf"><em>Source</em></a></figcaption></figure><h3>Chatbots</h3><p>According to a study by <a href="https://now.simplr.ai/consumer-chatbot-study">Simplr</a>, in 2022, chatbot usage doubled compared to 2020. This gap allows companies to stand out by upholding ethical practices, transparency, and fairness in AI.</p><p>To create a secure customer experience, watch out for these potential risks associated with AI customer service agents:</p><h3>Complexity with existing tools</h3><p>Implementing AI systems can be complex and expensive, especially for smaller companies with limited resources. The initial investment, maintenance and training costs, and integration with existing tools can pose financial challenges when establishing commercially viable AI operations.</p><h3>Data privacy concerns</h3><p>As AI systems rely on large amounts of data, customers’ private information is at risk of being misused and manipulated. To maintain customer trust, companies must comply with legal regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and be transparent about processing and protecting customer data.</p><h3>Lack of emotional intelligence</h3><p>AI interactions can feel impersonal and lack the emotional connection customers crave, as they often involve robotic messaging. While AI enhances efficiency, it doesn’t offer the empathy many customers seek.&nbsp;</p><h2>How to choose the right AI agent for your business</h2><p>With the wave of AI sweeping in, every business wants to hire an AI agent to make work simpler and more efficient. But every business is different, and so are the capabilities of an AI agent.</p><p>If you are new to the AI realm, consider these tips to find a tool that is most compatible with your business needs:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h3>Assess your needs</h3><p>Determine the specific functions you want the AI agent to handle. Evaluate the tasks it should automate or support, such as responding to queries, managing orders, or providing recommendations.</p><h3>Ensure it integrates with existing tools</h3><p>The AI agent you select should easily integrate with your existing systems, tools, and workflows. To speed up implementation, prioritize tools that provide pre-built APIs or connectors.&nbsp;</p><p>Moreover, a seamless integration process ensures the AI agent adds value without causing delays or technical difficulties.</p><h3>Consider scalability</h3><p>Select an AI agent that can scale its processing power, storage, and analytical capabilities to meet future demands. Evaluate its ability to handle larger datasets, integrate with evolving systems, and maintain performance under heavier workloads.&nbsp;</p><p>Scalability is essential for businesses looking to expand, ensuring the AI solution remains effective and reliable as the company grows.</p><h3>Ensure compliance and security</h3><p>Finally, ensure the AI agent safeguards the data by using data encryption, controlling access, and adhering to industry standards. Learning and utility-based agents, which process large amounts of data, require stronger security to prevent unauthorized access.&nbsp;</p><p>Opt for AI solutions that offer regular security updates, comply with regulations, and minimize risks like data breaches or cyber-attacks.&nbsp;</p><h2>Make interactions more personal with Plivo CX’s AI agents</h2><p>Going forward, there is a need for seamless integration between artificial and human intelligence.
