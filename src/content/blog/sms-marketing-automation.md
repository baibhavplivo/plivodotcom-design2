<p>As an ecommerce founder or marketer, you have to grapple with several recurring challenges, such as:</p><ul><li>High cart abandonment rates</li><li>Unsuccessful email campaigns&nbsp;</li><li>Low customer retention</li><li>Unexpected crises, such as shipping delays or product recalls.</li></ul><p>These issues derail well-strategized marketing campaigns and leave your team overwhelmed.&nbsp;</p><p>You may have tried different communication channels, such as email, social media, or paid ads, to solve these issues. However, they may not have been as effective because there are several touchpoints in the customer journey, each requiring a unique and personalized approach.</p><p>So, what's the ideal solution? It's SMS marketing automation.</p><p>By using automated SMS messages, you can combat cart abandonment with timely reminders, provide real-time updates, nurture customer loyalty, and handle crises effectively—without adding to your team’s workload.</p><p>In this article, we’ll explain why SMS marketing automation is necessary. We will also explore some top-rated tools that can help you overcome these challenges.</p><h2>Why SMS marketing automation is essential for ecommerce growth?</h2><p>Research shows that SMS open rates are as high as 98%, compared to just 20% for email. Additionally, about <a href="https://www.sender.net/blog/sms-marketing-statistics/#:~:text=SMS%20Marketing%20is%20Received%20Positively,SMS%20messages%20are%20usually%20short.">75% of consumers</a> are comfortable receiving SMS messages from brands as long as they’ve opted in.&nbsp;</p><p>Evidently, SMS is a direct, effective way to reach customers and drive engagement. When combined with automation, it allows businesses to deliver timely, personalized messages that improve the customer experience.</p><p>Let’s look at some reasons why you need to try SMS marketing automation for your ecommerce business:</p><h3>Higher conversion rates</h3><p>As per Validity Research data, <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">52% of customers</a> were influenced to purchase a product, 38% were influenced to buy a service, and 23% were influenced to buy a subscription.&nbsp;</p><p>However, reaching this level of influence manually isn't feasible. You need to deliver the right message to the right customer segment at precisely the right moment. That's where SMS marketing automation tools come in.</p><p>With SMS marketing automation, you can trigger flash sales or limited-time offers.</p><p>Also, using customer data from SMS marketing automation tools, you can suggest products based on past purchases and gently nudge customers of their abandoned shopping carts, which can recapture lost sales.</p><h3>Retention &amp; loyalty building</h3><p>With SMS marketing automation, you can send <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/sms-marketing-examples">various automated SMS messages</a>, such as welcome offers, event updates, thank you messages, etc., that help your customers feel exclusive and appreciated.&nbsp;</p><p>Through SMS marketing automation tools, you can also send personalized offers on birthdays, anniversaries and other special occasions—building a long-term relationship with customers and promoting brand loyalty.&nbsp;</p><h3>Create an omnichannel marketing strategy through integration&nbsp;</h3><p>While SMS marketing is highly effective, it works even better when combined with a broader omnichannel strategy. SMS marketing automation tools let you combine SMS with email, social media, or in-app notifications to create seamless and consistent communication across all touchpoints, creating a cohesive brand experience.</p><h3>Cost-effectiveness</h3><p>Automated SMS messages are highly cost-effective. Most SMS marketing automation tools charge based on the number of messages (volume-based pricing).&nbsp;</p><p>So, you can start small and easily scale your efforts as your ecommerce business grows.&nbsp;</p><h3>Real-time updates and crisis management</h3><p>SMS marketing is ideal for real-time updates and time-sensitive situations such as flash sales, last-minute offers, or important notifications (e.g., product recalls or shipping delays). It can also be used for crisis management, where clear and timely communication is essential.<br>‍</p><p>💡<strong>Fun fact:</strong> During the COVID-19 pandemic, Starbucks leveraged SMS to communicate with customers about store closures, operational changes, and new safety protocols. The company also informed customers about curbside pickup and mobile ordering options to ensure they could still get their coffee safely.&nbsp;</p><h3>Personalization at scale</h3><p>SMS marketing automation tools collect customer data from platforms like Shopify. You get information about your customers' past purchases, website visits, and abandoned carts.&nbsp;</p><p>You can use this data to personalize customer messages and increase the likelihood of a purchase.&nbsp;</p><h2>Set up SMS marketing automation: A step-by-step guide&nbsp;</h2><p>Now, let’s explore how you can set up SMS marketing automation easily:&nbsp;</p><h3>Choose the right SMS marketing platform</h3><p>A robust SMS marketing automation platform can streamline your SMS marketing efforts and provide you with data to personalize your messages.&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Look for a platform with an intuitive user interface.&nbsp;</li><li>Ensure the platform offers advanced automation features like trigger-based messages and drip campaigns.</li><li>If it comes with features like an AI writing<a href="https://cx.plivo.com/cx/engage/features/copywriter-ai"> assistant</a> to help you create impactful and personalized copy, that is a big plus.&nbsp;</li><li>The platform should seamlessly integrate with your existing CRM, ecommerce system, or other marketing tools.</li><li>Finally, the platform should help you manage consent and comply with regulations like GDPR or TCPA.</li></ul><h3>Map the customer journey&nbsp;</h3><p>Understanding your customer’s journey is crucial for creating relevant, timely SMS campaigns and outreach messaging. Map out each touchpoint where SMS can enhance the customer experience.</p><p><strong>Here’s how you can map the customer journey:</strong></p><ol><li>Break down the customer journey into stages like awareness, consideration, purchase, and retention.</li><li>For each stage, identify what triggers a message—a website visit, cart abandonment, or purchase completion.</li><li>Define your goals for each message, such as driving sales, nurturing leads, or encouraging customer feedback.</li></ol><h3>Segment your audience</h3><p>By segmenting your SMS subscribers, you can personalize your messages, leading to better resonance and response from diverse customer groups.</p><p><strong>Some criteria to segment your audience:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Demographics</strong>: Age, gender, location</li><li><strong>Behavior</strong>: Purchase history, browsing habits</li><li><strong>Engagement</strong>: Customers who open messages regularly vs. those who don’t</li><li><strong>Stage in the customer journey</strong>: New leads vs. loyal customers</li></ul><h3>Create engaging messages</h3><p>Crafting engaging SMS messages requires brevity, clarity, and a strong call to action. Since the character count is limited to 160 characters, every word must be purposeful.</p><p><strong>Follow these best practices for creating SMS messages:</strong></p><ul><li>Use the recipient’s name or reference their past interactions to create a more personalized message.</li><li>Increase urgency with phrases like "<a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/limited-time-offer">limited-time offer</a>" or "expires soon" can drive immediate action.</li><li>Add an actionable CTA</li><li>Include shortened links to product pages, sign-ups, or offers. A short link fits the word limit of messages and also makes the text visually appealing. Use URL shortener tools like <a href="https://bitly.com/">Bitly</a>.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>💡Example: "Hi [Name], your order is confirmed! Get 10% off your next purchase using code THANKYOU10. Shop now: [Link]."</p><h3>Test and optimize campaigns</h3><p>To ensure the success of your SMS marketing automation, continuously measure key metrics, such as open rates, click-through rates, and conversions. A/B test various text messages and optimizes them based on user responses.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Here are a few areas you can test:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Message content</strong>: Try different variations of text, tone, and CTAs.</li><li><strong>Send time</strong>: Experiment with sending messages at different times of the day. The most effective times to send SMS messages are generally between 12 PM and 1 PM and also during the early evening hours of 6 PM to 8 PM. Tuesday and Saturday are identified as the best days for SMS marketing.</li><li><strong>Frequency</strong>: <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">96% of customers</a> have felt annoyed by brands’ SMS messages, and the number one reason was “messaging too frequently.” 6 to 8 messages per month is often considered ideal for maximizing revenue per send while keeping opt-out rates low.&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Segment performance</strong>: Analyze how different segments respond to various campaigns. For example, send different messages to different segments and measure open rates and click-through rates to test effectiveness.&nbsp;</li></ul><h3>Ensure compliance&nbsp;</h3><p>Compliance with SMS marketing laws is critical to avoid penalties and maintain customer trust. To avoid potential fines, familiarize yourself with regulations like the <a href="https://www.fcc.gov/general/telemarketing-and-robocalls#:~:text=In%20an%20effort%20to%20address,artificial%20or%20prerecorded%20voice%20messages.">Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)</a> in the US or <a href="https://gdpr.eu/what-is-gdpr/">GDPR in Europe</a>.</p><p><strong>Key compliance points include:</strong></p><ul><li>Ensure you have opt-in consent from your customers before sending any marketing messages. You can add the opt-in forms on website homepages and emails.&nbsp;</li><li>Always provide a clear and easy way for customers to opt out or unsubscribe from messages. Don’t forget to add an ‘opt-out’ or ‘unsubscribe’ link at the bottom of each message.&nbsp;</li><li>Inform customers upfront about how often they can expect to receive messages.
