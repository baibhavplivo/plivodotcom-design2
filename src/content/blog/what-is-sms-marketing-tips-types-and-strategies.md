<p>If you’re not sending text messages to your customers, you’re leaving money on the table.</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">52% of customers</a> purchased a product and 28% purchased a service after being influenced by brand messaging received via SMS or other messaging platforms.&nbsp;</li></ul><ul><li><a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">57% of customers </a>admit that they’ve intentionally waited for brand text messages, hoping for incentives—only to go a long time without hearing from a brand. As a result, <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">32% of these customers</a> ended up purchasing from a competitor.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>In this blog post, we’ll explore SMS marketing, discuss its benefits, and explain how to get the most ROI from it.&nbsp;</p><h2>What is SMS marketing?</h2><p>Short message service (SMS) marketing refers to the process of marketing a product or service through short messages sent directly to the prospect’s mobile device or smartphone. These messages can be:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>promotional offers</li><li>discount coupons</li><li>shipping notifications</li><li>cart abandonment messages.</li></ul><p>Like email marketing, SMS marketing is an opt-in service. This means you must seek your customers’ permission before sending them messages.&nbsp;</p><p>SMS marketing increases access to customers at the right time and the likelihood of being seen since they receive messages directly in their mobile inbox—no logging in, connecting to the internet, or signing in.&nbsp;</p><h2>Importance and benefits of SMS marketing</h2><p>Here are some more benefits of SMS marketing for businesses:</p><h3>1. Reach customers faster</h3><p>Text messages are perfect for delivering time-sensitive information that can’t be communicated by email. In the US alone, people check their smartphones <a href="https://fortune.com/well/2023/07/19/how-to-cut-back-screen-time/">144 times a day</a>. Since text messages don’t need internet connectivity and are mostly displayed on the mobile banner, the likelihood of checking SMS increases.&nbsp;</p><p>You can send a text message about a flash sale or a new product launch to reach a large number of customers immediately and drive them to your website or store.</p><h3>2. Better open rates</h3><p>Text messages are delivered directly to a recipient's mobile device and usually appear as notifications, unlike emails. This makes them easier to open and read.&nbsp;</p><p>Moreover, <a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">45% of consumers</a> demonstrate a preference for sales and promotional messages from brands, which makes it a better channel to engage customers.&nbsp;</p><h3>3. Complement other marketing channels</h3><p>SMS marketing complements other marketing strategies like blog or social media marketing. You can use text messages to drive traffic to your website or social media page or promote email campaigns to increase engagement.&nbsp;</p><p>For example, after sending an email newsletter about a new product line, send a follow-up text with a discount code and a link to the product page on your website.</p><h3>4. Build customer loyalty</h3><p>You can establish a personal and direct connection with your customers. Sending personalized text messages with your customers’ names and purchase information fosters a sense of exclusivity and strengthens brand loyalty.</p><p>For example, send birthday or anniversary messages with special offers, offer loyalty program updates and rewards, or provide personalized recommendations based on past purchases.</p><h2>Types of SMS marketing campaigns</h2><p>There are various types of SMS marketing campaigns to achieve different goals and objectives:</p><h3>1. Promotional SMS marketing campaigns</h3><p>Promotional SMS campaigns are designed to generate buzz, drive sales, and increase brand visibility. These messages typically highlight special offers, discounts, promotions, or upcoming events.&nbsp;</p><p>You can announce a flash sale, offer exclusive discounts to loyal customers, or showcase a new product launch. The goal is to entice recipients to take immediate action, such as making a purchase or visiting a website.</p><h3>2. Transactional SMS marketing campaigns</h3><p>Transactional SMS campaigns are focused on delivering essential and time-sensitive information to customers regarding their transactions or interactions with a business.&nbsp;</p><p>Specific actions, such as order confirmations, shipping updates, appointment reminders, or account notifications, trigger these messages. Transactional SMS campaigns maintain transparency and trust throughout the customer journey.</p><h3>3. Conversational SMS marketing campaigns</h3><p>Conversational SMS campaigns prioritize building relationships and fostering two-way communication with customers. Unlike traditional one-way messaging, they encourage interaction and engagement by inviting recipients to respond and participate in a dialog with the brand.&nbsp;</p><p>For example, <a href="https://engage.plivo.com/accounts/login/">Plivo CX Engage</a> tracks customer behavior to create triggers and automatically sends personalized messages with discounts, surveys, etc, so customers feel tempted to engage.&nbsp;</p><p>Whether it's asking for feedback, conducting surveys, offering customer support, or initiating personalized conversations, conversational SMS campaigns humanize the brand and create meaningful connections with customers, ultimately strengthening loyalty and retention.</p><h2>Best practices to drive higher ROI from SMS marketing</h2><h3>1. Get consent with opt-in and opt-out messages.</h3><p>You need to obtain consent from your customers to send marketing text messages. There are a couple of ways you can do this:<br></p><ul><li>Ask for consent through opt-in forms to use their phone numbers for marketing.&nbsp;</li><li>Run keyword campaigns to allow your customers to text a specific word or code to your number to opt-in for text messages.</li><li>Offer an SMS opt-in checkbox for customers to receive SMS updates or exclusive offers during the online checkout process.</li></ul><p>Remember to leave them the option to unsubscribe via a link in each text message. Add a CTA</h3><p>The end goal of SMS marketing is to engage with customers and nudge them to take the desired action. Keep messages concise, relevant, and engaging</h3><p>Avoid jargon or filler content to keep your messages relevant to recipients. Adhere to privacy laws and regulations</h3><p>Ensure compliance with privacy laws and regulations, such as the <a href="https://www.fcc.gov/sites/default/files/tcpa-rules.pdf">Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)</a> and the <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business">CAN-SPAM Act</a> in the United States or the <a href="https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/policies/data-protection/data-protection-regulation/#:~:text=data%20protection%20rules-,What%20is%20the%20GDPR%3F,application%20on%2025%20May%202018.">General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)</a> in the European Union.&nbsp;</p><p>The TCPA outlines rules for obtaining customer consent before sending promotional messages via SMS.</p><p>To ensure SMS marketing compliance, businesses should:</p><ul><li>Obtain opt-in consent&nbsp;</li><li>Avoid misleading or deceptive content</li><li>Identify yourself and the purpose of your message</li><li>Respect do-not-call lists and time restrictions for sending messages</li><li>Provide opt-out options.</li></ul><p>After checking all these pointers, you’re ready to get started with SMS marketing.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h2>Choosing the right SMS marketing platform</h2><p>Features to consider when selecting an SMS marketing platform:</p><h3>1.
