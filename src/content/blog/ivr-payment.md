<p>Consumer demand for always-on, instant, self-service options has changed the contact center landscape. Meeting the ever-rising expectation for connected service experiences puts pressure on contact centers to deliver an outstanding customer experience (CX).&nbsp;</p><p>Giving your team the right tools to do their jobs more efficiently can help drive higher customer satisfaction and create a less stressful working environment. IVR payment services are one of those key tools.&nbsp;</p><p>IVR (Interactive Voice Response) payment services are automated systems that allow customers to make payments using their phones.&nbsp; This technology is no longer confined to the banking industry. Utility companies, telecom providers, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/ivr-for-contact-centers/">contact center customer service</a>, healthcare providers, and retailers use IVR payments.&nbsp;</p><p>In this blog post, we’ll dive into IVR payments: how IVR payments work, if they’re secure for your business, and how to choose the right IVR payment provider for your business. We’ll also highlight the top five benefits of IVR payments to demonstrate that this tool can elevate the customer experience in virtually every industry.&nbsp;</p><h2>What is an IVR payment?</h2><p>An <a href="https://www.plivo.com/use-case/ivr/">IVR</a> payment is a type of payment system that enables customers to make payments by phone without the assistance of an operator. Rather than speaking to an actual person, the customer goes through a recorded menu to safely key in their account number, PIN, credit card number, debit card number, or bank details on the spot.</p><p>IVR payments aim to provide a safe, effective, and easily accessible payment solution. It also integrates with payment solutions for firms that complete a high volume of transactions to avoid overworking their customer service and support teams.</p><p>IVR systems also enable organizations to collect ACH (Automated Clearing House) payments automatically.</p><p>This is particularly helpful when used for regular, routine payments, like membership fees, bills, or subscription services; this saves time and energy, minimizes the danger of delayed check/ - payments, and improves cash flow for the company.</p><p>{{cta-style-1}}</p><h2>How do IVR payments work?</h2><p>When customers reach an IVR payment system, they will hear a series of voice prompts asking for their payment details — such as their account number, payment details, and the invoice amount. The IVR system then processes the payment through ACH. This tender allows direct debit from the customer’s bank account to the business’s account.</p><p>Cloud-based IVR payments are designed to integrate seamlessly with a company’s existing phone systems, using existing lines and infrastructure. This means customers can access the payment service by calling the same number they use for other customer service needs.&nbsp;</p><p>Plus, you can integrate an IVR system with your payment processors. So, when a customer enters their payment information, the IVR system securely transmits this data to the processor for authorization. This integration ensures that payments are processed faster and funds are conveniently transferred to the company’s merchant account.</p><p>This streamlined setup allows businesses to automate payment collection, reduce manual handling, and improve efficiency while offering customers a convenient and secure way to pay over the phone.</p><p>For a better understanding, here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how IVR payments work:</p><ul><li><strong>Step 1: Customer initiates the call</strong>: The process begins when a customer calls a business's dedicated IVR payment line.</li><li><strong>Step 2: System greeting</strong>: The IVR system greets the caller with a recorded message, offering them options to proceed with making a payment.</li><li><strong>Step 3: Selection of payment option</strong>: The customer is prompted to select the payment method option, usually by pressing a number on their phone’s keypad.</li><li><strong>Step 4: Input of payment details</strong>: The system guides the customer to enter their payment details, such as a credit card number, expiration date, and security code, or bank account information for ACH payments, using the keypad.</li><li><strong>Step 5: Verification</strong>: Once the details are entered, the IVR system verifies the information, ensuring that everything is accurate and complete.</li><li><strong>Step 6: Confirmation</strong>: After verification, the system confirms the payment amount and asks the customer to approve the transaction.</li><li><strong>Step 7: Processing the payment</strong>: The IVR system securely accepts payments and transmits the data to the payment processor for authorization.<strong>‍</strong></li><li><strong>Step 8: Receipt and completion: Once the payment is successfully processed, the system provides a confirmation message and may offer to send a receipt via email or text. The call is then concluded.</strong></li></ul><h2>Four benefits of IVR payments</h2><p>IVR payments have plenty of benefits, but here are four important ones for businesses considering using IVR payments.</p><h3>1. It can lead to fewer late payments</h3><p>One of the main advantages of using an IVR payment service is that it can significantly reduce late or bounced payments. It gives customers the flexibility to make payments on their own, anytime, anywhere — reducing the chances of missing a payment deadline.&nbsp;</p><p>It becomes almost impossible for users to skip or miss payments unintentionally with ACH payments. Automatic payments made using ACH are easier and less time-consuming than writing or mailing checks. Plus, it’s usually quick and free, depending on where you bank and the type of payment transfer involved.&nbsp;</p><h3>2. It can increase customer satisfaction</h3><p>Customers no longer need to track and remember payment deadlines or make manual transactions. IVR technology guarantees customers that their payments will be made on time. It also helps route callers to the agent most qualified to solve customer problems and reduce hold times, resulting in happier customers.&nbsp;</p><p>For instance, with Plivo, you can set your <a href="https://www.plivo.com/use-case/call-forwarding-service/">call forwarding</a> with preferred routing rules to best fit your customer needs. Forward calls to agents in different time zones to ensure round-the-clock availability, or automatically move the call to another representative if an agent doesn’t pick up after 15 seconds.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><figure style="max-width:1427px" data-rt-max-width="1427px"><div><img src="/images/blog/66d17f761bf5b406e432e487_AD_4nXeSmIRj7MtZqI0nrcmsET6wMQODKslK9qAuU7PdEZCxH-CjWYu-0f7f4209.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="" loading="auto"></div></figure><h3>3. It improves payment security</h3><p>Often, IVR payment systems incorporate voice recognition technology—different from speech recognition—to verify an individual’s identity, adding an additional layer of security.
