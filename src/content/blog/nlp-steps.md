<p>Natural language processing (NLP) works behind the scenes in places you might not even notice — filtering spam emails, customer service chatbots, and even accurate Netflix recommendations. It’s woven across industries and is rapidly evolving, with the market projected to hit <a href="https://www.statista.com/forecasts/1449869/nlp-market-size-world">156.76 billion by 2030</a>.</p><p>It's natural to wonder whether NLP is too complex or worth integrating into your systems. This guide offers five digestible NLP steps so you can understand how the technology works and whether it’s a good fit for your business.</p><h2>What is NLP?</h2><p>Simply put, NLP lets a machine understand, interpret, and respond to human language, written or spoken. It's the reason voice assistants listen to your commands, chatbots answer your queries, and search engines predict what you’re looking for even before you finish typing.</p><p>The NLP process is powered by two key components.</p><p><strong>Natural language understanding (NLU) </strong>helps machines decode what humans mean, even if the content isn't totally clear. For example, customer support chatbots use NLU to recognize when a user is frustrated (based on words like “poor service” or “I need help now”) and escalate the issue appropriately.</p><p>Once the machine understands what the user is saying, <strong>natural language generation (NLG) </strong>generates a response in a way that sounds natural. Instead of just responding with pre-set phrases, the system personalizes the response based on the user’s request, offering logical next steps, similar to how a human agent would.</p><figure style="max-width:1318px" data-rt-max-width="1318px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/674ebe5867fbf5ffe152d152_AD_4nXdkW8sxk_wk52fGFwyoI56uCOnngD8iveYN4ebEBjIRdrqsWLwigY7P2BT4eVSFjYHMOXgenQIcdzDAmEoLTrvHCCMltCKFDkxLzGFpS88XEQWcp7PBbPEhdvq0x1DpgJgLZgtz1A.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Image displaying the basic working of NLP" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><em>Enhance your customer experience with Plivo-powered AI conversations&nbsp;</em></figcaption></figure><p>Here’s a quick rundown of the stages of NLP:</p><ul><li>Splits sentences into smaller chunks (tokenization in NLP) for easier processing.</li><li>Identifies keywords, grammar, and structure to understand context.</li><li>Detects names, dates, locations, and brands (e.g., virtual assistants retrieving order details).</li><li>Determines if a message is a question, request, or complaint for accurate chatbot/voice assistant responses.</li><li>Assesses tone and emotions to gauge customer satisfaction in chats.</li></ul><p>To better understand NLP techniques, let’s go through the most common everyday NLP applications.</p><h2>Most common everyday NLP applications</h2><p>Along with autocorrecting and filtering spam emails, NLP is most commonly used in the following use cases:</p><ul><li><strong>Chatbots and virtual assistants: </strong>NLP enables AI chatbots in customer service to understand and respond to human language, improving customer support and user engagement. These virtual assistants handle tasks like answering FAQs, scheduling appointments, and providing 24/7 customer service, mimicking human interactions to improve efficiency.</li><li><strong>Language translation: </strong>Tools like Google Translate use NLP to provide real-time multilingual support. You use this to communicate with global customers and break language barriers in customer service.</li><li><strong>Sentiment analysis: </strong>How does a machine know when a caller is frustrated, angry, or simply uninterested in your product? Sentiment analysis scans customer reviews, support tickets, or even social media mentions to determine if the feedback is positive, negative, or neutral. You can use it to gauge customer opinions.</li><li><strong>Speech recognition and voice AI:</strong> Speech recognition converts spoken words into text which helps businesses with real-time transcription, AI-driven interactive voice response (IVR) responses, and improved call routing. An advanced <a href="https://www.plivo.com/voice/automatic-speech-recognition/">ASR (automatic speech recognition)</a> tool like Plivo also masks inappropriate content in transcriptions and improves speech recognition accuracy by providing a list of hint words expected from the caller.</li></ul><p>Now that we know about the most basic everyday NLP applications, let’s understand the five NLP steps in detail.&nbsp;</p><h2>5 steps in natural language processing</h2><p>Let’s go through the five steps in NLP with examples.</p><h3>Step #1: Lexical analysis</h3><p>Text preprocessing in NLP precedes lexical analysis as it undergoes cleaning, tokenizing, and structuring raw data to make it readable for NLP models.</p><p>A <a href="https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/14/5/1737">study</a> emphasizes that understanding grammatical structures and keywords is vital for chatbots to provide coherent and relevant responses in real-world situations. Lexical analysis helps NLP do so, making it one of the most important natural language processing steps.</p><p>It converts a sequence of characters into a sequence of tokens. This tokenization in NLP deciphers and segments languages into units (lexemes), such as sentences, phrases, and words.</p><p>Some words can stand alone, like “cat” or “jump”. These are free morphemes because they don’t need any extra help to be complete words. However, then there are bound morphemes, which have to latch onto something else to make sense. For instance,&nbsp; “replaying”:</p><ul><li>“re-” (a prefix meaning “again”)</li><li>“play” (the root word that holds the meaning)</li><li>“-ing” (a suffix showing it's happening right now)</li></ul><p>Neither of these words can exist alone, they always need a root word to stick to.</p><p>Machine learning in AI plays an important role in lexical analysis by helping NLP systems continuously improve their ability to identify and segment words accurately. When NLP analyzes text, it goes through a process to understand these layers, which helps understand word meanings, grammatical structures, and even how words change depending on context.</p><p>Lexical analysis teaches AI to deconstruct language the way the human brain does, but faster.</p><h3>Step #2: Syntactic analysis</h3><p>Syntax is the foundation of language, it’s how words and phrases come together to form meaningful sentences. Syntactic analysis (or parsing) lets NLP systems understand sentence structure and relationships between words rather than analyzing them in isolation.</p><p>This ensures that AI-powered chatbots, smart IVR systems, and voice assistants interpret user queries correctly.</p><p>When NLP performs syntactic analysis, it:</p><ul><li><strong>Identifies sentence structure: </strong>Breaks a sentence into subject, predicate, objects, clauses, etc.</li><li><strong>Determines relationships between words: </strong>Determines which words depend on or modify others.</li><li><strong>Checks for grammatical correctness:</strong> Ensures the sentence follows valid language rules.</li></ul><p>Syntactic analysis is important for chatbots and virtual assistants to improve <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/smart-ivr/">customer experience with smart IVR</a>. When a customer says, “I need help to reset my password”, the system uses syntactic analysis to recognize:</p><ul><li>“I” as the subject</li><li>“need help” as the action</li><li>“resetting my password” as the specific request</li></ul><p>This structured understanding helps the AI respond appropriately, directing users to the right solution without confusion or misinterpretation.</p><h3>Step #3: Semantic analysis</h3><p>Semantic analysis in NLP deciphers meaning based on context, intent, and sentiment. The same word or phrase can imply different things depending on how it's used, and semantic analysis ensures AI comprehends these nuances accurately.</p><p>For instance, if a caller says, "I need to cancel my order because I wasn’t happy with the last one," a basic AI might fixate on the keyword "cancel" and proceed with the request.</p><p>But a Plivo-powered voice agent with semantic analysis understands the full context. It detects dissatisfaction from "wasn’t happy with the last one" and responds empathetically:</p><ul><li>“I’m really sorry about your previous experience. Let’s make this right.”</li><li>Apart from processing the query, it might offer a resolution: “How about a 20% discount on your current order as an apology?”</li></ul><figure style="max-width:1215px" data-rt-max-width="1215px"><div><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/656ac26f3a3f6e1cc08ba7a5/67d265081cba9aa3fbca994a_AD_4nXdSOHmUCoXk-c61r1Kmc3ZZXvc-JpXEkOw4_B1IjSWSZ_-XeGE3ONLlPm6K2QPYgUndnVdZiQpjckDhM4uPRVgAVcLTcNKQb-l39LJTi9F2q90qg2_b2KkK5zBgOp2-GVeExYwf.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Image showing Plivo’s AI-powered voice agent in action" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><a href="https://www.plivo.com/ai-voice-agents/">Source</a></figcaption></figure><p>Semantic analysis helps voice agents understand natural conversations instead of just keywords. This context-aware approach makes customer interactions more human, but you can also use these <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/voice-transcriptions-for-customer-analytics/">voice transcriptions for customer analytics</a> to make informed decisions.</p><h3>Step #4: Disclosure integration</h3><p>Discourse integration helps NLP models grasp the broader meaning of conversations by considering previous sentences for context. This ensures more accurate interpretations and reduces confusion in AI-driven interactions.</p><p>For example, in a customer support chat:</p><p><strong>Caller: </strong>"I lost my credit card yesterday."</p><p><strong>Bot: </strong>“I’m sorry to hear that. Would you like to report it as lost or request a replacement?"</p><p><strong>Caller: </strong>“Cancel it.”</p><p>Without discourse integration, the AI might struggle to understand whether the user wants to cancel the report or the card itself. By analyzing the conversation history, it correctly determines that "cancel it" refers to the lost card and proceeds accordingly.</p><p>This is important for businesses to automate customer interactions, and ensure smarter, context-aware responses in finance, healthcare, and beyond.</p><h3>Step #5: Pragmatic analysis</h3><p>Pragmatic analysis interprets meaning based on context, tone, and real-world knowledge rather than just literal word definitions so that the conversations feel natural and relevant. For instance, when a user asks, "What's the price of your premium subscription plan?"</p><p>The bot responds with, "Sure, our premium starts at $50 per month.”</p><p>Now, if the user simply responds, "That's a little steep," the bot automatically suggests, "Would you like to explore our standard plan starting at $30?"</p><p>While the user doesn't explicitly mention they want to explore a lower-priced option, pragmatic analysis recognizes the implied concern and suggests an alternative.</p><p>Businesses benefit from this by providing better customer engagement, fewer misunderstandings, and more effective automation.</p><p>While the steps in NLP help make customer experience better, it’s not all pros.&nbsp;</p><h2>Challenges and future advancements in NLP</h2><p>One of the biggest NLP challenges is the bias in AI models.</p><p>Since AI learns from human data, it can reflect societal biases, leading to unfair or misleading outputs. <a href="https://news.mit.edu/2024/researchers-reduce-bias-ai-models-while-preserving-improving-accuracy-1211">MIT researchers</a> developed a technique that reduces bias in AI models while preserving or even improving their accuracy for fairer and more reliable AI decision-making.</p><p>Another challenge is understanding regional dialects and slang. Many NLP models struggle with informal speech, code-switching, and cultural nuances, making accurate interpretation difficult in multilingual or diverse environments.</p><p>Ethical concerns, such as misinformation, privacy risks, and deepfakes (AI-generated content that falsely mimics real people) add to the complexity.</p><p>Looking ahead, context-aware AI conversations will improve chatbot and virtual assistant interactions, making them more natural and relevant. Real-time speech translation is also advancing, aiming for easy, culturally aware translations without robotic phrasing. As NLP evolves, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/how-will-voice-integration-shape-conversational-ai/">voice integration in conversational AI</a> will focus on making AI communication more inclusive, ethical, and human-like.</p><h2>How Plivo's AI-powered voice agents advance intelligent&nbsp; conversations</h2><p>Keeping up with customer queries can be overwhelming, especially as your business grows.
