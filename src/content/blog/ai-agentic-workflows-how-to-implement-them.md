<p>Workflows used to mean fixed paths: Click A, then B happens. One step led to another, like clockwork — predictable but inflexible.</p><p>Now, AI agentic workflows plan their own work, select tools, learn from mistakes, and adapt to changing conditions.</p><p><a href="https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/how-agents-can-improve-llm-performance/">Andrew Ng</a>, founder of Deeplearning.AI, finds this game-changing. He says, “<em>I think AI agent workflows will drive massive AI progress this year — perhaps even more than the next generation of foundation models.</em>”</p><p>The business adoption rate is on the rise, too. With Gartner predicting that AI agents will be part of <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/intelligent-agent-in-ai">33% of enterprise software apps</a>, leading to 15% of day-to-day work done autonomously without human intervention, the question isn't<em> if</em> you'll use this technology, but <em>when</em>.</p><p>In this guide, we’ll discuss everything you need to know about AI-driven workflow automation.</p><h2>What is an AI agentic workflow?</h2><p>AI agentic workflow is a sequential process that uses large language models (LLMs) to perform complex tasks with the help of AI agents. At their core, these agents combine generative AI's cognitive abilities, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/nlp-steps/">natural language processing</a> (NLP), and machine learning (ML). They make decisions based on context, learn from available data, communicate through plain language, and take specific actions to complete defined objectives.</p><p>Unlike standard automation, these workflows adapt as they run. They plan, assess progress, and change course when needed to complete tasks.</p><table>

These workflows distribute responsibilities across specialized agents working together rather than relying on a single AI agent for everything.</p><h2>What makes a workflow agentic?</h2><figure><div><img src="/images/blog/681b6c01c49cb05dfe7a2519_AD_4nXflTlCHxhWRVRd2ylQUgc5k48unOkLCB3pTwSixtcidCLJLONk-55612956.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Agentic workflow capabilities that make systems think and adapt" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><em>The capabilities that make a workflow agentic</em></figcaption></figure><p>For an AI workflow to be truly agentic, you’ll need these four capabilities.</p><ol><li>Task decomposition and planning</li></ol><p>Agentic workflows first use the agents to divide larger tasks into smaller, manageable components. When faced with a challenging goal, the AI agent:</p><ul><li>Analyzes the overall task.</li><li>Identifies logical subtasks.</li><li>Map dependencies between steps.</li><li>Creates a sequential priority list.</li></ul><p>For example, when you perform tasks like processing insurance claims, an agentic system identifies the necessary steps: validating customer information, reviewing policy details, checking for fraud indicators, and calculating payouts.</p><p>It then creates an execution plan that accounts for dependencies between these steps.</p><ol start="2"><li>Tool use and integration</li></ol><p>At execution time, agentic workflows pull data from many sources (sensors, databases, APIs) and decide what to do next.</p><p>The concept was originally developed to help with computer vision challenges. Early language models couldn't process images, so developers created functions that linked them to visual APIs. This approach expanded as models like Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) evolved.</p><p>Modern agentic workflows connect with external resources like:</p><ul><li>Web search engines to find current information.</li><li>Code interpreters to run computations.</li><li>APIs to interact with other services.</li><li>Data stores to retrieve specialized knowledge.</li></ul><p>The selection of tools can be predetermined or left to the agent's discretion. For complex tasks, allowing the agent to choose appropriate tools works best, while simpler workflows benefit from predefined tool selection.</p><ol start="3"><li>Reflect and iterate</li></ol><p>If you think the job is done after task execution, this is where it gets interesting. Agentic workflows improve through self-evaluation. Rather than delivering single-attempt outputs, they review their work, identify problems, and make refinements.</p><p>The workflows store context and feedback across interactions. This memory capability comes in two forms.</p><p>First, <strong>short-term memory</strong> keeps track of recent conversation history and current task progress, helping the agent maintain context and determine next steps.<strong> </strong>And second, <strong>long-term memory</strong> stores information across multiple sessions, enabling personalization and performance improvements over time.</p><p>Without memory, AI systems would restart from scratch with each interaction. Memory turns one-off interactions into ongoing, evolving relationships.</p><ol start="4"><li>Distribute responsibilities</li></ol><p>Complex tasks often require multiple types of expertise. Agentic workflows can distribute work across specialized AI agents, each handling different aspects of the work, much like how human teams collaborate on complex projects.</p><p>For example, this multi-agent collaboration in customer service automation would look like:</p><ul><li>One agent interprets customer requests.</li><li>Another searches knowledge bases for relevant information.</li><li>A third crafts personalized responses.</li><li>A supervisor agent coordinates the entire process.</li></ul><p>This division of labor enhances overall performance by leveraging each agent's strengths. It works particularly well for tasks requiring diverse skills or parallel processing.</p><h2>Key components of agentic workflows</h2><p>Under the hood, agentic workflows combine different technologies. When properly integrated, they create something far more powerful than any single component could achieve on its own.</p><h3>AI agents</h3><p>AI agents form the core intelligence within agentic workflows. Built on LLMs, these agents provide reasoning, planning, and reflection capabilities. The semantic engine (typically the LLM) provides both reasoning capabilities and a conversational interface. This allows agents to seek clarification or approval when needed while still working autonomously on routine tasks.</p><p>While traditional AI requires constant guidance, agentic AI evolves and adapts to new situations without much human guidance and training.</p><h3>Robotic process automation (RPA)</h3><p>RPA enables AI agents to handle rule-based, repetitive tasks across different applications. This technology creates software robots that mimic human actions like data entry, transaction processing, and report generation.</p><p>In an agentic workflow, RPA serves as the hands that perform structured work. For example, after an AI agent extracts information from unstructured invoice documents, RPA automatically enters that data into accounting systems, eliminating tedious manual work.</p><h3>Natural language processing</h3><p>NLP enables agentic workflows to understand and respond to human language. Here are a few critical functions that it takes care of:</p><ul><li>Interpreting user requests and questions.</li><li>Understanding context and intent.</li><li>Generating human-like responses.</li><li>Processing text documents for information.</li></ul><p>This component eliminates the need for specialized training or programming knowledge when working with AI agents. Users can simply express their needs in everyday language.</p><h3>Machine learning algorithms</h3><p>ML algorithms help agentic workflows learn from experience and improve over time. These algorithms identify patterns in data, make predictions, and optimize processes without explicit programming.</p><p>This self-improvement capability means workflows get better with use. They learn which approaches work best in specific situations and adapt their strategies accordingly.</p><h3>Prompt engineering</h3><p>The performance of agentic workflows depends heavily on how agents are instructed. Prompt engineering shapes agent behavior through carefully designed instructions and examples.</p><p>Some advanced prompt engineering techniques include:</p><ul><li><strong>Chain of thought:</strong> Guiding the agent through logical reasoning steps.</li><li><strong>Planning:</strong> Breaking complex tasks into manageable steps.</li><li><strong>Self-reflection: </strong>Enabling the agent to review and improve its work.</li></ul><p>These techniques help LLMs understand complex requirements and produce more accurate, relevant outputs consistently.</p><h3>Workflow orchestration</h3><p>This coordinates all components within an agentic system. It defines execution sequences, manages dependencies, and ensures tasks happen at the right time.</p><p>Orchestration tools provide interfaces for designing, monitoring, and troubleshooting complex workflows. They connect multiple technologies and handle scheduling, error management, and resource allocation to keep processes running smoothly.</p><h3>Integrations</h3><p>These connect agentic workflows with existing business systems and data sources. Integrations ensure agents can access required information and take meaningful actions within your environment. They can be of any type, such as:</p><ul><li><strong>Data integrations</strong> that consolidate information into accessible formats.</li><li><strong>Agent frameworks</strong> like LangChain, LangGraph, and crewAI that expand capabilities.</li></ul><p><strong>Tool integrations</strong> that give agents access to specialized functions.</p><table>

A typical workflow looks like this:</p><ol><li>An inquiry agent receives and categorizes the customer’s query, using NLP to identify the issue.</li><li>The response agent generates an appropriate response based on past interactions and customer data, offering a personalized solution.</li><li>If unresolved, the escalation agent escalates the query to a human agent, providing context and previous interaction history.</li></ol><p>This multi-agent approach ensures faster resolutions, improves customer satisfaction, and optimizes the workload for human agents.</p><h2>Steps for Implementing AI Agentic Workflows</h2><p>Now, let’s start building agentic workflows that think, adapt, and improve on their own. Here are the steps for effective implementation.</p><h3>Step #1: Set specific, actionable goals</h3><p>Check your existing infrastructure, available budget, and your team's technical expertise. Make sure everyone, from employees to executives and investors, understands why you're adopting AI agentic workflows.</p><p>For AI agents to deliver results, they need precise directions.</p><p>Generic goals like "improve operational efficiency" won't work. You must define exactly what you want to accomplish with measurable outcomes.</p><p>For example, if you want faster customer service, specify "reduce response time from 10 minutes to 2 minutes" or "increase first-contact resolution by 25%." This will give the workflow and the agents the direction needed.</p><h3>Step #2: Build teams of specialized AI agents</h3><p>AI agents work best when they focus on specific tasks. And just like skilled employees, each agent should handle what it does best.</p><p>For healthcare workflows, this means having one agent analyze medical data while another manages appointment scheduling. In financial systems, one agent might detect fraud patterns while another communicates with customers.</p><p>Identify what each step in your workflow needs, then assign the right agent with the right tools for each job.</p><h3>Step #3: Ensure strict data governance</h3><p>As agentic workflows become more prominent across industries, it’s important to ensure strict data governance and security policies. Apply metadata to build audit trails that track data from its origin through every access and transformation, ensuring accountability and compliance with privacy regulations.</p><p>Then, develop clear policies on how data moves through your workflow system. Define who can access what information and how it's used to avoid data breaches.</p><p>Regular audits also ensure your workflows maintain data integrity and stay within legal boundaries, even as regulations evolve.</p><table>
