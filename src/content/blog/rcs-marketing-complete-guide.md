<p>SMS marketing works, but let’s be honest: it feels a bit outdated compared to modern apps.</p><p>But what if you could send rich, interactive messages with branded content, images, buttons, and carousels straight to your customers’ native messaging apps?</p><p>Rich communication services (RCS) makes that possible.</p><p>If you’re ready to explore how RCS marketing can transform your engagement strategy, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know. Let’s get started.</p><h2>What is RCS marketing?&nbsp;</h2><p>RCS marketing uses rich communication services to send interactive, branded messages through a customer’s default messaging app. It’s a modern upgrade to SMS that lets businesses share images, buttons, carousels, and more — all without needing third-party apps.</p><p>A user on Reddit summed up this perfectly:</p><figure style="max-width:947px" data-rt-max-width="947px"><div><img src="/images/blog/684fba6f4faf029975dfd544_AD_4nXc-mCcBRQQlEK1YPFiH6J6vuWse2rMEwpmHHy5UlbIA5jft-ix-8d6f870f.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Screenshot of a Reddit comment explaining what RCS is" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><em>RCS explained by a Reddit user</em></figcaption></figure><p>RCS lets you send messages that are visually branded with logos and colors while remaining interactive. This turns static updates into an app-like experience inside a message.</p><p>This shift is part of a broader industry move, led by Google and backed by major mobile carriers, to upgrade messaging infrastructure and make RCS the default standard on Android devices.</p><p>As support continues to grow, businesses are adopting RCS as part of their customer engagement strategy. Platforms like Plivo make that adoption easier with a reliable, enterprise-grade gateway to deliver rich, reliable RCS campaigns at scale.</p><h2>RCS vs. SMS marketing: A quick comparison</h2><p>Marketers today are looking for ways to deliver more interactive and visual communication, and RCS is clearly leading the way.</p><p>While SMS still works well for simple alerts, it lacks the creativity and engagement that RCS marketing offers.</p><p>Let’s take a quick look at <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/rcs-vs-sms/">RCS vs. SMS</a> marketing.</p><style>

</table><h2>4 key benefits of RCS marketing</h2><p>RCS marketing makes messaging feel more natural for both you and your customers. And since you can see what’s working and what’s not, it’s easier to pivot your strategy and get better results.</p><p>Here are its four key benefits.</p><h3>1. Improved user interaction</h3><p>One of the biggest advantages of RCS marketing is how seamless it makes the experience for your customers. Instead of typing out replies or clicking a link to open a website, users can just tap a button right inside the message.</p><p>Want them to book a demo, check order status, or browse products? It’s all possible with just a tap.</p><p>Fewer steps mean less effort, and that leads to more people following through. In fact, individuals spend up to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jw8yzXTwerc&amp;t=25099s">37 seconds</a> engaging with RCS messages, which is a lot longer than most other types of mobile messaging.</p><figure style="max-width:1600px" data-rt-max-width="1600px"><div><img src="/images/blog/684fba704faf029975dfd54c_AD_4nXcM3wRN8WuEGU6PUTZRY-ufYfAWqUylecFEKAKw5uyOhiVLe1b-c30becbf.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt=" Image showing the engagement results of RCS messaging" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><em>People engage more with RCS than any other platform</em></figcaption></figure><p>That extra time and interaction can make all the difference when you’re trying to convert interest into action.</p><h3>2. Consistent brand experience</h3><p>RCS marketing doesn’t just tell people who you are — it shows them.</p><p>Verified business profiles help people know they’re getting messages from the real brand. Every message shows your brand’s logo, name, colors, and a checkmark. These small details make it clear that the message is coming from a genuine source.</p><figure style="max-width:686px" data-rt-max-width="686px"><div><img src="/images/blog/684fba6f4faf029975dfd53f_AD_4nXfZ0LvNB4_qbup1aU-IOT-k4O3P7YadX2o5Uyk_U4QmRyJdWck-c9d490a7.png" width="auto" height="auto" alt="Image showing that MAYI - HOMES sends a verified RCS message with branding" loading="auto"></div><figcaption><em>Verified RCS message from MAYI - HOMES</em></figcaption></figure><p>This consistency matters because <a href="https://www2.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/leadership/brand-trust-and-challenging-orthodoxies.html">88% of people</a> are more likely to buy from a brand they trust.</p><h3>3. In-depth analytics</h3><p>With RCS marketing, you can track open rates, button clicks, and how people interact with each part of your message.</p><p>You get clear visibility into what’s working and where users are dropping off.&nbsp;</p><p>This makes it much easier to measure the return on investment (ROI) and fine-tune your campaigns. The more you understand how people engage, the better you can shape your messaging for results.</p><h3>4. Higher conversion potential</h3><p>RCS marketing makes it easier for customers to take action — whether that’s browsing products, booking a service, or making a purchase — all within the message itself.</p><p>With fewer clicks and no need to switch apps, the path to conversion feels effortless. And when it’s that easy, more people follow through.</p><p>For example, <a href="https://jibe.google.com/success-stories/easemytrip/">EaseMyTrip</a> used RCS to run a post-COVID travel survey. They added quick-tap answer options and followed up with a thank-you coupon. The campaign saw a 4x higher click-through rate than email, 10x more survey completions, and a 2.7% increase in conversion rate.</p><h2>5 major use cases of RCS marketing</h2><p>Here are five major use cases showing how brands are using RCS marketing effectively.</p><h3>1. Let’s look at a few of the biggest myths and what’s actually true.</p><h3>Myth 1: RCS marketing is too expensive</h3><p>At first glance, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/rcs-business-messaging/">RCS business messaging</a> can seem like a pricey upgrade. Rich visuals, tap-to-action buttons, and branded layouts look premium, so it’s easy to assume they come with a hefty cost.</p><p>But cost alone doesn’t tell the full story.</p><p>What you get in return matters more. RCS drives significantly stronger engagement with higher click-through rates, increased interactions, and better overall outcomes.</p><p>Take Club Comex, the loyalty program of North American paint brand Comex. They sent two rich and interactive RCS campaigns to their members and saw a <a href="https://jibe.google.com/success-stories/club-comex/">10x higher click-through rate</a>, which helped increase revenue by 115%.</p><p>That’s the value side of the equation. Better targeting and richer content mean more people click, engage, and convert.</p><h3>Myth 2: RCS marketing doesn’t reach enough users to be worth it</h3><p>This concern made sense in the early days of RCS, when adoption was still catching up. But the landscape looks very different now.</p><p>In June 2024, the 12-month growth of RCS users <a href="https://www.mobilesquared.co.uk/business-messaging-tracker/">reached 36.3%</a>, showing faster uptake than other messaging channels. More Android devices support RCS by default, and it’s being rolled out across more networks globally. Even Apple has announced support, which means RCS is on track to reach a massive number of smartphone users worldwide.</p><p>With that kind of growth and widespread support, the hesitation around RCS is starting to fade. Brands can confidently invest in RCS marketing knowing it will connect with more customers than ever before.</p><h3>Myth 3: RCS gets treated like spam and ends up ignored just like emails</h3><p>Unlike email, RCS messages appear directly in the user’s primary messaging app alongside personal conversations. They include rich media and interactive elements, making them more engaging and less likely to be ignored.</p><p>This creates a more natural, conversational experience that drives higher open and response rates than traditional marketing channels.</p><h2>Why choose Plivo for your RCS marketing needs</h2><p>With RCS, you can turn simple messages into rich, branded conversations that feel more like chatting than broadcasting.</p><p>Plivo gives you the tools to make that shift without the hassle.
