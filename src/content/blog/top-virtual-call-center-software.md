<p>With the fourth Industrial Revolution underway, most knowledge workers expect flexible work environments. According to a <a href="https://resources.owllabs.com/hubfs/SORW/SORW_2021/owl-labs_state-of-remote-work-2021_report-final.pdf?utm_campaign=State%20of%20Remote%20Work%202021&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;_hsmi=180908804&amp;_hsenc=p2ANqtz-_QqLl-7bQetJbJYOdCoskUzSr2pErrPvrTL353dUDu9e3aetTHyMlktMDf-N_opd0g0eg2lZzzzMM4MFaCkoOPa9Edt73hZO7QXJGYUaOVMIId_nk&amp;utm_content=180908804&amp;utm_source=hs_automation">survey by Owl Labs</a>, 70% of respondents stated they’d prefer to work in a hybrid or remote setting.</p><p>This means businesses must move their operations to the cloud to stay relevant. For customer service and support, this includes a cloud-based call center—something that’s flexible, scalable, and location-independent.&nbsp;</p><p>And this is where virtual call center software comes into play. But how do you make sure the solution is as effective as it appears?&nbsp;</p><p>In this blog post, we will discuss the top-notch virtual contact center solutions you can use to help you select the best option.</p><h2>What is Virtual Call Center Software?</h2><p>Simply put, virtual call center software is a cloud-based platform that enables organizations to set up an effective contact center to handle customer interactions remotely.&nbsp;</p><p>It comes with features like call routing, recording, and callback options, enabling agents to provide prompt and quality support from anywhere with a good internet connection.</p><p>As it is not heavily dependent on hardware, it can be set up easily, allowing customer service agents to work from anywhere. Plus, organizations can hire global talent and ensure round-the-clock support, ensuring happy customers.&nbsp;</p><h2>Key Features to Look for in Virtual Call Center Software</h2><p>Virtual call center software directly impacts the quality of customer conversations and the productivity of your customer agents. Here are some must-have features to look out for in your next virtual call center platform:</p><h3>Voice bots&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</h3><p>Voice bots use voice recognition and natural language processing (NLP) to provide instant, round-the-clock, and multilingual customer support. They can resolve common queries, offer self-service options, and direct complex, more nuanced queries to human agents. Voice bots allow businesses to provide personalized customer support while reducing costs related to hiring and training additional resources.</p><h3>Metrics and reporting</h3><p>Metrics and reports optimize your virtual call center's efficiency. By analyzing key performance indicators like call resolution rates, average handle time, and agent performance, you can identify bottlenecks in your contact processes and workflows.&nbsp;</p><p>This data-driven approach empowers you to make informed decisions that enhance your overall operation.</p><p>Some specific features to look for include customer satisfaction score (CSAT), call recordings, real-time and historical reports, and so on.</p><h3>Omnichannel support for agents</h3><p>Nothing frustrates a customer more than repeating their issues to different agents and via different channels. With omnichannel support, you can ensure that your customers can not only reach agents through their preferred means—email, phone, WhatsApp, and more—but also sync all customer conversations into one place so all your customer service agents have access to previous conversations.</p><h3>Call routing to qualified agents</h3><p>Another feature to look for in your virtual call center platform is call routing. By routing calls to agents with the relevant expertise—whether that’s a feature, customer segment, or language—you can resolve issues at first contact without asking customers to hold or transfer them to others.&nbsp;</p><p>Call routing also helps streamline your operations—for example, you can route calls to different customer service agents based on their shift timings, availability, and more.</p><h3>Call recording</h3><p>Traditional call centers have always practiced recording customer calls, and with good reason. It helps you evaluate an agent’s performance, fosters transparency between your business and the customer, and allows marketing teams to understand trends in customer behavior.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>So, taking this practice into your virtual call center with a call recording feature should be non-negotiable.</p><p>Live coaching</p><p>Real-time coaching creates a proactive learning environment where agents get immediate feedback on current interactions, increasing the efficiency and quality of customer service. Automated coaching tools ensure quality and compliance across all interactions on all channels. <br><br>When live coaching is made an integral part of daily operations, new agents can be trained directly on live interactions, minimizing onboarding costs and time. Call barging is another feature that facilitates quicker issue resolution and on-the-spot coaching.</p><h3>Self-service chatbot</h3><p>Another feature that can benefit your customer service team is a self-service chatbot. For common questions or requests, chatbots can provide immediate answers or send customers to the right help resources.&nbsp;</p><p>AI-powered chatbots can even hold human-like conversations and respond to questions with contextual awareness. This ensures that customers have round-the-clock access to support and allows your human agents to focus on the more tricky customer requests.</p><h2>Virtual Call Center Solutions at a
