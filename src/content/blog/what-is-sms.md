<p>SMS — short message services — is one of the best tools for building relationships with your customers. <a href="https://www.localproject.net/docs/texting-stats/" target="_blank">81% of Americans use SMS every day, and more than three-quarters of Americans wish they could text with a business</a>. SMS is one of the most popular and frictionless communication channels in the world.</p><p>How popular is SMS? <a href="https://www.statista.com/statistics/185879/number-of-text-messages-in-the-united-states-since-2005/">According to Statista</a>, more than 2 billion text messages were sent in America in 2019. According to <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/marketing/insights/articles/tap-into-the-marketing-power-of-sms">Gartner</a>, “various sources report SMS open and response rates as high as 98% and 45%, respectively.”</p><h2><strong>What Is SMS?</strong></h2><p>SMS, or text messaging, lets people send messages of up to 160 alphanumeric characters between cell phones and other computer devices. Most carriers support longer messages through “concatenated SMS,” which automatically segments messages longer than 160 characters into multiple text messages and combines them at the receiving end.</p><p>SMS is almost 30 years old. The first text message, <a href="https://mashable.com/2012/09/21/text-messaging-history/" target="_blank">sent in 1992</a>, simply read, “Merry Christmas.” The service was first offered to consumers in 1993, and growth was slow, in part because, initially, consumers couldn’t exchange messages with people who had service on other carriers. Also, early “dumb” mobile phones had only numerical keypads, so sending alpha characters required multiple keypresses, using a system called T9 — short for “text on 9 keys.” After Nokia offered the <a href="https://www.timetoast.com/timelines/evolution-of-the-mobile-phone-keyboard" target="_blank">first smartphone with a QWERTY keyboard</a> in 1996, texting began to get easier.</p><p>SMS popularity increased in 2007 when the iPhone was released. <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/text-messaging-explodes-in-america/" target="_blank">CBS News reported</a> that in the first quarter of 2006, Americans sent and received 65 texts per month. By the second quarter of 2008, Americans were sending and receiving a whopping 357 text messages a month — a 450% increase. “Nicholas Covey, director of insights for Nielsen Mobile, attributed the spike in messaging to the spread of QWERTY-style keypads, whose users send 54% more text messages than those with ordinary keypads,” <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/29/technology/29drill.html?scp=3&amp;sq=texting&amp;st=cse" target="_blank">the New York Times reported</a>. By the fourth quarter of 2007, American cell phone subscribers sent more text messages than they made phone calls. </p><p>Since then, SMS usage has grown further. As of 2020, <a href="https://99firms.com/blog/sms-marketing-stats/" target="_blank">according to 99firms</a>, 5 billion people in the world could send and receive SMS messages, and over 90% of text messages are read within three minutes. </p><h2><strong>Types of SMS: P2P and A2P</strong></h2><p>There are two primary types of SMS content: person-to-person (P2P) and application-to-person (A2P).</p><p>P2P SMS is what most people imagine when they think of text messaging. P2P SMS is generally conversational, and it takes place between real people, generally in real time.</p><p>A2P SMS comes in three varieties, as we explain in our <a href="https://www.plivo.com/resources/sms-api-best-practices/" target="_blank">SMS best practices guide</a>:</p><ol><li><strong>Informational messaging</strong> happens when a consumer gives their phone number to a business and asks to be contacted in the future.</li><li><strong>Promotional messaging</strong> occurs when a message is sent that contains a sales or marketing promotion.</li><li><strong>Transactional messages</strong> are non-marketing messages that provide important information to customers, such as a welcome message, an order conﬁrmation, or a shipping update.</li></ol><p>If a business wants to send SMS messages to customers, they have to take into account certain legal and regulatory considerations. For example, senders of A2P SMS messages must obtain consent from recipients, and they must send messages from an approved SMS number type.</p><p>Different countries have different requirements and regulations regarding A2P SMS, so businesses should check local rules.</p><h2><strong>Common ways businesses can leverage SMS</strong></h2><p>SMS is the most popular messaging platform, but not all businesses take advantage of it.
