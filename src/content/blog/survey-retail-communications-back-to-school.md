<p>With back-to-school season upon us, it’s time for a little pop quiz (remember those?). Do you know how back-to-school shoppers prefer to communicate with your brand? </p><p>If you’re not sure, you’re in the right place.</p><p>In early August, Plivo commissioned The Harris Poll to conduct a survey among more than 2,000 US adults aged 18 and older.* We found that nearly 9 in 10 Americans (87%) are open to receiving communications from retailers they shop or want to shop with, and the percentage was even higher — 94% — among US adults who planned to do back-to-school shopping.</p><p>Of those adults, 70% preferred email notifications, 53% preferred SMS/text messaging, and 32% preferred direct mail. Only 27% were open to in-app messaging and 26% were open to receiving phone calls.</p><figure><div><img src="/images/blog/658112ae2144d45074a03f02_retailsurvey3-1.jpeg" alt="Plivo survey conducted by Harris Poll" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div></figure><p>When we looked into those who were specifically shopping for back to school, we discovered four more key insights:</p><h3>Men and women are both doing back-to-school shopping</h3><p>While men are less likely than women to do back-to-school shopping, a significant number of them still do. According to our survey, 36% of men and 34% of women are doing back-to-school shopping.</p><h3>Older shoppers prefer email and direct mail communications</h3><p>Reaching out to older shoppers? Be sure to reach out via their preferred channels. Back-to-school consumers over the age of 65 noted that their preferred form of communication is email (70%) and direct mail (65%), while SMS (19%) and phone call (7%) were their least preferred. By contrast, younger consumers between the ages of 18 and 34 most prefer email (65%) and SMS (56%), and least prefer direct mail (24%).</p><h3>91% of back-to-school shoppers would opt in to SMS/text message notifications from retailers</h3><p>In great news for brands, we found back-to-school consumers are very open to opting in to SMS notifications. They’re willing to sign up for:</p><ul><li>Shipping/delivery notices and updates (58%)</li><li>Coupons (57%)</li><li>Sale promotions (51%)</li><li>Notification that product is back in stock (42%)</li><li>Curbside pickup (37%)</li><li>New product notifications (34%)</li></ul><p>Clearly these consumers are most interested in deals and updates on existing orders. Back-to-school shoppers likely have specific supply lists or items in mind this time of year, so they’re looking for communications that help them find what they need, at a good price, delivered when they want it.</p><h3>As income and education increases, so does the likelihood of opting in to SMS notifications</h3><p>For the most part, the likelihood of opting in to retailers’ text notifications increases with income and education level. 88% of US adults with an income of under $50,000 said they would opt in, while 95% of those with an income over $100,000 would opt in. Meanwhile, 85% of those with a high school education or less would opt in, while of those with a college degree or more, 94% would opt in.</p><p>If you’re not already communicating with your back-to-school customers in their preferred channels, go back to school with us! Check out our guide to <a href="https://www.plivo.com/resources/sms-notifications-best-practices/" target="_blank">SMS notification use cases and best practices</a>.</p><p><em>*This survey was conducted online within the United States by The Harris Poll on behalf of Plivo from August 10-12, 2021, among 2,082 U.S. adults ages 18 and older, of whom 688 plan to shop for back to school this year. For complete survey methodology, please contact </em><a href="mailto:press@plivo.com"><em>press@plivo.com</em></a>.</p><p><br></p>
