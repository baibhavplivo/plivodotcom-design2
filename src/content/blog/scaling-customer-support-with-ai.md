<p>Your customers expect you to be there every step of the way, but that’s difficult to do as your business scales. More customers also mean more tickets—which increases your agent workload and decreases the efficiency of your support delivery.&nbsp;</p><p>This results in longer response times, unsatisfied customers, and a declining ROI.</p><p>To avoid such an outcome, you need to scale your support along with your growth. And that’s where AI comes in.&nbsp;</p><p>Businesses using AI have reported a significant <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/ai-customer-service-statistics">30% drop</a> in customer support volume and a 45% cut in customer service operations cost. Interestingly, 52% of consumers now want AI to help improve their experiences and 61% prefer faster responses from AI than waiting to talk to an agent.</p><p>AI adoption to scale customer support is therefore a necessity if you want to maintain an edge over your competitors. In this article, we’ll explore a few ways to do that.</p><h2>5 ways to scale customer support with AI</h2><p>Utilizing AI to scale your customer support enhances customer experience and satisfaction while reducing operational cost, agent workload, and burnout.</p><p>To get started, here are some expert-recommended strategies you can try:</p><h3>1. Implement AI-powered chatbots to resolve routine queries</h3><p>A typical payment system, say Payoneer, will likely face a barrage of routine queries such as transaction status updates, account verification issues, delayed payments, withdrawal failures, currency conversion rates, refund processing, and fee clarifications.</p><p>Imagine assigning an agent to constantly retrieve transaction status updates for customers when they could be handling more complex technical issues instead.</p><p>For a small business with only a couple of queries daily, that might not be any issue. But it’s a game changer for larger brands or those scaling rapidly. The consequences are far reaching:</p><ul><li>Routine and monotonous queries can cause agent burnout, which in turn, affects delivery.</li><li>Customers with technical complaints receive diffused attention and eventually a poor experience.&nbsp;</li><li>Unsatisfied customers are more likely to switch to competitors, reducing your customer retention rate and increasing acquisition costs.</li></ul><p>You can proactively avoid these issues by transferring your routines to Chatbots. These bots are capable of addressing repetitive requests by leveraging pre-trained data and access to your knowledge base.&nbsp;</p><p>According to a recent report from Comm100, AI-chatbots can reduce routine queries by up to <a href="https://www.comm100.com/blog/state-of-automated-customer-service/#:~:text=Competition%20is%20at%20an%20all,so%20too%20does%20the%20latter.">80%</a>, thus freeing up your agents to do other core tasks.</p><p>Sephora is an example of a brand using chatbots to reduce the queue and provide excellent support. The beauty retailer integrated AI-driven chatbots into its social media platforms such as Facebook’s Messenger.&nbsp;</p><p>‍</p><ul><li>It not only handles repetitive questions, but also provides product recommendations and helps customers try out their choice before making a purchase.</li><li>Sephora also introduced smart bots on its website to handle common queries.&nbsp;</li><li>According to Renascence, this implementation has helped it reduce response time by <a href="https://www.renascence.io/journal/how-sephora-enhances-customer-experience-cx-through-personalization-and-digital-innovation">40%</a> and boost customer satisfaction even at peak hours.</li></ul><p>As an all-in-one, omnichannel customer service platform, Plivo CX offers an <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/chatbot">OpenAI-powered chatbot</a> to help deliver exceptional customer support around the clock and take the load off your agents.</p><p>‍</p><figure><div><img src="/images/blog/67fe0a70ed99c64d444902c0_67d98bf8c05b379bf8bd1293_AD_4nXf1qGEDlqXPKThjlMEwEq94wa-a362dd70.png" alt="Image showing Plivo's AI chatbot in action" width="auto" height="auto" loading="auto"></div><figcaption>&nbsp;Enhance customer support with AI-driven chatbots for faster query resolution.</figcaption></figure><p>‍</p><h3>2. Empower agents with generative AI for real-time assistance</h3><p>We get it—it’s quite unavoidable to get stuck choosing the appropriate words to respond with. And sometimes, peak hours won’t give your agents the luxury of time to decide on one. This is where GenAI is a perfect fit as a solution.&nbsp;</p><p>GenAI primarily uses ML and NLP to analyze customer queries and data. The insights from this analysis are then used to provide real-time suggestions, context-aware responses, and automated knowledge retrieval during interactions.&nbsp;</p><p>That means your agents can copy and paste an auto-generated response template tailored to the customer they’re helping.</p><p>Besides harshing out templates, GenAI can also auto-complete your sentences, especially when the complaints are so common. This helps to improve response time, reduce the workload on your agents, and clear ticket logs faster, thus highlighting why <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2024-12-09-gartner-survey-reveals-85-percent-of-customer-service-leaders-will-explore-or-pilot-customer-facing-conversational-genai-in-2025">85%</a> of customer service leaders hope to integrate the technology in 2025.</p><h3>3. Use an AI-optimized knowledge base to reduce ticket load</h3><p>Empowering your agents is a good customer support strategy, but empowering your customers is even better. According to Gartner, approximately <a href="https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2024-08-19-gartner-survey-finds-only-14-percent-of-customer-service-issues-are-fully-resolved-in-self-service">73%</a> of customers use self-service options such as knowledge bases and FAQs to find answers at some point in their customer journey.&nbsp;</p><p>This effectively reduces the number of queries logged and helps your agents focus on issues customers can’t resolve by themselves.</p><p>However, only 14% of customer support issues are fully resolved via self-service, leaving the remaining 86% to agents and chatbots. Additionally, 45% of customers who engage self-help feel companies don’t understand their problems. In 43% of failed self-service cases, the reason for failure is that customers can’t find relevant content to their queries.</p><p>Not finding a useful solution frustrates your customers. Frustration weakens customer trust in your brand and damages your relationship.</p><p>To avoid this, you can employ AI to create a rich knowledge base through automated tagging.
