<p>There are over <a href="https://www.itu.int/en/ITU-D/Statistics/Pages/stat/default.aspx">8.58 billion mobile subscriptions worldwide</a>, making text messaging a game changer for how businesses connect with customers. Short Message Service (SMS) has long been a reliable and universally compatible choice for communication. Rich Communication Services (RCS), on the other hand, introduces advanced capabilities like rich media, read receipts, and branded messages that transform interactions.</p><p>While SMS boasts a massive user base of 9.3 billion subscribers, <a href="https://www.juniperresearch.com/resources/infographics/a2p-sms-rcs-ott-whats-the-outlook-for-2024/">RCS has grown to 1.2 billion users</a>, sparking a heated debate: RCS vs. SMS.</p><p>Engaging Android phone users through RCS chats or relying on SMS for a broad reach both offer unique advantages.</p><p>When businesses compare RCS and SMS, the key differences often include interactivity, compatibility, and cost. In this blog, we’ll explore SMS and RCS's features, business use cases, strengths, and limitations to help you find the right balance to meet business goals.</p><h2>SMS: The standard since day one</h2><p>SMS is a communication protocol that allows users to send short, text-only messages between mobile devices. It relies on cellular networks and not on an internet connection.</p><p>Therefore, it has been the backbone of mobile text communication for decades, as it virtually works with all mobile devices, including basic phones.</p><h3>Are SMS and iMessage the same?</h3><p>No, SMS and iMessage are not the same.</p><p>iMessage offers interactive features and allows multimedia-rich content sharing between two iOS devices. SMS only sends short, text-based messages between all mobile devices.</p><h2>RCS: The new feature-rich customer engagement stream</h2><p>RCS is an upgraded messaging protocol that relies on Wi-Fi or cellular data. It delivers an interactive experience with support for high-resolution multimedia, group chats, and other advanced features.</p><h3>Are RCS and iMessage the same?</h3><p>No, while they share similar features, RCS and iMessage differ.</p><p>iMessage is exclusive to Apple devices, while RCS is a universal protocol supported by various messaging apps on compatible devices.</p><h2>RCS vs. SMS comparison</h2><p>Let's explore the key differences between RCS and SMS for businesses evaluating their messaging strategies and deciding which option is best for them.</p>

However, RCS business messaging (RBM) was added to iOS 18.1. Now, iOS supports RCS for iPhone users for person-to-person (P2P) messaging in select markets.</p><p>While a global rollout is pending, this integration improves messaging between Android and Apple devices. Users can check and enable RCS messaging in their app’s chat features settings.</p><h2>Find a solution no matter your messaging strategy</h2><p>Choosing the right messaging protocol depends on your goals and audience.
