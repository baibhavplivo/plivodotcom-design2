<p>Text messaging is a highly effective tool for businesses, with SMS boasting a <a href="https://go.plivo.com/hubfs/Resources/Plivo_SMS_Notifications_ebook.pdf?_gl=1*f42j36*_gcl_au*MTE0MDg1NjM2NS4xNzMzNzYzMTI0">98%</a> global delivery rate. This reliability makes it ideal for reaching customers quickly and consistently.&nbsp;</p><p>However, managing high volumes of customer communications manually can be challenging for businesses. An SMS gateway makes it possible for a broader range of devices and applications to communicate with each other.</p><p>Whether you want to launch a marketing campaign, send automated alerts, or engage in two-way conversations with customers, an SMS gateway simplifies the process.</p><p>Using SMS gateway services, you can send a message from your computer to a phone (or vice versa).</p><p>In this guide, we’ll dive into how SMS gateway providers work, the different applications of SMS gateway services, and how you can choose the right provider for your business’ needs.</p><p>So, let’s begin!</p><h2>What is an SMS gateway?</h2><p>An SMS gateway is a technology that allows software applications or computers to send text messages to mobile phones. Often, messages sent from a computer are not in a format that’s compatible with SMS. In such cases, SMS gateways serve as translators, converting the message into a format that mobile devices can understand.</p><p>The main job of an SMS gateway is to ensure the message is compatible with mobile networks. Businesses use SMS gateways to send bulk text messages to customers directly from a computer or software platform.</p><p>Interestingly, SMS gateway services can also work in reverse, allowing mobile users to send messages back to computers.</p><p>While previously SMS gateways were offered as physical hardware with SIM cards and embedded radios, businesses and consumers no longer need hardware-based solutions today. Instead, they use cloud-based systems that seamlessly translate and transmit messages.</p><p>These advancements are powered by <a href="https://www.plivo.com/sms/">SMS APIs</a>, which allow messages to be delivered to specific devices at the right time, without the need for physical hardware.</p><h2>How does SMS gateway technology work?</h2><p>Here’s a simple step-by-step breakdown of how modern SMS gateway services operate:</p><ol><li><strong>Create the message</strong>: Compose your text message within your business application.</li><li><strong>Send via API</strong>: The message is sent to the SMS gateway through an <a href="https://www.plivo.com/blog/best-sms-api-provider/">SMS API</a>.</li><li><strong>Convert the message</strong>: The gateway uses protocols like HTTP or SMPP (Short Message Peer-to-Peer) to format the message for mobile networks. Protocols like SMPP play a key role in the smooth operation of SMS gateways. They enable fast and reliable communication between the gateway and mobile carriers, ensuring your messages reach their destination promptly — even during high-volume campaigns.</li><li><strong>Route the message</strong>: The gateway forwards the message to the appropriate telecom carrier.</li><li><strong>Deliver the message</strong>: The telecom carrier ensures the text reaches the recipient’s phone.</li><li><strong>Confirm delivery</strong>: The SMS gateway receives a delivery report from the carrier and relays it back to your application, confirming the message was received.</li></ol><p>Imagine you’re running SMS marketing campaigns and want to send a promotional text to all your customers. Your audience is spread across the U.S., using different carriers like AT&amp;T, Verizon, or T-Mobile.&nbsp;</p><p>Instead of dealing with each network separately, an SMS gateway simplifies the process. You connect once, and the gateway handles the rest — distributing your message to everyone at the same time, no matter which network they’re on.</p><h3>Types of SMS gateways</h3><p>When it comes to SMS marketing campaigns, two types of SMS gateways are most commonly used:</p><p>Email to SMSThis type of gateway converts <a href="https://www.plivo.com/docs/messaging/use-cases/sms-to-email-gateway">email messages into SMS format</a>, ensuring they meet the technical requirements for mobile delivery. It’s a two-way system, allowing businesses to send text messages to email inboxes and receive replies in return.</p><p>Web to SMSFrequently used by application-to-person SMS platforms, this gateway connects mobile phones with web applications. It enables businesses to send both SMS and MMS messages directly from a web application to recipients’ mobile devices. Web to SMS gateways are more feature-rich than other options, offering enhanced flexibility and functionality.</p><h2>Applications of SMS gateways</h2><p>SMS gateways have a wide range of applications for businesses and organizations, enabling them to communicate quickly and effectively with their customers.
