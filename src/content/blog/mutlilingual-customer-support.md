<p>It’s ten minutes past 2 pm. A new high-value customer submits a technical complaint—but, in Spanish. The problem? None of your service reps speak Spanish.&nbsp;</p><p>Now, you have only two options.</p><p>First, you say, “<em>Thank you for reaching out. We currently accept queries only in English. Please resend your message in English so we can assist you. Thank you for your understanding!</em>"&nbsp;</p><p>Or, you scramble from Google’s translator to other free versions of online interpreters, which might give you an inaccurate response.&nbsp;</p><p>Whichever you choose, your customer will likely not find it encouraging. Your inability to efficiently serve customers in their native language passes a message; that they’re not a valued part of your brand.&nbsp;</p><p>According to Hyken’s report, <a href="https://hyken.com/customer-service/customer-service-week-a-time-to-celebrate-customers-and-employees/">65%</a> of consumers want to be treated valuably. Failure to deliver this expectation results in a poor support experience, increased churn rates, and a negative brand reputation.</p><p>To avoid such outcomes, you need to implement multilingual customer support, and this article will show you how to do that.</p><h2>What is multilingual customer support?</h2><p>Multilingual customer support is the ability to provide consistent, high-quality assistance in multiple languages to your customers while accounting for geographical, cultural, and contextual differences that influence communication.</p><p>As your business scales, there is also an increasing need to serve a diverse market audience. This mandates your support team to be capable of sustaining interactions with individuals of different languages outside your native business borders.</p><p>Even within your business borders, you’re bound to interact with customers of different linguistic needs. According to the <a href="https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2022/12/languages-we-speak-in-united-states.html?utm_campaign=20221206msacos1ccstors&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_source=govdelivery">United States Census record</a>, the number of residents who utilize other languages besides English has nearly tripled in the last decade.&nbsp;</p><p>So, you must be ready to cater to this diversity once necessary.</p><h2>Benefits of multilingual customer support</h2><p>Multilingual support increases customer satisfaction, boosts retention, and ensures you can tap into diverse markets for more opportunities. Here’s how it does that:</p><h3>Improved customer satisfaction</h3><p>According to <a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">Unbabel’s 2021 multilingual CX report</a>, one in three global consumers cite a lack of multilingual support as their top concern in localized customer experience.&nbsp;</p><p>Uni- or bilingual provisions lead to frustration since your customers can’t express themselves the way they want, and this culminates in a bad customer experience.&nbsp;</p><p>On the other hand, providing multilingual support can enhance customer satisfaction and boost your customers’ LTV. This explains why <a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">64%</a> say they will pay more for a service or product if the brand offers a multilingual support service.</p><h3>Higher customer retention</h3><p><a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">57%</a> of global consumers see it as a bias when a brand fails to offer multilingual support. Two in three will also switch to another brand if their current brand does not offer broader language support.</p><p>But what happens if you fill up this communication gap? <a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">73%</a> of respondents in Unbabel’s survey say they will remain loyal.&nbsp;</p><p>This means multilingual provisions can help you retain your existing customers and also gain an edge over your competitors in attracting new ones.&nbsp;</p><p>Interestingly, <a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">40%</a> of consumers will likely overlook poor customer service many times if it is offered in their native language. This gives you enough time to address issues plaguing your support delivery before customers bounce.</p><h3>Broader reach and competitive advantage</h3><p>Scaling your business often requires crossing borders and serving new audiences. Multilingual support ensures you can easily resolve the issues coming up in these regions, eliminate communication barriers, and rapidly build a robust brand reputation.</p><p>Also, multilingual support can serve as the spearhead of your competitive advantage in new markets. That’s because your potential customers will likely choose you over competitors who do not offer multilingual support, all things being equal. So, you can make it a focus of your marketing strategy.</p><h2>7 must-know strategies to implement multilingual support</h2><p>One in four consumers say they will not spend more than <a href="https://unbabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-the-unbabel-global-multilingual-cx-report-2021.pdf">$500</a> on brands offering limited language support. You can avoid this by implementing a sturdy multilingual support system.&nbsp;</p><p>Let’s go through how you can do that.&nbsp;</p><h3>1. Be upfront about your available language options</h3><p>First, inform your customers and audience about your current capacity.
