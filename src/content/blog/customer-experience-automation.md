<p>The importance of customer experience needs no introduction— <a href="https://wisernotify.com/blog/customer-experience-stats/#customer-experience-impact-report">96% of customers</a> remain loyal to a brand that considers brand perception a priority.&nbsp;</p><p>However, delivering a consistent and customer-centric experience is not as straightforward as it seems, especially when you have to do it manually. There are tons of repetitive and error-prone tasks that can affect your efficiency or negatively impact delivery.</p><p>And that’s where customer experience automation comes in.</p><p>Automation helps you streamline your customer service by delegating tasks, such as ticket routing, rerouting, follow-up emails, and data entry, to AI-powered tools. This means human agents can focus on more strategic and complex customer interactions.</p><p>In this article, we’ll explain customer experience automation, its benefits, and how to use it to boost your ROI.</p><h2>What is customer experience automation?&nbsp;</h2><p>To answer this, let’s split our definition into Customer experience and customer experience automation.</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/ai-customer-experience">Customer experience</a> is a sum of all customer interactions with your business, from their first contact to post-purchase support and the impression they form through these interactions. This encompasses every touchpoint, including browsing your website, engaging with your marketing efforts, interacting with your sales team, using your product or service, and receiving customer support.</li></ul><ul><li>Customer experience automation (CXA) means using AI-driven tools to streamline, optimize, and enhance these interactions with minimal human intervention while still delivering great results. This approach, which allows businesses to automate repetitive tasks, workflows, and communications, results in reduced human errors, consistent support, and personalized services.</li></ul><h3>Customer experience automation vs marketing automation</h3><p>Marketing automation refers to using software to streamline marketing processes and manage multi-channel campaigns. However, it focuses heavily on pre-purchase interactions and lead generation.</p><p>In contrast, customer experience automation covers your marketing and sales funnel, including pre-purchase and post-purchase interactions. This approach aims to build customer satisfaction, retention, and loyalty.</p><h3>Customer experience automation vs customer relationship management</h3><p>While both CXA and CRM aim to nurture brand-customer relationships, the latter primarily does that by centralizing your customer data. CRMs also have minimal or zero customer interface, meaning only your business interacts with the system, not your customers themselves.</p><p>On the other hand, CXA offers a customer interface through AI agents like chatbots and integrates with CRMs, ERPs, and other home-grown systems to utilize customer data to deliver personalized experiences.</p><h2>How does customer experience automation work?</h2><p>Customer experience automation leverages three crucial components to streamline each touch point in your customer’s journey for faster and more effective service delivery:</p><ul><li><strong>AI agents</strong>: AI-powered chatbots engage your leads and customers on the front end to simulate authentically human-like conversations. An example is the all-in-one customer service platform Plivo CX’s <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/chatbot">self-service chatbot</a>, which is an OpenAI-developed bot capable of custom training for accuracy and relevance.</li><li><strong>Agent assistance</strong>: Automation tools provide valuable support to your human agents in the backend through real-time suggestions, seamless retrieval of relevant customer data, and automation of routine tasks during interactions.</li><li><strong>Workflow optimization:</strong> Escalation workflows, interactive voice response, ticket routing, and sticky rerouting standardize your processes to boost overall efficiency.</li></ul><p>These automation components work in tandem to eliminate drags in your customer experience and enhance satisfaction.</p><h2>Key advantages of customer experience automation for ecommerce</h2><p>As HubSpot data suggests, <a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/customer-experience-trends#:~:text=71%25%20of%20leaders%20plan%20on,success%20team%20and%20other%20departments.">86%</a> of customer experience leaders believe automation will positively impact their service delivery, and 71% plan to invest more in AI agents like chatbots. Let’s explore some advantages of doing the same for your ecommerce business:</p><h3>1.Offer 24/7 support</h3><p><a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/service/customer-experience-trends">67%</a> of consumers want a response to their queries within 3 hours, and over 60% want 24/7 support availability. That’s not something a 9-5 helpdesk offered by your human agents can help offer, especially when serving cross-border buyers outside your local timezone.</p><p>Customer experience automation helps you eliminate these geolocational and support barriers by using round-the-clock AI-powered chatbots built to handle more than <a href="https://www.invespcro.com/blog/chatbots-customer-service/">80%</a> of routine questions.&nbsp;</p><p>Moreover, you don’t need to hit a break with your customer data collection and analysis. Your automation works nonstop while you nap.</p><h3>2.Reduce operational costs</h3><p>For a mid-sized ecommerce business with tens of thousands of customers, you’ll need to set up a bogus team of human agents and data analysts to have a chance at providing a near-efficient experience for your customers. That’s difficult to scale in terms of resource and skill availability.</p><p>On the other hand, automating your customer experience with AI agents can help save up to <a href="https://www.invespcro.com/blog/chatbots-customer-service/">30%</a> in cost. A single bot can do the work of multiple humans, thus reducing your recruitment costs. Also, good customer experience enhances retention rate and slashes customer acquisition costs.</p><h3>3.Increase customer satisfaction</h3><p>Companies using AI report a <a href="https://wisernotify.com/blog/customer-experience-stats/#customer-experience-impact-report">20%</a> increase in satisfaction rates. Automation reduces time to resolution, ensures quick escalation to the right channels when necessary, and matches customer data to deliver accurate experiences.</p><p>Also, delegating these weights to AI enables companies to allocate resources such as human agents and capital towards improving other core aspects of the customer journey, which serves your customers’ interests.</p><h3>4.Personalize communications</h3><p>According to Forrester, <a href="https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbesagencycouncil/2018/12/06/personalization-2-0-identity-matching-for-individualized-marketing/">77%</a> of consumers have purchased from or recommended brands that provide personalized experiences.</p><p>Customer experience automation ensures this by collating and analyzing customer data from different sources, such as multiple communication channels, e-commerce platforms like Shopify, and CRMs.</p><p>Structured data gives valuable insight into your customers’ pain points, previous support history, and other information that can assist in making informed decisions.</p><p>A great example is the omnichannel contact center solution Plivo CX's<a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/features/uad"> unified desktop agent</a>, which consolidates fragmented customer data from multiple channels, such as email, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/channels/voice">voice</a>, <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/channels/messaging">SMS</a>, WhatsApp, and <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/service/channels/live-chat">live chat</a>, into a single, easily accessible interface.<br><br>This empowers your human agents with real-time access to valuable insights and enables your chatbots to provide tailored recommendations and assistance to customers.</p><h3>5.Offer consistent experiences</h3><p><a href="https://wisernotify.com/blog/customer-experience-stats/#customer-experience-impact-report">79%</a> of consumers expect consistency in service delivery; however, <a href="https://www.salesforce.com/resources/articles/customer-expectations/">55%</a> say they often feel like they are talking to different departments instead of one organization. This disconnect often occurs when departments operate in silos. <br><br>For example, the marketing team may focus on lead generation without aligning with the customer service team, leading to fragmented communication and a disjointed customer experience.</p><p>Customer experience automation prevents silo operations by breaking the barriers between your marketing, sales, and customer support teams. This results in a <a href="https://www.plivo.com/cx/blog/plivo-commitment-to-contact-center">unified contact center</a> and allows access to valuable data, ensuring your customers have a consistent experience pre- and post-purchase.</p><h3>6.Boost customer relationships</h3><p>Providing faster responses to queries, accelerating order delivery, reducing touch points to purchase, and offering personalized experiences through automation all boost customer satisfaction. And satisfied customers are more likely to become loyal or connected to your brand.&nbsp;</p><p>Customers who feel connected to a brand are worth <a href="https://wisernotify.com/blog/customer-experience-stats/#customer-experience-impact-report">306%</a> more in lifetime value. Loyal customers are also good brand advocates and will recommend your business to others 71% more than those who are not.</p><h2>5 ways to use customer experience automation</h2><p>While customer experience automation is a must-have superpower, knowing where and how to use it can significantly impact your results.
