In this post, we will explore detailed steps, tips, strategies, and best practices.&nbsp;</p><h2><strong>What is SMS Marketing for eCommerce?&nbsp;</strong></h2><p>Before diving into the strategies, let's first understand what SMS marketing is for eCommerce businesses.&nbsp;</p><p>SMS marketing for eCommerce is using text messages to communicate with customers, promote products, and build long-term brand loyalty.&nbsp;</p><p>These messages could include promotions, order updates, complaint status, feedback, etc. It can cover the entire customer journey, from sign-up, promotions, purchases, repeat orders, and trying to win back a lost customer.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h2><strong>5 Reasons to Use SMS as a Marketing Tool for eCommerce</strong></h2><p>The top five reasons to invest in SMS marketing are as follows:</p><h3><strong>1. Preferred channel of consumers</strong></h3><p>A new study reveals that <a href="https://martech.org/75-of-consumers-made-purchases-as-a-result-of-sms-marketing/">75% of U.S. consumers </a>make purchases due to SMS messages from companies, and 60% expect to receive texts from brands at least weekly, highlighting the growing importance of SMS marketing.</p><p>In fact, consumers aged 25 to 54 prefer to communicate with brands via SMS over email. Based on evolving consumer preferences, investing in SMS marketing is definitely a wise choice.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>2. Fast communication</strong></h3><p><a href="https://www.zerobounce.net/email-statistics-report/">56%</a> of people check email less than three times a day. On the other hand, people check their phones as much as <a href="https://www.reviews.org/mobile/cell-phone-addiction/">144 times</a> a day. This behavior makes SMS a much faster and more intimate way to reach consumers.</p><h3><strong>3. Easy personalization</strong></h3><p>You can easily personalize SMS campaigns. It's also easy to segment users and run campaigns based on their demographics and order history.&nbsp;</p><p>For instance, you can offer first-time coupons to new users, launch deals based on regional festivals, and give exclusive discounts to customers who have not ordered for a long time.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>4. Effective for mobile commerce</strong></h3><p>Consumers check phones frequently, so SMS campaigns are perfect for flash deals and limited-time offers for mobile commerce. You can easily use SMS for all the major functions of mobile commerce, such as:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Order updates</li><li>Promotions</li><li>Feedback collection</li><li>Loyalty programs</li></ul><h3><strong>5. Suitable for customer service</strong></h3><p>SMS is more than just a marketing channel. Considering its quintessential nature of short and real-time communication, it is highly effective for customer service.&nbsp;</p><p>You can build SMS workflows to keep consumers updated on orders, refunds, returns, complaints, etc. SMS also allows two-way communication, so consumers can reply to messages for any follow-up action or feedback if required.&nbsp;<strong><br>How to Start Using SMS Marketing for eCommerce</strong></p><p>If you want to start implementing SMS marketing for your eCommerce business, here are five steps to begin:&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>1. Define your goals and budget</strong></h3><p>Before starting with SMS marketing, assess your requirements. A few parameters to check are:</p><ul><li>Number of users you expect to reach out to (Current and how much it can grow)</li><li>In which geography your customers are based out, and you will be sending messages in that region</li><li>Number of messages you estimate to send per month</li><li>Budget for SMS marketing</li></ul><p>It's not possible to set these parameters in stone in the beginning. Still, having some general idea or ballpark estimates will help you choose the right SMS marketing platform.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>2. Choose an SMS marketing platform</strong></h3><p>The next step is to choose an SMS marketing platform.&nbsp;</p><p>SMS marketing software is a technology platform used to run automated SMS campaigns. Common features of SMS marketing platforms are:</p><ul><li>Maintaining SMS list</li><li>Sending campaigns</li><li>Conducting automated analysis of campaigns or tracking KPIs related to SMS</li><li>Enabling two-way messaging to get replies</li></ul><p><strong>Key factors to check in an SMS marketing software are:</strong></p><ol><li>Does the pricing fit your budget, considering the number of consumers, expected size of campaigns, and target geography?&nbsp;</li><li>&nbsp;Is the software easy to use and has detailed documentation for initial implementation?</li><li>Does the software divide consumers into meaningful segments to run personalized campaigns?&nbsp;</li><li>Can the software be integrated with your current tech stack?</li><li>Does the software company have an easily accessible customer support team?</li><li>Does the software have any dashboards to track all the critical KPIs?</li></ol><p>You can check these factors to finalize an SMS marketing software. You'll also get a list of top SMS marketing software towards the end of this post.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>3. Grow your SMS list</strong></h3><p>After finalizing the SMS marketing software, you can now get your SMS list ready. For this, you can either persuade existing customers to join your SMS list or attract new users. A few strategies to grow the SMS list are:</p><ul><li>Offer discounts or coupons to incentivize users to join the SMS list</li><li>Convert your existing email subscribers by sending out a campaign. You can also offer a discount in return for getting added to SMS lists</li><li>Promote your SMS marketing list on social media (Youtube, Insta) in return for offers/exclusive deals/early access to products</li><li>Add a sign-up form for the SMS list to your website</li><li>Hold contests or giveaways that require participants to opt-in to your SMS list as part of the entry process</li></ul><h3><strong>4. Design your first campaign</strong></h3><p>Once you have a full-blown SMS list, it's time to design your first campaign. It could be a welcome message, promotion, flash deal, etc.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Tip:</strong> SMS marketing platforms provide ample templates. You can make use of any existing templates to design your first campaign.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>5. Assess and refine your campaigns</strong></h3><p>Success in SMS marketing (or any type of marketing) is all about continuously evolving. Keep up with the character limit</strong></h3><p>Keep your message crisp, considering the 160-character limit and the nature of the SMS. Consumers want to take a quick look at it. So, a short and crisp message garners more attention.</p><h3><strong>2. Use simple language</strong></h3><p>Keep the text as simple as possible so the reader doesn't have to re-read it (which they won't). Maintain a conversational tone to avoid losing the reader.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>3. Send messages at the right time</strong></h3><p>You don't want to be blocked by sending an SMS at 2 AM in the night. Identify the right time to send a message based on the receiver's time zone.&nbsp;</p><p>The <a href="https://www.fcc.gov/sites/default/files/tcpa-rules.pdf">Telephone Consumer Protection Act</a> allows marketers to send marketing messages in the US only between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM local time. You can also check around which time you have a maximum click-through rate.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Tip</strong>: <a href="https://cx.plivo.com/pungis2">Plivo CX </a>offers intuitive AI support to help you identify the right time to send messages.</p><h3><strong>4. Keep a clear call-to-action</strong></h3><p>Add a simple and clear call-to-action stating what action you expect from a consumer. For example, ‘Buy now’ and ‘Check here’ etc.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>5. Add an opt-out option</strong></h3><p>Include an opt-out option at the end of every message to avoid being reported as spam.</p><p><a href="https://www.validity.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/The-State-of-SMS-Marketing-in-2023.pdf">96%</a> of consumers have felt annoyed at least occasionally by SMS marketing messages at some point in time. Also, 56% of respondents have reduced or stopped purchasing from a brand if they found their SMS annoying.</p><p>Sending SMS to consumers who don't want to receive them can directly hamper your business.&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>6. Ensure compliance</strong></h3><p>Check for data, consumer, and privacy laws specific to your target country. Follow all the laws to avoid getting in hot waters with authority.&nbsp;</p><p>DoorDash, a San Francisco-based company, got in trouble with Australian authorities. They were hit by a <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2023-08-16/doordash-issued-2m-fine-for-spam-texts/102735232">$2 million</a> fine for large-scale bulk messaging and crossing the spam limit as per local laws.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h3><strong>7. Maintain consistent brand messaging</strong></h3><p>When you add a new channel to your marketing ecosystem, it represents your brand.
